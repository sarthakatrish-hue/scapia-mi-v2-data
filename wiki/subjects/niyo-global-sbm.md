## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Zero forex parity** — Scapia also offers 0% forex markup, neutralising Niyo's flagship differentiator on every international spend.
**No FD lock-in** — Scapia requires no fixed deposit, lowering the capital barrier for customers who don't want to park funds.
**Travel-native rewards** — Scapia's unlimited lounge access and travel rewards beat Niyo's spend-gated 1-lounge-per-quarter Premium offer.

### Where they're ahead
**Telecom distribution moat** — The Vi partnership creates a captive pre-travel acquisition funnel of hundreds of millions of subscribers — a distribution channel Scapia has no equivalent to [^niyo-vi-2026-06-22].
**Doorstep KYC velocity** — 24-hour doorstep delivery with assisted onboarding is frictionless for last-minute travellers; Scapia's digital-only onboarding requires more self-serve effort [^niyo-vi-2026-06-22].
**Bundled travel protection ecosystem** — Vi's broader travel bundle (Aditya Birla travel insurance up to ₹40L, Blue Ribbon baggage cover) amplifies Niyo's card offer into a fuller pre-departure product [^niyo-vi-2026-06-22] [Inferred · Medium].
**Introductory membership hook** — Free ₹999 Niyo Premium as a welcome benefit creates a tangible, time-limited reason to apply that Scapia's onboarding currently lacks [^niyo-vi-2026-06-22].

### What to do
**Watch for more telecom deals** — If Niyo replicates this model with Airtel or BSNL, it could own the pre-travel fintech moment at scale; Scapia should evaluate whether a telco or travel-platform distribution channel is feasible [Inferred · High].
**Counter the Premium bundle narrative** — Niyo's free membership positions its card as a travel benefits platform, not just a card; Scapia's comms should reinforce unlimited lounge (vs Niyo's gated quarterly access) as the clearer, unconditional win.
**Monitor doorstep KYC adoption** — Assisted onboarding at doorstep addresses a real friction point for less digitally confident travellers; if this drives strong activation, Scapia should assess whether its onboarding funnel needs a human-touch option [Inferred · Medium].

## Coverage

### Niyo Scores Vi Telecom Distribution — 2026-06-22

Vodafone Idea (Vi) and Niyo have announced a partnership to deliver Niyo Zero Forex Markup Cards to Vi customers within 24 hours, with doorstep KYC assistance from a Niyo executive [^niyo-vi-2026-06-22]. Vi calls this a first-of-its-kind telecom-fintech collaboration. Customers apply through the Vi App; those outside the doorstep zone activate digitally via the Niyo App. As an introductory offer, Vi customers receive Niyo Premium (₹999/year) free, including quarterly lounge access, zero convenience fees on visa and flight bookings, and 10% off international hotels [^niyo-vi-2026-06-22].

The strategic significance for Scapia is distributional, not product-based [Inferred · High]. Niyo's core card proposition — 0% forex, 180+ country acceptance — is already well-known. What this deal adds is a pre-travel acquisition funnel baked into a telco with over 200 million subscribers, triggered at the moment a customer activates international roaming [^niyo-vi-2026-06-22]. That timing — when a traveller is actively preparing to go abroad — is precisely the moment Scapia most wants to own. Vi's broader travel bundle (Aditya Birla insurance up to ₹40L, Blue Ribbon baggage cover) makes the Niyo card the financial anchor of a fuller pre-departure stack [^niyo-vi-2026-06-22].

Scapia's 0% forex and unlimited lounge access remain structurally superior to Niyo's spend-gated quarterly lounge and FD-backed model [Inferred · High]. But the Vi partnership signals that Niyo is competing on distribution intensity, not just product quality. If this model proves effective and Niyo extends it to Airtel or other telcos, it could establish a telecom-as-card-distributor playbook that becomes difficult to displace in the mass-market pre-travel segment [Inferred · Medium].
