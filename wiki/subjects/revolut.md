## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Credit line access** — Scapia is a credit card with Federal Bank underwriting; Revolut India is PPI-only with no credit product, leaving a material gap for spending-power users [Inferred · High]
**Travel rewards depth** — Scapia's zero-forex-markup + Scapia Coins rewards loop is travel-native; Revolut's RevPoints and Lifestyle layers are not yet live in India [^rev-tc-2026-06-01] [Inferred · High]
**Incumbent card rails** — Scapia rides Visa/RuPay credit rails and Federal Bank's banking license; Revolut is limited to prepaid rails until it upgrades its regulatory status [^rev-tc-2026-06-01]

### Where they're ahead
**Multi-currency experience** — Revolut's core global identity is multi-currency wallets and disposable virtual cards — a proven, polished product that frequent international travellers already trust [^rev-tc-2026-06-01]
**Brand awareness, organic pull** — ~820K downloads without a full launch and a 450K waitlist signal Revolut has significant brand gravity among digitally native Indians [^rev-tc-2026-06-01]
**UPI + wallet combo** — Revolut bundles UPI, e-wallet, prepaid card, and multi-currency in one app — a broader everyday-use surface than a single travel credit card [^rev-tc-2026-06-01]
**Global emerging-market momentum** — Brazil downloads up 487% YoY; Revolut is allocating real capital to emerging market growth, India included [^rev-tc-2026-06-01]

### What to do
**Own the credit wedge now** — Revolut cannot offer credit in India under a PPI license; Scapia should aggressively communicate its credit + rewards advantage to the 25–45 travel segment before Revolut fills that gap [Inferred · High]
**Watch RevPoints launch timing** — When Revolut activates RevPoints in India, the loyalty comparison with Scapia Coins becomes direct; monitor and prepare a differentiation narrative in advance [Inferred · Medium]
**Track regulatory upgrade signals** — If Revolut applies for a banking license or NBFC partnership, the competitive threat escalates sharply into credit; treat any such signal as a priority alert [Inferred · High]

## Coverage

### Revolut Named in HNI Research — 2026-06-28

Consumer research on affluent Indian travellers names Revolut alongside Wise as a recommended fintech platform for pre-travel forex optimisation, targeting the segment spending ₹8L-₹20L annually on international trips[^revolut-affluent-2026-06-28]. The guide explicitly advises users to open Revolut accounts and compare rates across platforms 30-45 days before departure — confirming Revolut is gaining mindshare in the HNI planning phase even before its full India launch.

This is corroborative rather than a new competitive development: Revolut's HNI targeting was already documented in the beta-launch coverage. The significance is that Revolut is achieving brand recognition in affluent-traveller research content without yet being fully live — compounding the competitive pressure it will exert once RevPoints and a full launch activate [Inferred · Medium].

### Revolut Enters India in Beta — 2026-06-01

British fintech Revolut has quietly begun a controlled beta rollout in India, with a few thousand users already active and ~450,000 on the waitlist [^rev-tc-2026-06-01]. Operating under an RBI prepaid payment instrument (PPI) license obtained after acquiring Arvog Forex in 2022, Revolut is offering UPI payments, e-money wallets, domestic prepaid cards, multi-currency cards, virtual cards, and disposable cards [^rev-tc-2026-06-01]. RevPoints loyalty and Lifestyle features are planned but not yet live.

Revolut is targeting 150 million "globally aspiring, digitally native" Indians aged 25–45, with a stated goal of 20 million users by 2030 and $7 billion in transaction volume [^rev-tc-2026-06-01]. Its app has already logged ~820,000 downloads in India, with over a third in 2025 and early 2026 — before any full public launch [^rev-tc-2026-06-01]. The company declined to give a specific broader launch date but indicated direct onboarding would open in the "near future."

For Scapia, this is a material competitive arrival. Revolut's target demographic — digitally native, internationally mobile, 25–45 — overlaps precisely with Scapia's core cardholder profile [Inferred · High]. The near-term threat is limited by Revolut's PPI-only status (no credit product), but if Revolut adds a loyalty layer and eventually secures a banking partnership or license, the overlap intensifies sharply [Inferred · High].
