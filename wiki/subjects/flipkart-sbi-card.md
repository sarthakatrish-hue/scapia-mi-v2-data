## What we know

**Flipkart ecosystem cashback** — 7.5% on Myntra, 5% on Flipkart/Shopsy/Cleartrip, 4% on Zomato/Uber/PVR/Netmeds, all capped at ₹4,000/quarter per category[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**Joining and annual fee ₹500** — Waived if annual spends exceed ₹3.5 lakh; welcome benefit worth ₹1,250 in Flipkart e-gift cards and Cleartrip vouchers[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**Auto-credit cashback mechanism** — Cashback credited directly to SBI Card account within two days of statement generation, removing redemption friction[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**High forex markup** — 3.5% on all foreign currency spends; article explicitly flags this as a key drawback for international travellers[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**Dual network availability** — Card issued on both Mastercard and Visa payment networks[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**Cleartrip as travel play** — 5% cashback on Cleartrip bookings positions this card as a partial travel card within the Flipkart ecosystem[^fksbi-2026-06-30]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Zero forex markup advantage** — Flipkart SBI Card charges 3.5% forex markup — among the highest in market; Scapia's near-zero forex fee is a direct structural win for any international traveller[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**Open travel earn vs ecosystem lock-in** — Cashback is gated to Flipkart/Cleartrip ecosystem; Scapia rewards travel spend across airlines, OTAs, and hotels without brand lock[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**No annual fee friction** — Scapia carries no joining or annual fee; Flipkart SBI Card's ₹500 fee requires ₹3.5 lakh annual spend to waive, a threshold many mid-tier users won't hit[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**Premium travel segment untouched** — Card is explicitly "not ideal for heavy international travellers"; Scapia's core segment is precisely that cohort[^fksbi-2026-06-30]

### Where they're ahead
**E-commerce cashback depth** — 7.5% on Myntra and 5% on Flipkart are among the strongest category cashback rates available; Scapia does not compete in online shopping rewards[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**Ecosystem stickiness** — Flipkart, Myntra, Shopsy, Cleartrip, Zomato, Uber cross-category coverage creates high daily-use frequency Scapia cannot match[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**SBI Card distribution** — Co-issuer SBI Card is India's largest credit card issuer, with unmatched branch-reach and pre-approved customer base[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**Auto-credit simplicity** — Two-day auto-credit of cashback removes redemption complexity and is a tangible UX advantage over points-based programmes[^fksbi-2026-06-30]

### What to do
**Own the international travel contrast** — Flipkart SBI Card's 3.5% forex markup is a ready-made negative for Scapia to surface in acquisition messaging targeting frequent flyers and international shoppers[^fksbi-2026-06-30]
**Target Cleartrip users who also travel abroad** — Cardholders using Cleartrip for domestic bookings but frustrated by high forex costs on international trips are a natural Scapia acquisition cohort [Inferred · Medium]
**Reinforce "no ecosystem lock" positioning** — Flipkart SBI Card's value evaporates for anyone outside the Flipkart universe; Scapia should highlight earn-anywhere travel rewards as a universal proposition[^fksbi-2026-06-30]

## Coverage

### Flipkart SBI Card Launched
SBI Card and Flipkart have launched a co-branded credit card delivering up to 7.5% cashback on Myntra, 5% on Flipkart/Shopsy/Cleartrip, and 4% on Zomato, Uber, and PVR — with a joining/annual fee of ₹500+GST, waivable at ₹3.5 lakh annual spend[^fksbi-2026-06-30]. The card is available on both Visa and Mastercard networks and features an auto-credit cashback mechanism that settles rewards within two days of statement generation, reducing redemption friction for daily-use shoppers[^fksbi-2026-06-30].

The card is clearly positioned for India's digital-first, Flipkart-ecosystem shopper — not for international travellers, a fact the article itself concedes given the steep 3.5% forex markup[^fksbi-2026-06-30]. For Scapia, the Cleartrip integration is the closest competitive overlap, but the ecosystem lock-in and forex penalty mean this card competes at the lifestyle/cashback end of the market rather than Scapia's travel-premium segment [Inferred · High]. The SBI Card–Flipkart partnership nonetheless signals that India's largest card issuer is doubling down on co-brand breadth and e-commerce stickiness as growth levers.
