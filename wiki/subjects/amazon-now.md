## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Travel-card specificity** — Scapia is purpose-built for travel rewards and zero forex markup; Amazon Now is a grocery-and-essentials play with no travel category overlap.
**No subscription gate** — Scapia benefits require no Prime membership; Amazon Now's stickiest engagement is gated behind Prime, creating churn risk for non-subscribers. [Inferred · High]

### Where they're ahead
**Ecosystem flywheel** — Prime membership, Amazon Pay UPI, and Amazon Now together create a daily-habit loop that reinforces Amazon's financial-services touchpoints far beyond Scapia's reach[^amznow-2026-06]
**Geographic reach ambition** — 300-city coverage will embed Amazon Pay as the default payment at checkout for millions of daily quick-commerce orders — expanding Amazon Pay's transaction volume significantly[^amznow-2026-06]
**CEO-level conviction** — A personal CEO visit and $300M committed investment signals a multi-year runway that competitors cannot match on pace[^amznow-2026-06]

### What to do
**Monitor Amazon Pay integration** — Watch whether Amazon Now checkout drives Amazon Pay UPI or co-brand card adoption; if so, Amazon Pay's everyday-spend stickiness intensifies against Scapia's travel positioning. [Inferred · High]
**Own the travel occasion** — Amazon Now competes on daily essentials, not travel; Scapia should sharpen messaging that travel rewards are a distinct, higher-value occasion Amazon's ecosystem does not serve.

## Coverage

### Amazon Now eyes 300 cities — 2026-06-30

Amazon CEO Andy Jassy visited India as the company announced Amazon Now — its quick-commerce unit launched in June 2025 — would expand from 15 locations to more than 300 cities[^amznow-2026-06]. Orders have doubled every quarter since launch, making it the fastest-growing business unit in Amazon India's history, and Country Manager Samir Kumar credited Prime members, who triple purchase frequency after adopting the service[^amznow-2026-06].

For Scapia, the direct product overlap is minimal — Amazon Now targets groceries and essentials, not travel. The strategic read is indirect but material: each Amazon Now checkout is another Amazon Pay UPI or co-brand card touchpoint, deepening the Prime ecosystem's financial lock-in and widening Amazon Pay's everyday-spend advantage over a travel-first card. [Inferred · High] A $300M India operations investment signals this is a sustained, boardroom-backed bet[^amznow-2026-06].
