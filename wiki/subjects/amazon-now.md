## What we know

**Market crossed ₹64,000 crore** — India's quick-commerce market exceeded ₹64,000 crore in 2025, targeting ₹1 lakh crore by 2030[^qcomm-overview-2026]
**Amazon Now launched 2025** — Amazon pivoted from Amazon Fresh (2–4 hour delivery, ~120 cities) to Amazon Now in 2025, targeting 10–20 minute dark-store delivery in Delhi and Mumbai first[^qcomm-overview-2026]
**1,000+ dark stores targeted** — Amazon is reportedly targeting 1,000+ dark stores by end of 2026, replacing Amazon Fresh in top 10–15 Tier-1 cities while Fresh continues in smaller markets[^qcomm-overview-2026]
**Blinkit leads at 46–50% share** — Blinkit (Zomato) holds the largest dark-store network and the highest market share; Zepto holds ~20–30%, Swiggy Instamart 18–22%, BigBasket BB Now 5–7%, Flipkart Minutes <3%[^qcomm-overview-2026]
**Average order values remain small** — Typical q-commerce basket is ₹200–₹600 vs ₹1,580–₹1,650+ for traditional e-commerce, reinforcing that q-commerce is a high-frequency, low-ticket habit loop[^qcomm-overview-2026]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Travel-card specificity** — Scapia is purpose-built for travel rewards and zero forex markup; Amazon Now is a grocery-and-essentials play with no travel category overlap.
**No subscription gate** — Scapia benefits require no Prime membership; Amazon Now's stickiest engagement is gated behind Prime, creating churn risk for non-subscribers. [Inferred · High]

### Where they're ahead
**Ecosystem flywheel** — Prime membership, Amazon Pay UPI, and Amazon Now together create a daily-habit loop that reinforces Amazon's financial-services touchpoints far beyond Scapia's reach[^amznow-2026-06]
**Geographic reach ambition** — 300-city coverage will embed Amazon Pay as the default payment at checkout for millions of daily quick-commerce orders — expanding Amazon Pay's transaction volume significantly[^amznow-2026-06]
**CEO-level conviction** — A personal CEO visit and $300M committed investment signals a multi-year runway that competitors cannot match on pace[^amznow-2026-06]

### What to do
**Monitor Amazon Pay integration** — Watch whether Amazon Now checkout drives Amazon Pay UPI or co-brand card adoption; if so, Amazon Pay's everyday-spend stickiness intensifies against Scapia's travel positioning. [Inferred · High]
**Own the travel occasion** — Amazon Now competes on daily essentials, not travel; Scapia should sharpen messaging that travel rewards are a distinct, higher-value occasion Amazon's ecosystem does not serve.

## Coverage

### India Q-Commerce Landscape 2026

India's quick-commerce market crossed ₹64,000 crore in 2025 and is on a path to ₹1 lakh crore by 2030, driven by Blinkit (46–50% share), Zepto (~20–30%), Swiggy Instamart (18–22%), and a chasing pack that includes Flipkart Minutes and Amazon Now[^qcomm-overview-2026]. The model's defining architecture — dark stores within 2–3 km, 2,000–3,000 SKUs, sub-20-minute delivery — creates a dense daily-habit loop with average baskets of ₹200–₹600.

Amazon Now enters this race late but with deep pockets, pivoting away from Amazon Fresh's 2–4 hour window to a direct 10–20 minute dark-store play starting in Delhi and Mumbai, with a target of 1,000+ dark stores by end-2026[^qcomm-overview-2026]. For Scapia, the competitive read remains indirect: every Amazon Now checkout is a potential Amazon Pay touchpoint reinforcing daily-spend stickiness that a travel-occasion card must consciously counterprogram. [Inferred · Medium]

### Amazon Now at 1,000 Dark Stores — 2026-06-24

Amazon Now has reached 1,000 dark stores across 50+ cities, confirmed in a sector-wide snapshot published June 24[^qcomm-2026-06-24]. It sits at the lower end of city penetration compared to Flipkart Minutes (130+ cities at the same store count) and well behind Blinkit's 2,243-store, 200-city network — signalling Amazon Now is still consolidating depth before breadth.

The broader quick-commerce picture is one of rapid infrastructure arms-racing, with all five major players expanding simultaneously[^qcomm-2026-06-24]. For Scapia, the relevance remains indirect: each Amazon Now or Flipkart Minutes checkout deepens a rival ecosystem's payment habit loop — Amazon Pay or PhonePe — reinforcing daily-spend stickiness that a travel-occasion card must consciously counterprogram. [Inferred · Medium]

### Amazon Now eyes 300 cities — 2026-06-30

Amazon CEO Andy Jassy visited India as the company announced Amazon Now — its quick-commerce unit launched in June 2025 — would expand from 15 locations to more than 300 cities[^amznow-2026-06]. Orders have doubled every quarter since launch, making it the fastest-growing business unit in Amazon India's history, and Country Manager Samir Kumar credited Prime members, who triple purchase frequency after adopting the service[^amznow-2026-06].

For Scapia, the direct product overlap is minimal — Amazon Now targets groceries and essentials, not travel. The strategic read is indirect but material: each Amazon Now checkout is another Amazon Pay UPI or co-brand card touchpoint, deepening the Prime ecosystem's financial lock-in and widening Amazon Pay's everyday-spend advantage over a travel-first card. [Inferred · High] A $300M India operations investment signals this is a sustained, boardroom-backed bet[^amznow-2026-06].
