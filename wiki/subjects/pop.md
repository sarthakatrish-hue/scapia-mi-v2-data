## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Travel-specific value prop** — Scapia is purpose-built for travel: zero forex markup, lounge access, flight/hotel rewards; POP's POPcoins are horizontal multi-brand currency with no disclosed travel depth.
**Regulated Visa co-brand** — Scapia operates a Federal Bank co-branded Visa card accepted globally; POP's RuPay card is domestically focused and early-stage at 40,000 cards [^pop-razorpay-2026-06-22].
**Cardholder base maturity** — Scapia has an established cardholder base; POP is in early card-issuance phase with limited data on credit quality or retention.

### Where they're ahead
**Daily UPI habit** — 6 lakh daily transactions creates a daily engagement loop Scapia's card-only model cannot match; habit formation at the UPI layer is structurally stickier [^pop-razorpay-2026-06-22].
**Merchant network backing** — Razorpay's millions of existing merchants become instant POP redemption partners — a ready-made rewards network Scapia would take years to assemble [^pop-razorpay-2026-06-22] [Inferred · High].
**Fresh capital at scale** — $30 million dedicated to product, merchant growth, and rewards subsidisation gives POP room to outspend on user acquisition and reward rates [^pop-razorpay-2026-06-22].

### What to do
**Flag travel redemption expansion** — POPcoins in travel (flights, hotels, visa fees) would make POP a direct Scapia substitute; monitor product announcements closely.
**Watch card issuance velocity** — If POP scales past 2–3 lakh RuPay cards under Razorpay ownership, it becomes a meaningful card competitor in the digitally-native segment Scapia targets.

## Coverage

### POP Raises $30M, Razorpay Majority Stake
POP, a rewards-first UPI payments app launched in June 2024, raised $30 million from Razorpay, which simultaneously acquired a majority stake in the Bengaluru startup [^pop-razorpay-2026-06-22]. The company reports 6 lakh daily transactions and 1 million unique monthly users in its first year, and has issued over 40,000 RuPay credit cards with Yes Bank as its banking partner [^pop-razorpay-2026-06-22]. Proceeds will be deployed across product development, merchant base expansion, and its rewards programme.

POP's central product is POPcoins — a multi-brand rewards currency that users earn on UPI payments and redeem across the POP merchant network [^pop-razorpay-2026-06-22]. The Razorpay acquisition adds instant scale: Razorpay's existing merchant payment infrastructure becomes a natural distribution and redemption layer for POPcoins, closing a rewards loop that previously required POP to build its merchant network from scratch [^pop-razorpay-2026-06-22] [Inferred · High].

For Scapia, POP represents a new-model competitor: not a bank issuing a premium card, but a UPI-native platform building rewards habit at the daily-spend layer, with a credit card as a secondary instrument [Inferred · High]. The overlap is sharpest among digitally-native, rewards-motivated consumers — Scapia's core target — who may find their rewards appetite satisfied within the POP ecosystem before they graduate to a dedicated travel card [Inferred · Medium]. The one current distinction is travel specificity: Scapia's zero-forex, lounge-access, travel-rewards stack has no equivalent in POP's current offering, but that gap is a product decision away from closing.
