## What we know

**iShop value-back quantified** — On ₹1.8L spend, ICICI Emeralde via iShop returns ₹43,800 — above HSBC Rewards Marketplace (₹39,900) but below HDFC SmartBuy (₹56,668) [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]
**Broader redemption than HSBC** — iShop allows redemption against travel bookings, vouchers, and other categories — not travel-only like HSBC Marketplace [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]
**Hotel earn parity with HSBC Premier** — Emeralde's hotel earn via iShop matches HSBC Premier at ₹28,800 on ₹80K hotel spend; stronger on shopping (₹5,400 vs ₹900) [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Zero forex edge** — Scapia's 0% forex markup beats Emeralde's 2% on every international spend.
**No annual fee** — Scapia's ₹0 vs Emeralde's ₹12,000 + GST; no spend waiver required.
**No gating** — Scapia's travel benefits are universally accessible; iShop and Emeralde are premium-tier locked.

### Where they're ahead
**Multi-category redemption** — iShop supports voucher and shopping redemption alongside travel, adding flexibility Scapia's travel-first model lacks.
**Mid-tier portal value** — ₹43,800 return on ₹1.8L spend (~24.3%) is a meaningful value-back that attracts aspirational premium spenders [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26].
**Strong hotel earn rate** — ₹28,800 reward on ₹80K hotel spend is competitive with top-tier HSBC Premier and appeals to frequent hotel bookers.

### What to do
**Target the gap below SmartBuy** — Emeralde holders know they're getting less than SmartBuy; Scapia can intercept with zero-fee, zero-forex travel positioning for those unwilling to pay ₹12,000 fees.
**Probe iShop partner depth** — Track new partner additions to iShop voucher/shopping categories; any expansion could push Emeralde's value-back closer to SmartBuy territory [Inferred · Medium].

## Coverage

### iShop Benchmarked Against Rivals — 2026-06-26

A Paisabazaar comparison places ICICI Emeralde's iShop portal in the middle of the premium travel rewards pack — returning ₹43,800 on ₹1.8L spend, ahead of HSBC Rewards Marketplace (₹39,900) but meaningfully behind HDFC SmartBuy (₹56,668) [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]. iShop's advantage over HSBC lies in its broader redemption scope — vouchers and shopping in addition to travel — while its deficit vs SmartBuy stems from lower flight and shopping earn multipliers.

For Scapia, iShop reinforces that ICICI Emeralde competes on ecosystem breadth rather than rate purity [Inferred · Medium]. The 2% forex and ₹12,000 annual fee remain structural openings: Scapia can credibly argue that its zero-cost, zero-markup structure delivers comparable real-world savings for the frequent international traveller who doesn't need a multi-category voucher ecosystem [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26].
