## What we know

**Airline Co-brand Debut** — BOBCARD entered the airline co-brand space with two Etihad Guest Credit Cards via BOB Financial Solutions; the Premium variant is a metal card targeting frequent international travellers[^bobcard-etihad-issuer-2026-06-30]
**Etihad Partnership Migration** — Etihad Airways ended its five-year SBI Card partnership and moved exclusively to BOBCARD, giving BOBCARD a significant airline loyalty anchor[^bobcard-etihad-issuer-2026-06-30]
**Gold Status as Acquisition Hook** — Etihad Guest Gold status — normally requiring 50,000 Tier Miles — is granted on a single etihad.com purchase, a powerful hook for Etihad loyalists[^bobcard-etihad-issuer-2026-06-30]
**Zero Forex on Premium Variant** — 0% foreign currency markup on the Etihad Guest Premium card stacks with miles earn on international spends[^bobcard-etihad-issuer-2026-06-30]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**No joining fee barrier** — Scapia is fee-free; BOBCARD Etihad Premium costs ₹5,000+GST to open, even with a ₹5L spend waiver pathway[^bobcard-etihad-issuer-2026-06-30]
**Alliance-agnostic rewards** — Scapia's value is not locked to a single non-alliance carrier; BOBCARD Etihad's proposition collapses if the cardholder doesn't fly Etihad[^bobcard-etihad-issuer-2026-06-30]

**Aspirational status layer** — Etihad Guest Gold status with business class lounge access and chauffeur service is a premium hook Scapia has no equivalent for[^bobcard-etihad-issuer-2026-06-30]
**Metal card signal** — Physical metal card targets premium aspirational travellers; Scapia does not currently offer a metal variant[Inferred · Medium]

### What to do

## Coverage

### BOBCARD Adds Etihad Co-brand Anchor — 2026-06-30

BOBCARD debuted in the airline co-brand space with the Etihad Guest Premium Credit Card, inheriting the Etihad partnership that SBI Card held for five years before its discontinuation[^bobcard-etihad-issuer-2026-06-30]. The metal card adds a meaningful new dimension to BOBCARD's travel strategy: on top of its multi-OTA discount blitz (tracked separately), it now offers a premium frequent-flyer product with Etihad Guest Gold status, 0% forex markup, and no-spend-gate lounge access at a ₹5,000+GST fee waivable at ₹5L spend.

For Scapia, BOBCARD is now running a two-pronged travel offensive — mass-market OTA discounts and a premium airline co-brand — that makes it a more complete travel competitor than any single prior move suggested[^bobcard-etihad-issuer-2026-06-30]. The Etihad card's long exclusion list, low miles redemption value, and single-carrier lock-in are structural weaknesses Scapia can exploit, but the Gold status hook and metal card positioning signal BOBCARD is serious about owning the premium travel segment [Inferred · High].

### BOBCARD's Multi-OTA Travel Push — 2026-05-29

BOBCARD Ltd, the credit card arm of Bank of Baroda, launched a wide-ranging summer travel offer spanning Air India, MakeMyTrip, EaseMyTrip, Goibibo, Paytm, Yatra, Ixigo, and Flipkart Travel — valid through June 30 2026[^bobcard-travel-2026-06-25]. The headline benefits include up to ₹8,000 off Air India bookings and up to 20% on Flipkart Travel weekends, with most deals structured around no-cost EMI tenures of three to 24 months rather than flat cashback.

For Scapia, the competitive read is clear: BOBCARD is staking out travel as its primary engagement category and using EMI-gating to drive deeper credit usage[^bobcard-travel-2026-06-25]. The breadth of OTA tie-ups is a gap Scapia should watch. However, BOBCARD's offers are time-bounded and transaction-restricted; Scapia's always-on forex-free model and persistent rewards remain structurally simpler — and the expiry of BOBCARD's deals in days presents an acquisition window [Inferred · Medium].
