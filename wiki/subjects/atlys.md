## What we know

**Series C funding closed** — Atlys raised $36M led by Susquehanna Asia Venture Capital; Elevation Capital, Long Journey Ventures, and Peak XV Partners also participated.[^atlys-c]
**MakeMyTrip joins as investor** — India's largest OTA entered as a new investor, signalling a likely distribution or co-product relationship.[^atlys-c]
**Scale and growth trajectory** — Atlys runs at 700K+ annual visa applications and has recorded 11X growth since its Series B in 2024.[^atlys-c]
**International markets now half the business** — UAE, US, UK, and Australia together contribute nearly 50% of overall revenue.[^atlys-c]
**Artionis UK acquisition** — In February 2025 Atlys acquired the UK unit of visa-services firm Artionis in an all-cash deal to deepen European infrastructure.[^atlys-c]
**AI roadmap being scaled** — Capital will fund AI deployment across document verification, eligibility checks, and traveller support.[^atlys-c]
**Funding history** — Seed $4.25M (Oct 2021) → Series A $12M (Sep 2023) → Series B $20M (Sep 2024) → Series C $36M (2026).[^atlys-c]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Card-linked travel rewards** — Scapia bundles zero-forex markup and reward points on travel spend; Atlys has no credit or rewards layer.[Inferred · High]
**One-app travel wallet** — Scapia integrates flights, stays, and card management; Atlys is purely a visa-application tool with no broader booking surface today.[Inferred · High]
**Federal Bank trust signal** — Co-branding with a regulated bank provides credibility for high-value travellers that a standalone fintech cannot replicate immediately.[Inferred · Medium]

### Where they're ahead
**Visa processing depth** — 700K+ annual applications across 120+ destinations[^atlys-c] is a moat Scapia has zero current answer to; visa facilitation is a key travel-card use case.
**AI investment velocity** — Fresh $36M earmarked for AI across document verification and eligibility[^atlys-c] will widen the capability gap quickly.
**MakeMyTrip distribution** — New strategic investor gives Atlys access to India's largest OTA audience[^atlys-c], a distribution channel Scapia does not control.
**Global infrastructure** — Artionis UK acquisition[^atlys-c] gives Atlys on-the-ground processing assets in Europe; Scapia has no equivalent.

### What to do
**Explore visa partnership** — A co-branded or affiliate visa-facilitation feature (even white-labelled) would close the most visible travel-card gap for Scapia users.[Inferred · Medium]
**Monitor MakeMyTrip overlap** — MakeMyTrip's Atlys stake could erode Scapia's ability to negotiate preferential booking integrations; review current OTA relationships now.[Inferred · Medium]
**Lean into rewards differentiation** — Promote zero-forex and reward-on-visa-fee spend messaging to own the "cost of travel" framing before Atlys adds a payment layer.[Inferred · High]
