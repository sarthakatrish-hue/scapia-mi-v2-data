## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Travel-category exclusivity** — Flipkart Minutes targets grocery and electronics for rapid delivery; Scapia's rewards are built around travel spend — no direct category overlap today.
**No subscription gate for core benefits** — Scapia's travel rewards require no Flipkart Plus or equivalent membership; lower friction for infrequent travel buyers.

### Where they're ahead
**Expansion velocity** — 100 dark stores per month is the fastest build pace among all six players; at this rate Flipkart Minutes reaches Zepto-level physical scale by mid-2026[^qcwar-2026-06-07]
**Electronics category** — Uniquely targets high-ticket electronics via dark stores, which could anchor higher-AOV q-commerce habits and eventually support EMI or co-brand card products on Flipkart's existing NBFC infrastructure[^qcwar-2026-06-07]
**Walmart balance sheet** — Rs 3,248 Cr injection signals a multi-year commitment; Flipkart can sustain losses longer than pure-play startups[^qcwar-2026-06-07]

### What to do
**Watch for Flipkart co-brand card extension** — Flipkart already has the Flipkart Axis Bank card; if Minutes drives daily checkout habits, Scapia's cards category faces intensified everyday-spend competition. [Inferred · High]
**Track IPO timeline** — A listed Flipkart with a standalone Minutes app and fintech ambitions represents a step-change in competitive intensity across both store and cards categories.

## Coverage

### Flipkart Minutes' 100-Store Monthly Sprint — 2026-06-07

Flipkart Minutes is the most aggressive expander in India's quick-commerce war, adding roughly 100 dark stores every month in 2026 per TechCrunch and Bernstein notes, placing it on track for ~1,200 stores by June — broadly matching Zepto and Swiggy Instamart in physical scale despite holding only 3-4% market share[^qcwar-2026-06-07]. Flipkart's Singapore entity injected Rs 3,248 Cr to fund the push, and the platform is the only major player targeting electronics and phones alongside grocery in dark stores[^qcwar-2026-06-07].

For Scapia, the read is forward-looking: today's overlap is minimal, but Flipkart's existing co-brand card relationship with Axis Bank, combined with daily Minutes checkout habits, could deepen everyday-spend card stickiness. A standalone Minutes app and Flipkart IPO in the next 12 months would sharpen that risk materially. [Inferred · High]
