## What we know

**TCS from first rupee** — Overseas tour packages attract 5% TCS from rupee one; self-booked forex gets a ₹7 lakh nil-TCS runway before 20% kicks in[^tcs-travel-2026]
**Packages defined broadly** — Any booking bundling two or more of flight, hotel, or similar expenses on one invoice qualifies as a package and triggers the stricter rate[^tcs-travel-2026]
**Creditable, not a cost** — TCS is deposited against the traveller's PAN and appears in Form 26AS and AIS; it offsets income tax liability and any surplus is refunded[^tcs-travel-2026]
**Separate threshold buckets** — Package TCS and general LRS TCS limits are tracked independently, not pooled, which can work in the traveller's favour[^tcs-travel-2026]
**Financial-year reset** — The ₹7 lakh threshold resets every 1 April; splitting large trip payments across two FYs can keep more spend in the 5% band[^tcs-travel-2026]

## Regulatory Intel

**Status:** Active — rules in effect since October 2023, operative in FY 2025-26 and 2026-27[^tcs-travel-2026]

**What it requires:** Tour operators, banks, and forex dealers must collect TCS at source — 5% on overseas tour packages up to ₹7 lakh aggregate per financial year, 20% above ₹7 lakh; nil up to ₹7 lakh for self-booked forex/LRS remittances, 20% beyond. Sellers deposit the amount against the buyer's PAN and must issue Form 27D.[^tcs-travel-2026]

**Posture:** TCS is a cash-flow friction, not a permanent cost — it is fully creditable against income tax liability and refundable via ITR filing. The asymmetry between packaged and self-booked travel creates structural incentives for travellers to unbundle bookings.[^tcs-travel-2026]

**Implication for Scapia:** Scapia's card-based, self-booked model sits in the more favourable LRS bucket (nil TCS up to ₹7 lakh), giving it a natural positioning advantage over bundled tour operators when communicating total cost to value-conscious travellers. Scapia should make this distinction explicit in acquisition and education content — the effective price difference between booking a package vs. booking flights and hotels independently via a travel card is material at typical mid-market spend levels [Inferred · High]. Any future co-branded package product or OTA partnership that bundles two or more travel components on one invoice would shift Scapia-referred bookings into the 5% package TCS regime and should be structured carefully.[^tcs-travel-2026]

## Coverage

### TCS Rules Shape Travel Booking Behaviour

The 5% TCS rate on overseas tour packages — applying from the first rupee, unlike the ₹7 lakh nil-threshold for self-booked forex — creates a structural cost asymmetry between bundled and unbundled travel spending[^tcs-travel-2026]. At ₹4 lakh of holiday spend, a package buyer parts with ₹20,000 upfront in TCS while a card-based self-booker owes nothing until FY-end reconciliation.

The money is not lost — TCS flows into Form 26AS against the traveller's PAN and is fully set off against income tax, with any surplus refunded after ITR filing[^tcs-travel-2026]. For Scapia, whose card-first model sits in the favourable LRS bucket, this regulation is a quiet tailwind: the platform can credibly position independent booking as both flexible and tax-efficient relative to packaged tours [Inferred · High].
