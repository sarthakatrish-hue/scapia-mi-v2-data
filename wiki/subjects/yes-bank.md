## What we know

**Credit-on-UPI issuer** — YES Bank is the banking partner powering BharatPe Flex, a Credit-on-UPI product launched June 2026 [^bharatpe-flex-2026-06-02]
**Fintech co-brand expansion** — YES Bank is actively partnering with fintechs (BharatPe, Uni NxtWave) to extend credit via UPI and card rails, signalling a deliberate fintech-distribution strategy [^bharatpe-flex-2026-06-02]
**UPI credit infrastructure** — Bank positions itself as having strong UPI technology infrastructure capable of embedding credit lines into existing UPI payment flows [^bharatpe-flex-2026-06-02]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Zero-fee travel focus** — Scapia's lifetime-free travel card targets a distinct segment; YES Bank's Credit-on-UPI play is everyday-spend oriented, not travel-rewards oriented.

### Where they're ahead
**UPI credit distribution** — YES Bank is embedding credit directly into UPI flows via fintech partners, reaching mass-market users at the payment moment — a channel Scapia does not occupy [^bharatpe-flex-2026-06-02].
**Multi-partner fintech reach** — With both BharatPe and Uni NxtWave as issuing partners, YES Bank is assembling a fintech-first credit distribution network that broadens its addressable user base well beyond branch walk-ins [^bharatpe-flex-2026-06-02].

### What to do
**Monitor UPI credit penetration** — If Credit-on-UPI products from YES Bank partnerships gain scale, they could pre-empt the credit-first consumer before they ever seek a travel card — watch adoption metrics closely.

## Coverage

### YES Bank Powers BharatPe Credit-on-UPI — 2026-06-02

BharatPe launched BharatPe Flex, a Credit-on-UPI product powered by YES Bank, enabling customers to make UPI payments — QR codes, online, bills, travel, fuel — against an integrated credit line with up to 45 days interest-free repayment and EMI conversion options [^bharatpe-flex-2026-06-02]. Onboarding is fully digital with approvals in under three minutes for eligible users [^bharatpe-flex-2026-06-02].

For YES Bank, this confirms an accelerating strategy of plugging its credit infrastructure into high-distribution fintech apps rather than competing on branch acquisition alone [^bharatpe-flex-2026-06-02]. The bank is now a confirmed issuing partner for at least two fintech credit programmes (BharatPe Flex and Uni NxtWave), positioning itself as a behind-the-scenes credit engine for the next wave of embedded finance [Inferred · High].

For Scapia, the indirect signal is notable: YES Bank's UPI-credit model meets consumers at the transactional layer with flexible everyday credit, which is a structurally different — but potentially competing — use case for the same credit-seeking, digitally-native consumer Scapia targets [Inferred · Medium]. Travel rewards card acquisition could face earlier-stage competition if UPI credit lines satisfy routine spending needs before users graduate to a dedicated travel card.
