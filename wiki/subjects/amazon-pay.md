## What we know

**Rewards Gold programme launched** — Amazon Pay introduced Rewards Gold, offering up to 5% cashback to users who complete 25 transactions in any 3-month rolling window.[^amzpay-20250710]
**Prime vs non-Prime split** — Prime members earn 5% cashback; non-Prime users earn 3% on eligible transactions — no stated cap on either tier.[^amzpay-20250710]
**Broad category coverage** — Cashback applies across groceries, clothing, food delivery (Zomato, Domino's/District, Ola), hotel bookings, entertainment (JioHotstar, Xbox), and offline at 55,000+ merchants.[^amzpay-20250710]
**Travel vertical included** — Hotel bookings explicitly earn 5% unlimited cashback, bringing Amazon Pay into direct competition on the stays category.[^amzpay-20250710]
**Stacks with ICICI co-brand** — Rewards Gold cashback is additive to Amazon Pay ICICI credit card rewards, deepening the moat for existing ICICI cardholders.[^amzpay-20250710]
**Activation threshold is UPI-friendly** — Any 25 transactions — UPI, QR scan, recharge, send money — count toward qualification, lowering friction significantly.[^amzpay-20250710]
**Timed to Prime Day** — Launch comes days before the July 12–14 Prime Day sale, suggesting an acquisition and engagement push timed to peak spend.[^amzpay-20250710]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**No qualification gate** — Scapia benefits are active from card activation; Amazon Pay's 5% rate sits behind a 25-transaction/3-month threshold that lapses if usage drops. [Inferred · High]
**Travel-first design** — Scapia's zero forex markup, unlimited domestic lounge access, and dedicated travel rewards are purpose-built for travelers; Amazon Pay's travel cashback is one line in a generalist programme.[^amzpay-20250710]
**Zero forex markup** — International spends on Scapia cost nothing extra; Amazon Pay UPI and cashback have no comparable cross-border proposition. [Inferred · High]
**No Prime dependency** — Scapia's reward rate does not require an additional subscription; Amazon Pay's best tier (5%) is locked behind Prime membership.[^amzpay-20250710]

### Where they're ahead
**Distribution scale** — Amazon Pay reaches hundreds of millions of Amazon.in and UPI users; Scapia's cardholder base is a fraction of that addressable pool.[^amzpay-20250710]
**Offline merchant network** — 55,000+ merchants and 5,000+ brand stores give Rewards Gold broad everyday-spend coverage that a travel card cannot replicate.[^amzpay-20250710]
**ICICI card stacking** — Existing Amazon Pay ICICI holders double-dip, creating a sticky two-product bundle that raises switching cost significantly.[^amzpay-20250710]
**Entertainment & grocery breadth** — Hotstar, Xbox, Zomato, grocery — categories where Scapia has no specific reward differentiation — are core Rewards Gold earners.[^amzpay-20250710]

### What to do
**Expose the gate** — In acquisition messaging, contrast Scapia's always-on benefits against Rewards Gold's 25-transaction unlock bar; many aspirational users will not qualify every quarter. [Inferred · High]
**Double down on travel depth** — Amazon Pay's travel cashback is shallow (hotels only, no flights, no lounge, no forex edge); Scapia should own the "travel card, not a payments app" narrative.
**Target lapsed Prime users** — Non-Prime users earn only 3% under Rewards Gold; Scapia offers a consistent proposition regardless of subscription status — a clear message for that segment. [Inferred · Medium]
**Watch stays competition** — 5% on hotel bookings is a direct shots-fired moment for Scapia's stays category; monitor whether Amazon Pay integrates deeper OTA inventory or adds flight cashback.

## Coverage

### Amazon Pay Launches Rewards Gold — 2025-07-10

Amazon Pay launched Rewards Gold, a tiered cashback programme that pays Prime members 5% and non-Prime users 3% on eligible transactions after they complete any 25 payments or purchases within a rolling 3-month window.[^amzpay-20250710] The programme covers groceries, food delivery, offline retail, entertainment, and — directly relevant to Scapia — hotel bookings, with cashback described as unlimited and assured.[^amzpay-20250710] The timing, days before Prime Day (July 12–14), signals a deliberate acquisition and habit-formation push.[^amzpay-20250710]

For Scapia, the stay-category inclusion is the sharpest competitive edge.[^amzpay-20250710] Amazon Pay is effectively telling travelers they can earn 5% on hotel bookings through an app they already use daily — without a dedicated travel card. The ICICI co-brand stacking further entrenches existing Amazon Pay card users, reducing their incentive to seek a second card.[^amzpay-20250710] The volume-gating mechanism is Rewards Gold's structural weakness: users who dip below 25 transactions lose status, creating a churn risk Amazon Pay will need to manage. [Inferred · High]

Scapia's clearest counter is specificity: Rewards Gold is a generalist cashback layer with a travel line item, while Scapia is a travel credit card with zero forex markup, unlimited lounge access, and no subscription or transaction gate.[^amzpay-20250710] The risk is that for travelers who are already Prime members and heavy Amazon Pay UPI users, the marginal case for a dedicated travel card narrows — particularly on domestic hotel bookings where the 5% Rewards Gold rate is competitive. [Inferred · Medium]
