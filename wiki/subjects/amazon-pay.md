## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**No Prime lock-in** — Scapia's travel rewards are unconditional; Amazon's highest-value quick-commerce and cashback benefits funnel through Prime membership, adding a subscription dependency.[^amzn-jassy-2026-06-24]
**Travel-first clarity** — Amazon Now is groceries, daily essentials, and convenience; Scapia owns the dedicated travel card narrative — zero forex, lounge access, flight/hotel rewards — with no conflation.[Inferred · High]
**Zero forex on cross-border** — Amazon Pay has no cross-border forex proposition; as Indians travel internationally, Scapia's structural cost advantage remains unchallenged by Amazon's India build-out.[Inferred · High]

### Where they're ahead
**Scale and velocity** — 300+ cities, $35B committed, orders doubling every quarter — Amazon's India investment dwarfs what any co-branded travel card can match in distribution breadth.[^amzn-jassy-2026-06-24]
**Prime flywheel compounding** — Prime members tripling frequency on Amazon Now deepens daily-spend lock-in, making Amazon Pay the habitual payment method long before a travel moment arises.[^amzn-jassy-2026-06-24]
**AI and cloud infrastructure moat** — Amazon's AI data centre investment creates ecosystem stickiness (Alexa, AWS, personalisation) that can eventually enhance Amazon Pay's offer quality.[^amzn-jassy-2026-06-24]
**Flipkart Minutes benchmark pressure** — Flipkart's 1,000 MFC network across 130 cities and 5x order growth shows the quick-commerce bar is rising industry-wide, with payment apps as the default wallet.[^amzn-jassy-2026-06-24]

### What to do
**Own the "after checkout" travel moment** — Amazon Now wins the daily grocery mission; Scapia should own the travel planning and booking mission — a distinct, infrequent, high-value spend that quick-commerce can't replicate.
**Target Prime users planning international trips** — These users are already Amazon Pay heavy users domestically; the forex saving and lounge access pitch is credible and adds value Prime doesn't offer.[Inferred · Medium]
**Monitor Amazon Pay + Now integration** — If Amazon ties Amazon Pay cashback directly to Amazon Now orders at higher rates, it could shift wallet-of-choice dynamics for urban millennials who also travel.[Inferred · High]

## Coverage

### Jassy's India Blitz — Quick Commerce War — 2026-06-24

Amazon CEO Andy Jassy's first India visit since 2021 centred on a decisive quick-commerce offensive: Amazon Now will expand to 300+ cities, backed by a $35 billion India investment through 2030 covering AI, cloud, ecommerce, and logistics[^amzn-jassy-2026-06-24]. Orders on the service have doubled every quarter since launch, and Prime members who adopt it triple their shopping frequency — tightening the flywheel between Amazon Pay and daily consumer spend[^amzn-jassy-2026-06-24].

For Scapia, the threat is structural rather than direct: Amazon is building the payment habit infrastructure for urban India's fastest-growing consumer segment at a scale that dwarfs travel-card distribution[^amzn-jassy-2026-06-24]. Flipkart Minutes' parallel surge — 1,000 MFCs, 5x order growth, 42x Tier-II expansion — confirms quick commerce is the new battleground for wallet share. Scapia's insulation lies in owning the travel moment: zero forex, lounges, and flight/hotel rewards remain outside what any grocery delivery network can replicate.[Inferred · High]

### Amazon Pay Confirmed UPI Top 5
The 2026 UPI landscape article confirms Amazon Pay holds a top-5 position among Indian UPI apps, on a platform that now serves 500 million unique users and processes 21 billion transactions monthly[^upi2026-amzp-jun24]. PhonePe (48.3%) and Google Pay (37%) dominate, but the sheer scale of UPI growth means even sub-5% players like Amazon Pay are riding a structurally expanding base — reinforcing the distribution advantage Amazon Pay already wields via its e-commerce and Prime ecosystem.

### Amazon Pay Launches Rewards Gold — 2025-07-10

Amazon Pay launched Rewards Gold, a tiered cashback programme that pays Prime members 5% and non-Prime users 3% on eligible transactions after they complete any 25 payments or purchases within a rolling 3-month window.[^amzpay-20250710] The programme covers groceries, food delivery, offline retail, entertainment, and — directly relevant to Scapia — hotel bookings, with cashback described as unlimited and assured.[^amzpay-20250710] The timing, days before Prime Day (July 12–14), signals a deliberate acquisition and habit-formation push.[^amzpay-20250710]

For Scapia, the stay-category inclusion is the sharpest competitive edge.[^amzpay-20250710] Amazon Pay is effectively telling travelers they can earn 5% on hotel bookings through an app they already use daily — without a dedicated travel card. The ICICI co-brand stacking further entrenches existing Amazon Pay card users, reducing their incentive to seek a second card.[^amzpay-20250710] The volume-gating mechanism is Rewards Gold's structural weakness: users who dip below 25 transactions lose status, creating a churn risk Amazon Pay will need to manage. [Inferred · High]

Scapia's clearest counter is specificity: Rewards Gold is a generalist cashback layer with a travel line item, while Scapia is a travel credit card with zero forex markup, unlimited lounge access, and no subscription or transaction gate.[^amzpay-20250710] The risk is that for travelers who are already Prime members and heavy Amazon Pay UPI users, the marginal case for a dedicated travel card narrows — particularly on domestic hotel bookings where the 5% Rewards Gold rate is competitive. [Inferred · Medium]
