## What we know

**Broad rewards rollback** — BankBazaar data shows a pattern of steady devaluation across SBI Card, Amex, Axis, HDFC, and ICICI — not isolated cuts[^deval-2026-06-25]
**Benefit-to-behaviour shift** — Adhil Shetty (BankBazaar CEO) confirms issuers are moving from flat cashback to spending-behaviour-linked structures[^deval-2026-06-25]
**Lounge access tightened** — SBI Card restricted domestic lounge benefits on select co-branded cards from January 10 2026[^deval-2026-06-25]
**Insurance removed** — SBI Card Elite and Miles Elite lost Rs 1 crore air accident insurance cover from July 15 2025[^deval-2026-06-25]
**Fuel earns zeroed** — Amex India stopped awarding Membership Rewards points on fuel spends from June 12 2025[^deval-2026-06-25]
**Fragmented disclosure** — Axis, HDFC, and ICICI are communicating changes via SMS/email rather than public notices, making tracking difficult for customers[^deval-2026-06-25]
**Co-brand discontinuations** — SBI Card has discontinued Etihad Guest and OLA Money variants as part of portfolio rationalisation[^deval-2026-06-25]
**Fee threshold hike** — BPCL SBI Credit Card annual fee waiver threshold doubled from Rs 50,000 to Rs 1 lakh[^deval-2026-06-25]

## Market Intel

**Direction:** Tailwind

**What's shifting:** Incumbents across SBI Card, Amex, Axis, HDFC, and ICICI are systematically narrowing reward eligibility, tightening lounge access, raising fee-waiver thresholds, and communicating changes through fragmented channels — shifting from universal "benefit-led" cards to opaque "behaviour-led" structures that favour heavy, category-specific spenders[^deval-2026-06-25].

**Why it matters:** Each devaluation widens the trust deficit between large issuers and their cardholders. Users who signed up for premium bundled benefits are losing them quietly; the BankBazaar CEO explicitly calls this a "reset in the rewards system"[^deval-2026-06-25]. Devaluation fatigue is a real acquisition lever for transparent challengers.

**Implication:** Scapia's lifetime-free, travel-first card with no hidden category carve-outs is well-positioned to capture users fleeing devaluation fatigue. The discontinuation of Etihad-linked SBI Card variants specifically creates a pool of frequent-flyer-oriented users now without a co-branded travel card — a direct acquisition target [Inferred · High]. Scapia should amplify messaging around rewards consistency and transparent benefit structures while incumbents absorb reputational friction from their cuts.

## Coverage

### India's Card Devaluation Wave — 2026-04-09

India's major credit card issuers are in the middle of a sustained benefits rollback, with SBI Card, American Express, Axis, HDFC, and ICICI all tightening rewards, lounge access, and insurance cover across 2025–26[^deval-2026-06-25]. BankBazaar CEO Adhil Shetty describes it as a structural shift — "from benefit-led cards to behaviour-led cards" — where passive spend no longer earns meaningful rewards[^deval-2026-06-25]. Notably, SBI Card has axed its Etihad Guest co-brand entirely, and Amex has zeroed out fuel earnings.

The strategic read for Scapia: every devaluation announcement by an incumbent is an unsolicited advertisement for a simpler, transparent alternative. The pool of disenchanted premium cardholders — particularly former Etihad SBI Card holders now without a travel co-brand — is a warm acquisition segment. Scapia's consistent, travel-first rewards structure and lifetime-free model look relatively stronger with each round of incumbent cuts [Inferred · High].
