## What we know

**Hopper-powered portal** — HSBC launched Rewards Marketplace in partnership with Hopper, letting select cardholders earn up to 12X on hotels and 6X on flights [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]
**Travel-only redemption** — Points redeem only against flights and hotels (up to ₹1/point); no vouchers or shopping unlike HDFC SmartBuy or ICICI iShop [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]
**Select card access** — Available only to Privé, Premier, TravelOne, Visa Platinum, and RuPay Platinum cardholders; not universal [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]
**Bonus points cap** — Accelerated rewards capped at 18,000 bonus points per month; base rewards uncapped [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]
**Protective travel features** — Offers zero forex fees (bookings in INR), price drop protection (up to ₹1,500 refund in 10 days), best-price guarantee, and cancel-for-any-reason (up to 100% refund) [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]
**Trails SmartBuy on value** — Head-to-head comparison on ₹1.8L spend: SmartBuy returns ₹56,668 vs Rewards Marketplace ₹39,900 [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Zero booking fees** — Scapia charges no convenience fees; HSBC levies ₹300 domestic and ₹700 international per leg per customer.
**No forex markup** — Scapia's 0% forex beats HSBC's travel portal requirement to book in INR only (masking underlying conversion spread).
**Open access** — Scapia benefits apply across all spend; HSBC Rewards Marketplace is gated to five selected card tiers.

### Where they're ahead
**High hotel earn rate** — Up to 12X on hotel bookings for top-tier Privé holders is a strong accelerated reward hook Scapia cannot directly match today.
**Protective travel features** — Price drop protection, best-price guarantee, and cancel-for-any-reason (3-hour window) add tangible peace-of-mind Scapia lacks.
**Airline & hotel partners** — 1:1 redemption to Air India, Singapore Airlines, Marriott, IHG, and Accor gives aspirational premium redemption optionality [Inferred · High].

### What to do
**Counter the 12X hook** — Highlight Scapia's uncapped, fee-free travel benefits vs HSBC's capped (18K bonus points/month) and gated accelerated earn.
**Attack the fee friction** — Call out ₹300–₹700 convenience fees explicitly in acquisition messaging targeting HSBC cardholders considering a travel card switch.
**Monitor Hopper rollout** — Track whether HSBC extends Marketplace access beyond five card tiers; broader access would raise competitive intensity materially [Inferred · Medium].

## Coverage

### HSBC Launches Hopper Travel Portal
HSBC has launched its Rewards Marketplace in partnership with travel platform Hopper, offering select cardholders up to 12X reward points on hotel bookings and 6X on flights [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]. The portal supports 1:1 point redemption with major airlines (Air India, Singapore Airlines, British Airways) and hotel chains (Marriott, IHG, Accor), with protective features including price drop refunds and cancel-for-any-reason — positioning it as HSBC's answer to HDFC SmartBuy and ICICI iShop.

However, the platform's reach is limited to five card tiers and redemption is restricted to travel bookings only — no vouchers or shopping — leaving it trailing SmartBuy's ₹56,668 vs its ₹39,900 total value-back on an equal ₹1.8L spend comparison [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]. For Scapia, the key attack vectors remain: zero convenience fees, 0% forex, and no earn caps — all areas where HSBC Rewards Marketplace introduces friction that Scapia can credibly exploit in positioning.
