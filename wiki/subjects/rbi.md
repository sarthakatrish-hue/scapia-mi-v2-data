## What we know

**LRS governs all forex purchases** — Forex transactions including foreign currency and forex card purchases fall under RBI's Liberalised Remittance Scheme and attract TCS per purpose and threshold.[^rbi-forex-tcs-2026-05-02]
**20% TCS above ₹10L threshold** — General-purpose remittances above ₹10 lakh in a financial year attract 20% TCS; education and medical remittances attract only 2% above threshold.[^rbi-forex-tcs-2026-05-02]
**Overseas tour packages taxed from rupee one** — Unlike other LRS purposes, overseas tour packages attract 2% TCS on the full amount, not just above ₹10 lakh.[^rbi-forex-tcs-2026-05-02]
**TCS is reclaimable via ITR** — TCS paid on forex is adjustable against income tax liability; excess is refunded, making it a compliance tool rather than a net tax burden.[^rbi-forex-tcs-2026-05-02]
**GST on forex transactions is tiered** — GST applies to the transaction value at 0.18% (up to ₹1L), tapering to 0.018% above ₹10L, capped at ₹10,800.[^rbi-forex-tcs-2026-05-02]
**Budget 2026 updated TCS rates** — New TCS rates proposed in the 2026 Union Budget are effective from April 1, 2026.[^rbi-forex-tcs-2026-05-02]

## Regulatory Intel

**Status:** Active — circular issued June 2026, effective immediately for all regulated entities offering UPI-linked credit lines.

**What it requires:** Banks and fintechs offering pre-sanctioned credit lines via UPI must apply the same prudential treatment (capital, provisioning, classification) as required for the underlying credit facility. Only credit products already permitted under existing regulations can be channelled through UPI; no new lending categories may be created via payment-mode routing.[^rbi-upi-credit-2026-06-23]

**Posture:** Clarificatory / tightening — closes a potential regulatory arbitrage gap where lenders might have applied lighter norms to UPI-delivered credit relative to equivalent direct loan products.

**Implication:** For Scapia and Federal Bank, any future UPI-linked credit or pay-later feature must be structured so its prudential treatment mirrors the equivalent credit card or loan product — there is no lighter-touch regulatory path via the UPI channel. [Inferred · High] This raises the compliance cost for new UPI credit-line product design but levels the playing field, preventing nimbler competitors from gaining an unfair regulatory edge by routing credit through UPI.

## Coverage

### LRS Tax Rules for Forex Cards — 2026-05-02

India's LRS framework — administered by RBI — subjects all foreign currency and forex card purchases to TCS and GST.[^rbi-forex-tcs-2026-05-02] For most travellers spending under ₹10 lakh annually, TCS does not apply (except on tour packages), and GST is minimal and tiered. The 2026 Budget updated TCS rates effective April 1, 2026, keeping the framework live for travel-card issuers and fintechs to monitor.

For Scapia, this is directly relevant: travel cardholders who use a forex card or make large LRS remittances face TCS friction that a zero-forex-markup credit card avoids entirely. [Inferred · High] The tour-package carve-out — where 2% TCS applies from the first rupee — reinforces Scapia's positioning of direct hotel/flight bookings over bundled tour packages as the tax-efficient path for international travel spending.[^rbi-forex-tcs-2026-05-02]

### RBI Equalises UPI Credit Norms — 2026-06-23

The RBI issued a clarification requiring that pre-sanctioned credit lines delivered through UPI carry the same prudential norms — capital, provisioning, and classification — as the underlying credit product, irrespective of channel.[^rbi-upi-credit-2026-06-23] The move closes a potential regulatory arbitrage loophole that lenders could have exploited by routing credit through a payment mechanism to benefit from lighter treatment.

Critically, the RBI also barred banks from inventing new lending categories via UPI routing — only already-permitted credit products qualify.[^rbi-upi-credit-2026-06-23] For Scapia and Federal Bank, this means any UPI-linked credit or buy-now-pay-later overlay must be designed to full credit-card or loan prudential standards from the outset — a compliance reality check for product roadmaps. [Inferred · Medium]
