## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Travel-occasion depth** — Scapia's rewards are purpose-built for flights, stays, and forex — Amazon Now targets daily essentials and home goods, not the high-value travel booking moment.
**Zero forex markup** — A structural card benefit Amazon cannot replicate through a delivery app; relevant for every international trip a Scapia cardholder takes.

### Where they're ahead
**Prime loyalty flywheel** — Triple shopping frequency among Prime members creates a compounding engagement loop that dwarfs episodic travel card usage; Amazon's existing 100M+ Prime base in India is a massive head-start[^amzflip-2026-07-02]
**Capital and scale** — Amazon's willingness to waive fees, offer 25% cashback, and build 100 new fulfilment centres signals a war-chest spend no fintech can match[^amzflip-2026-07-02]
**Category creep into travel goods** — Luggage, travel accessories, and electronics in quick-commerce scope means Amazon Now could capture pre-trip spend occasions Scapia currently owns at checkout[^amzflip-2026-07-02]

### What to do
**Own the booking moment, not the product moment** — Amazon wins on physical travel goods delivery; Scapia should sharpen messaging around the financial transaction of travel — flights, hotels, forex — where Amazon Pay is not dominant.
**Watch Amazon Pay co-brand expansion** — If Amazon deepens its credit card offerings (currently with ICICI) to reward Now usage, the competitive surface widens into Scapia's core card territory. [Inferred · High]

## Coverage

### Amazon Eyes Largest Delivery-in-Minutes Network — 2026-07-02

Amazon CEO Andy Jassy, during his India visit, declared Amazon Now's ambition to become India's "largest delivery-in-minutes network," targeting 300+ cities with aggressive onboarding incentives — up to 25% cashback on first five orders and zero platform fees[^amzflip-2026-07-02]. Jassy noted Prime members triple their shopping frequency once they adopt Amazon Now, with orders doubling every quarter, making it Amazon's fastest-growing e-commerce unit in India[^amzflip-2026-07-02]. The company is also building 100 urban fulfilment centres stocking luggage and travel accessories, edging toward pre-trip spend occasions.

The market context is stark: quick commerce is projected to hit $65–70 Bn by 2030, accounting for up to 40% of India's online retail sales[^amzflip-2026-07-02]. Amazon's deep-pocket entry — alongside Flipkart Minutes' 1,000+ micro-fulfilment centres — raises competitive pressure on Blinkit and Zepto, and signals that the daily-spend battleground is intensifying. For Scapia, the near-term read is indirect, but Amazon's Prime loyalty machine and potential credit product expansion remain a forward watch. [Inferred · High]
