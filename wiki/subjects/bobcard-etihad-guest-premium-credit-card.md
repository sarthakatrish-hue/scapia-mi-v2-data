## What we know

**Airline Co-brand Launch** — BOBCARD entered the airline co-brand space with two Etihad Guest cards; the Premium variant is a metal card targeting frequent international travellers[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Gold Status on One Transaction** — Cardholders get Etihad Guest Silver on first swipe, then Gold after a single purchase on etihad.com within 6 months — bypassing the normal 50,000 Tier Miles requirement[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Zero Forex Markup** — 0% foreign currency markup on all international transactions, stacking with rewards[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Generous Lounge Access, No Spend Gate** — 12 domestic (3/quarter) and 8 international (2/quarter) lounge visits with no spend-based eligibility conditions[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Fee Waiver at ₹5L** — Annual fee waived at ₹5 lakh annual spend — notable since most airline co-brands don't offer a waiver[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Etihad–SBI Card Partnership Ended** — Etihad terminated its five-year SBI Card tie-up (SBI Etihad Guest Premier Card) and migrated exclusively to BOBCARD via BOB Financial Solutions[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Long Exclusion List** — Rewards are blocked on education, hospital, telecom, supermarkets, fast food, and more — unusually broad for a premium card[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Miles Value Caveat** — Etihad Guest miles carry low typical redemption value (~50 paise/mile in most cases), limiting real-world return for non-optimisers[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Milestone Upside at Scale** — At ₹12L annual eligible spend, the card can yield ~70,000 miles (~5.8% effective return if miles valued at ₹1)[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Gold Status Scope Limited** — Etihad is not in any airline alliance; Gold benefits apply to Etihad flights only, unlike Oneworld/Star Alliance status cards[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**No joining fee barrier** — Scapia charges no joining or annual fee; BOBCARD Etihad Premium costs ₹5,000+GST upfront, even with a waiver pathway at ₹5L spend[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Programme-agnostic rewards** — Scapia's rewards are not tied to a single airline ecosystem; BOBCARD's value is almost entirely contingent on flying Etihad or optimising its partner redemptions[Inferred · High]
**No exclusion list friction** — Scapia does not block everyday categories like groceries, hospitals, or telecom; BOBCARD Etihad's unusually long exclusion list materially reduces earn on daily spends[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Alliance breadth** — Etihad has no alliance membership; Scapia's travel rewards are redeemable broadly, not restricted to one carrier's network[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]

### Where they're ahead
**Gold status hook** — Complimentary Etihad Guest Gold status via one online transaction is a powerful acquisition lever for Etihad loyalists — a benefit Scapia has no equivalent for[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Metal card premium signal** — The physical metal card targets aspirational premium travellers; Scapia does not currently offer a metal variant[Inferred · Medium]
**Lounge access volume** — 12 domestic + 8 international visits with no spend gate is a strong lounge proposition that matches or exceeds Scapia's current offering[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Airline co-brand depth** — Business class lounge access on Etihad flights and chauffeur service for First/Business bookings are aspirational benefits with no Scapia parallel[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Exclusive OTA partnership** — Etihad.com earns 6 miles/₹100 (3× base); Scapia has no comparable accelerated earn on a specific airline's booking platform[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]

### What to do
**Target displaced SBI Etihad holders** — Etihad ended its SBI Card partnership; those orphaned cardholders are travel-aware and in the market — Scapia should message the simplicity and zero-fee advantage directly[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Quantify the exclusion list cost** — Model how BOBCARD Etihad's exclusions on groceries, telecom, and hospitals reduce real earn rates for typical Indian spenders vs Scapia's broader earn coverage[Inferred · High]
**Counter the miles-value illusion** — At 50 paise/mile real-world value, BOBCARD's 5.8% headline rate is actually ~2.9%; Scapia should make transparent INR-equivalent comparisons prominent in competitive content[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]
**Own the "alliance-free travel" message** — Scapia's rewards work across any travel platform; BOBCARD Etihad's value collapses if the cardholder doesn't fly Etihad — a segmentation Scapia can exploit for broader travel spenders[Inferred · Medium]

## Coverage

### BOBCARD Launches Etihad Premium Co-brand
BOBCARD, via BOB Financial Solutions, launched the Etihad Guest Premium Credit Card as its debut airline co-brand, directly absorbing the Etihad partnership that SBI Card held for five years before its discontinuation[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]. The metal card offers 0% forex markup, complimentary Etihad Guest Gold status achievable with a single etihad.com transaction, and 12 domestic + 8 international lounge visits per year with no spend gate — at a ₹5,000+GST fee waivable at ₹5L spend. CardExpert rates it 4.5/5, calling it "a solid airline co-brand card" for foreign travellers[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30].

For Scapia, the card is a material competitive development on two fronts: it matches Scapia's zero forex core proposition while adding an aspirational status layer Scapia currently lacks, and it directly captures the orphaned SBI Etihad cardholders — a travel-focused, points-literate cohort that is now actively shopping[^bobcard-etihad-premium-2026-06-30]. The critical caveat is that Etihad miles carry low redemption value (~50 paise/mile typical), the exclusion list is unusually long for a premium card, and the programme's benefits are locked to a non-alliance carrier — all weaknesses Scapia's broader, fee-free proposition can exploit [Inferred · High].
