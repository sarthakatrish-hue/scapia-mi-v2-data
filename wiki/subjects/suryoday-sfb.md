## What we know

**CLOU launched August 2025** — Suryoday SFB debuted Credit Line on UPI in partnership with Paytm, targeting digital-first prime borrowers[^suryo-2026-06-27]
**Prime-heavy portfolio** — 90% of sanctioned customers have CIBIL 725+; average drawdown ~₹7,000, utilisation 28% of sanctioned limits[^suryo-2026-06-27]
**Rapid NTB acquisition** — 97% of CLOU customers are new-to-bank, making the product a customer-acquisition vehicle at scale[^suryo-2026-06-27]
**Geographic footprint** — Delhi-NCR >20%, Jaipur ~14% of portfolio; North and West India dominate; Bengaluru leads South India[^suryo-2026-06-27]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Travel rewards depth** — Scapia offers flight and hotel rewards CLOU structurally cannot replicate, retaining travel-occasion spend.
**Card acceptance universality** — Visa/Rupay card rails work globally; CLOU is UPI-merchant-dependent and limited to domestic ecosystems.
**Premium positioning** — Scapia targets aspirational travellers; Suryoday's SFB brand carries less premium cachet in that segment.

### Where they're ahead
**Frictionless onboarding** — App-native CLOU with Paytm distribution removes annual-fee friction that travel cards carry.
**NTB acquisition scale** — 97% new-to-bank customers shows Suryoday can acquire prime users faster via embedded credit than card issuers can via branch/digital funnels[^suryo-2026-06-27]
**No switching cost** — CLOU sits inside existing Paytm wallets; zero incremental app or card needed from the user.

### What to do
**Monitor overlap metros** — Track CLOU penetration in Delhi-NCR, Mumbai, Bengaluru — Scapia's core growth geographies — for spend displacement signals.
**Reinforce travel reward differentiation** — Ensure reward earn-rates and redemption experiences on travel are clearly communicated where CLOU convenience narrative is loudest.

## Coverage

### Suryoday SFB Scales CLOU — 2026-06-23

Suryoday Small Finance Bank published a white paper revealing that its Credit Line on UPI product — launched in August 2025 with Paytm — has sanctioned credit lines to 530,000 customers with total limits exceeding ₹362 crore[^suryo-2026-06-27]. Crucially, 90% of users are CIBIL 725+, and 97% are new-to-bank, establishing CLOU as both a prime-credit product and an aggressive customer-acquisition engine for a small finance bank punching above its distribution weight.

The competitive read for Scapia: Suryoday-Paytm's CLOU is not targeting sub-prime users but the same digitally active, creditworthy urban cohort that travel cards pursue[^suryo-2026-06-27]. The geographic overlap — Delhi-NCR, Mumbai, Bengaluru — is direct. Suryoday's path to scale runs through Paytm's 300M+ user base, giving it reach that its SFB branch network alone never could. Scapia's defensive moat is travel-reward depth and the aspirational card identity CLOU cannot credibly claim. [Inferred · High]
