## What we know

**No-fee metal card** — Lifetime free metal card, strong in-app controls and instant insights [^oc1]
**Low forex but not zero** — 1% markup — better than 3.5% average but not zero like Scapia [^oc1]
**Category rewards** — 5X rewards on top 2 spend categories (rotating) [^oc1]
**FY25 revenue near ₹2,000 cr** — Operating revenue grew 32% YoY to ₹1,878 cr in FY25 from ₹1,425.5 cr in FY24 [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]
**Losses shrinking but still deep** — Net loss reduced 26% to ₹297.5 cr in FY25 from ₹401 cr in FY24; EBITDA margin at -15.71% [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]
**Ad spend cut nearly 40%** — Advertising and promotional spend dropped from ₹488 cr to ₹294 cr, signalling a shift from acquisition blitz to efficiency [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]
**Unit economics improving** — Spent ₹1.17 to earn ₹1 of revenue in FY25, vs ₹1.31 in FY24 [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]
**Three-bank co-brand model** — Partners with IDFC First Bank, Federal Bank, and SBM Bank for card issuance; also runs OneScore for credit tracking [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]
**RBI scrutiny on data sharing** — RBI reportedly asked partner banks to halt new co-branded card issuances, seeking clarity on how data is shared between OneCard and its banking partners [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]
**Debt fundraise in progress** — Raising ~₹40 cr in debt from existing investor Alteria Capital; total equity raised over $270 million to date [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Zero forex markup** — Scapia's 0% forex beats OneCard's 1% on every international transaction, a compounding edge for frequent travellers.
**Travel-first positioning** — Scapia is built around travel rewards and lounge access; OneCard's "top 2 categories" model is generic, not travel-native.
**Single issuer simplicity** — Scapia's focused Federal Bank partnership vs OneCard's three-bank spread means cleaner product iteration and less regulatory surface area [Inferred · Medium].

### Where they're ahead
**Revenue scale and longevity** — At ₹1,878 cr operating revenue, OneCard's financial base dwarfs Scapia's current scale, giving it more buffer to weather regulatory headwinds [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22].
**Credit score ecosystem** — OneScore creates a top-of-funnel acquisition and retention loop that Scapia has no equivalent to — a structural stickiness advantage [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22].
**Multi-bank resilience** — Three issuing partners (IDFC First, Federal Bank, SBM) means OneCard can route around a single bank freeze; Scapia is entirely dependent on Federal Bank [Inferred · High].

### What to do
**Exploit RBI overhang** — OneCard's data-sharing scrutiny from RBI is the same regulatory pressure hitting Federal Bank; position Scapia's compliance posture as cleaner and more consumer-aligned where possible.
**Monitor Federal Bank overlap** — Federal Bank is a shared partner; any RBI action that expands from OneCard's programme to Federal Bank's entire co-brand portfolio directly impacts Scapia acquisition [Inferred · High].
**Attack the ad-spend gap** — OneCard slashed marketing 40% to chase efficiency [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]; this creates a window for Scapia to take share in travel-intent audiences where OneCard is pulling back.

## Coverage

### OneCard FY25 Financials, RBI Probe
OneCard posted operating revenue of ₹1,878 cr in FY25, a 32% jump from ₹1,425.5 cr in FY24, approaching the ₹2,000 cr mark [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]. Net losses fell 26% to ₹297.5 cr as the company cut advertising spend by nearly 40% to ₹294 cr and improved its cost-per-rupee-earned ratio from ₹1.31 to ₹1.17 [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]. The company raised over $270 million to date and is adding ~₹40 cr in debt from Alteria Capital [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22].

The more strategically significant signal for Scapia is the RBI scrutiny [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]. The regulator reportedly asked OneCard's partner banks — which include Federal Bank, Scapia's sole issuing partner — to pause new co-branded card issuances pending clarity on data-sharing practices. This is the same regulatory theme already documented in the Federal Bank freeze, and it confirms the pressure is industry-wide, not bank-specific [Inferred · High].

For Scapia, the dual read is: OneCard is a well-funded, revenue-scaling competitor with a credit-score ecosystem moat, but it faces the same regulatory headwinds and is visibly pulling back on acquisition spend [^oc-fy25-2026-06-22]. The RBI data-sharing concern is a shared threat, but OneCard's multi-bank structure gives it more issuing optionality than Scapia currently holds [Inferred · High].
