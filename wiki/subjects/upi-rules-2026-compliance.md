## What we know

**Travel payments get higher cap** — UPI transactions for travel qualify for the ₹5,00,000 exception limit, up from the standard ₹1,00,000 daily cap[^upi2026-2026-06-25]
**New UPI setup restricted** — Fresh UPI registrations are capped at ₹5,000 in the first 24 hours, a friction point for new-to-UPI users[^upi2026-2026-06-25]
**PF withdrawal via UPI expected** — Salaried users may be able to withdraw PF directly through UPI from March 2026, subject to operationalisation[^upi2026-2026-06-25]
**GST QR thresholds formalised** — Business QR/UPI receipts crossing ₹40L (goods) or ₹20L (services) annually trigger mandatory GST registration; non-compliance risks penalties[^upi2026-2026-06-25]
**Cashback must be declared** — UPI app cashback is taxable income and must be reported in ITR, increasing compliance burden for rewards-heavy users[^upi2026-2026-06-25]
**Gift UPI capped at ₹500** — Non-relative, non-wedding UPI gift receipts above ₹500 per person are treated as taxable income[^upi2026-2026-06-25]

## Market Intel

**Direction:** Neutral

**What's shifting:** UPI's regulatory framework is tightening around compliance, traceability, and tax linkage — higher limits are carved out for specific categories (including travel), but the overall environment demands greater financial discipline from users and merchants alike[^upi2026-2026-06-25]

**Why it matters:** The ₹5,00,000 exception for travel payments is a direct tailwind for Scapia's core use-case — large travel bookings can flow via UPI without hitting the standard cap. However, the GST QR threshold rules and cashback-as-income treatment add friction for small merchants and reward-seeking users, and the 24-hour new-user cap is onboarding friction[^upi2026-2026-06-25] [Inferred · High]

**Implication:** Scapia should (a) surface the travel-category UPI exception in user education to reduce checkout drop-off on high-value bookings; (b) flag cashback tax-reporting obligations in its rewards communications to stay ahead of user complaints; (c) monitor whether the new-UPI registration ₹5,000 cap creates friction for fresh card + UPI onboarding flows [Inferred · Medium]

## Coverage

### UPI 2026 Rules — Key Limits — 2026-06-25

A TaxGuru explainer outlines the UPI compliance framework taking shape from 2026: the standard daily cap remains ₹1,00,000, but travel, medical, education, and a handful of other categories qualify for a ₹5,00,000 exception[^upi2026-2026-06-25]. New UPI registrations face a ₹5,000 ceiling in the first 24 hours, and PF withdrawal via UPI is expected to go live in March 2026 for salaried users[^upi2026-2026-06-25].

The article's compliance angle is significant for Scapia: the travel-payments carve-out in the higher UPI limit directly benefits users booking flights or hotels via UPI-linked flows[^upi2026-2026-06-25]. At the same time, cashback from UPI apps must now be declared as income in ITR — a detail Scapia's rewards communications will need to address proactively to avoid user confusion[^upi2026-2026-06-25] [Inferred · Medium].
