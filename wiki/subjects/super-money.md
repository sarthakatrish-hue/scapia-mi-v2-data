## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Travel-native rewards depth** — Scapia coins are purpose-built for flights, stays, and travel categories; SuperCoins are a generic retail currency now stretched into mobility [Inferred · High].
**No ecosystem lock-in** — Scapia rewards work across open travel inventory; SuperCoins require spending within Flipkart's walled garden (Cleartrip, Minutes) to redeem meaningfully [Inferred · Medium].

### Where they're ahead
**High-frequency earn surface** — Embedding SuperCoins into Uber rides gives Flipkart a daily-touchpoint earn loop that a travel credit card, used less frequently, cannot replicate [^flipkart-uber-2026-06-29].
**Cleartrip travel redemption angle** — SuperCoins redeemable on Cleartrip means the loyalty loop now closes inside a travel OTA, blurring the line between retail loyalty and travel rewards [^flipkart-uber-2026-06-29].
**Cross-platform coalition** — Linking Flipkart + Uber + Cleartrip + Minutes into one coin creates a multi-vertical coalition rewards programme Scapia currently has no equivalent to [^flipkart-uber-2026-06-29].

### What to do
**Audit Cleartrip redemption rates** — If SuperCoins offer competitive value on flights/hotels via Cleartrip, Scapia's travel-rewards moat narrows; benchmark coin-to-rupee value quarterly.
**Accelerate earn-rate storytelling** — Counter the high-frequency Uber earn narrative by surfacing Scapia's superior per-rupee travel-spend rewards in acquisition messaging.

## Coverage

### SuperCoins Rides Into Mobility — 2026-06-29

Flipkart and Uber have partnered to let users earn SuperCoins on eligible rides — 4% of fares up to 150 coins per trip, with no overall cap — by linking their accounts [^flipkart-uber-2026-06-29]. Coins are credited to Flipkart accounts and redeemable across its ecosystem, including Cleartrip and Flipkart Minutes, with bonus coins offered to re-engage lapsed Uber users [^flipkart-uber-2026-06-29]. Flipkart VP Gaurav Arora framed this as SuperCoins "evolving into a broader rewards layer across everyday use cases," with mobility as a natural next category [^flipkart-uber-2026-06-29].

For Scapia, the strategic read is a sharpening threat: SuperCoins can now be earned daily (Uber rides) and redeemed on travel (Cleartrip), replicating the earn-on-spend → redeem-on-travel loop that is Scapia's core value proposition — but anchored to a far larger daily-use distribution base [Inferred · High]. The moat that remains is Scapia's travel-native earn rates and open-inventory redemption; if Cleartrip's SuperCoin redemption value approaches Scapia's coin rates, competitive intensity escalates materially [Inferred · Medium].

### Super.money Bets on Secured Cards — 2025-10-29

Flipkart's fintech arm Super.money announced a partnership with Kotak Mahindra Bank's digital unit Kotak811 to launch a "3-in-1 Super Account" combining a savings account, UPI payments, and a fixed-deposit-backed secured credit card [^sm-kotak-2025-10-29]. The deal targets issuance of ~2 million cards in 12 months — 60% to first-time borrowers — scaling to 5 million within two years [^sm-kotak-2025-10-29]. The minimum FD to open is ₹1,000, with no income proof required for the embedded credit line [^sm-kotak-2025-10-29].

The move crystallises Super.money's core thesis: UPI is an acquisition engine, not a profit centre, and the secured card is how it converts free-payment users into monetisable credit customers [^sm-kotak-2025-10-29]. With $36M annualized revenue, 85% user retention, and 60–70% of transactions from under-30 users [^sm-kotak-2025-10-29], Super.money is scaling into exactly the demographic Scapia serves — and Kotak's regulated infrastructure gives it an issuing partner currently unconstrained by the RBI freeze affecting Federal Bank [Inferred · High].

A simultaneous Juspay one-click checkout partnership for ~1,000 D2C merchants signals Super.money's ambition to build a commerce spending layer on top of its payments and credit stack [^sm-kotak-2025-10-29]. For Scapia, the near-term risk is manageable given Super.money's generalist cashback (vs. Scapia's travel-native rewards), but the medium-term risk rises sharply if Flipkart's retail flywheel accelerates card spend and Super.money introduces category-specific travel rewards [Inferred · Medium].
