## Market Intel

**Direction:** Headwind
**What's shifting:** India's dominant airport lounge aggregator has lost key venue partnerships, fragmenting the lounge-access layer that underpins a major credit and debit card perk for millions of cardholders. Banks with DreamFolks dependency now face an access gap of uncertain duration, while alternatives (Elite Assist, Priority Pass, in-house) are unevenly available across issuers.

A sustained disruption erodes cardholder trust in the perk itself, not just in individual banks — making the entire "lounge benefit" category less credible as a product differentiator. Banks slow to announce alternatives risk cardholder complaints and churn during peak festive travel.

## Coverage

### DreamFolks Lounge Suspension Ripples — 2026-06-18

DreamFolks Services, India's largest airport lounge aggregator, suspended its domestic lounge business after three major venue operators — Adani Digital, Semolina Kitchens, and Encalm Hospitality — terminated their partnerships[^df-lounge-2026-06-18]. The fallout is uneven: IDFC First Bank has already migrated debit card holders to Elite Assist, maintaining access at 45+ lounges, while IndusInd Bank, Kotak Mahindra Bank, and YES Bank remain on DreamFolks with no replacement announced. ICICI Bank and HDFC Bank had begun reducing DreamFolks reliance earlier in 2025; SBI Card was never exposed.

The disruption lands during peak festive travel months, when lounge access complaints carry maximum reputational weight[^df-lounge-2026-06-18]. For Scapia, the episode highlights a structural risk in the lounge aggregator model that its own RuPay-native access route may avoid — a differentiation angle worth activating quickly in cardholder-facing messaging targeting users of the three banks still without an announced alternative. [Inferred · High]
