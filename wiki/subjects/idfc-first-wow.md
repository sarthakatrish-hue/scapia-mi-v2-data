## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**No capital lock-in** — Scapia requires no fixed deposit; WOW! Black's ₹20,000 FD ties up cash that earns less than a savings account for most users [Inferred · High].
**Unlimited lounge access** — Scapia's unlimited lounge vs WOW! Black's 2 domestic visits per quarter is a decisive edge for any traveller who transits airports regularly[^wow-cardtrail-2026-06-28].
**Uncapped credit line** — Scapia issues a credit line based on creditworthiness; WOW! Black's credit limit equals its FD amount, making it impractical for large travel spends[^wow-cardtrail-2026-06-28].

### Where they're ahead
**MakeMyTrip partnership hook** — WOW! Black bundles MakeMyTrip discounts, creating a recognisable travel-booking benefit that resonates with first-time travellers[^wow-cardtrail-2026-06-28].
**No income or CIBIL barrier** — FD-backed issuance means students and gig workers with no credit history can access zero-forex benefits that Scapia's income-based underwriting may exclude[^wow-cardtrail-2026-06-28].
**Credit-building narrative** — Positioned explicitly as a tool to build credit history while travelling; Scapia does not own this entry-level onramp segment[^wow-cardtrail-2026-06-28].

### What to do
**Exploit the FD ceiling** — Highlight Scapia's uncapped credit line in creative targeting anyone researching secured cards; the "can't pay a ₹50,000 hotel bill" limitation is a real pain point to amplify[^wow-cardtrail-2026-06-28].
**Watch MMT partnership** — MakeMyTrip's inclusion as a WOW! benefit means IDFC is building a co-brand-adjacent relationship with a major OTA; monitor whether this deepens into a full MMT-IDFC co-branded product that competes more directly[Inferred · Medium].
**Ignore credit-builder segment** — WOW! Black's student/no-CIBIL segment is structurally outside Scapia's addressable market given Federal Bank's underwriting requirements; no action needed beyond awareness[Inferred · High].

## Coverage

### WOW! Black in 2026 Forex Guide — 2026-06-28

Cardtrail's 2026 zero-forex ranking covers the IDFC FIRST WOW! Black, confirming its current name and fleshing out benefits not previously on record: 2 domestic lounge visits per quarter, MakeMyTrip discounts, and accelerated reward points[^wow-cardtrail-2026-06-28]. The guide positions it as the "zero markup on training wheels" card — a smart entry point for students and career-starters who want to build credit history while also avoiding forex fees[^wow-cardtrail-2026-06-28].

The structural ceiling is clear: credit limit equals the ₹20,000 FD, making large travel spends impossible on this card alone[^wow-cardtrail-2026-06-28]. Cardtrail verified the card carries no hidden cross-currency conversion fees, placing it alongside Niyo and IDFC FIRST Select in the genuinely-zero tier[^wow-cardtrail-2026-06-28]. For Scapia, WOW! Black occupies a distinct, lower segment — its MakeMyTrip tie-up is worth monitoring for signs of a deeper OTA partnership that could eventually compete in Scapia's core market[Inferred · Medium].
