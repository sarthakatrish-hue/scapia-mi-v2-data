## What we know

**22% market share, third place** — Zepto holds 22% of India's quick-commerce market per Datum Intelligence data cited by Reuters, January 2026[^qcwar-2026-06-07]
**Revenue crossed Rs 11,100 Cr in FY25** — Up ~150% YoY per MCA filings cited by industry trackers; fastest revenue growth among pure-play q-commerce startups[^qcwar-2026-06-07]
**~1,200 dark stores, 600K-700K daily orders** — Physical scale broadly matches Swiggy Instamart; store count places it second in the sector by locations[^qcwar-2026-06-07]
**IPO deferred to 2026** — DRHP filing pushed from 2025 to late 2026; CEO Aadit Palicha says company is close to EBITDA breakeven[^qcwar-2026-06-07]
**Category expansion strategy** — Differentiates via Zepto Cafe and 10-minute pharmacy, moving beyond grocery to defend against e-commerce giants[^qcwar-2026-06-07]
**Backed by YC Continuity, StepStone, Glade Brook** — Founded 2021 in Mumbai by Aadit Palicha and Kaivalya Vohra; privately held pending IPO[^qcwar-2026-06-07]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Travel-occasion exclusivity** — Zepto is a grocery and essentials play; Scapia's rewards are designed for travel spend, a category Zepto does not serve — distinct wallet targeting.
**No subscription dependency** — Zepto has no membership moat comparable to Amazon Prime; Scapia's benefits are open to cardholders without a gating subscription.

### Where they're ahead
**Daily frequency habit** — 600K-700K daily orders embeds Zepto as a daily-use app; Scapia's card is a travel-occasion card with far lower touch frequency, making loyalty compounding harder[^qcwar-2026-06-07]
**Profitability race pressure** — Zepto's IPO preparation forces unit-economics discipline; a leaner, profitable Zepto post-IPO could leverage its user base for financial products including co-branded cards[^qcwar-2026-06-07]

### What to do
**Monitor post-IPO financial product moves** — A listed Zepto with fintech ambitions (co-brand card, BNPL, UPI) would directly compete for daily-spend wallet share. [Inferred · Medium]
**Travel rewards as the antidote** — Zepto wins on essentials frequency; Scapia should sharpen messaging that travel rewards compound on high-value, low-frequency moments that grocery spend never unlocks.

## Coverage

### Zepto's IPO Squeeze — 2026-06-07

Zepto sits at 22% of India's $11.5 Bn quick-commerce market with ~1,200 dark stores and revenue of Rs 11,100 Cr in FY25 — up ~150% YoY — but faces a mounting profitability squeeze as Amazon Now and Flipkart Minutes enter the top tier with deep-pocketed parents[^qcwar-2026-06-07]. CEO Aadit Palicha deferred the DRHP filing from 2025 to late 2026, citing proximity to EBITDA breakeven, while the company leans on Zepto Cafe and 10-minute pharmacy to differentiate beyond commodity grocery delivery[^qcwar-2026-06-07].

For Scapia, Zepto today is an indirect competitor — daily essentials versus travel occasions. The forward watch is a post-IPO Zepto with capital, scale, and potential fintech ambitions; a co-branded card or BNPL layer on top of 600K+ daily orders would shift that assessment quickly.
