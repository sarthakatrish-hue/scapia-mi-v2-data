## What we know

**2,200+ dark stores as of March 2026** — Blinkit has expanded its dark-store count to 2,200+ serving 200+ cities, up from 1,500+ cited in earlier coverage[^amzflip-2026-07-02]
**Only profitable q-commerce player** — Reported adjusted EBITDA of Rs 370M in Q4 FY25 (March quarter) and Rs 40M the prior quarter — the sole operator at operating profit[^amzflip-2026-07-02]
**Amazon targets more cities than Blinkit** — Amazon Now's 300+ city plan would exceed Blinkit's 200+ city footprint, the first time a challenger has announced a broader geographic ambition[^amzflip-2026-07-02]
**Runaway market leader** — Blinkit holds 46% of India's quick-commerce market per Datum Intelligence data cited by Reuters in January 2026[^qcwar-2026-06-07]
**Sector consolidation to 2-3 players** — CLSA India analyst Aditya Soman cited Blinkit as a certain survivor; the remaining 1-2 slots are contested between Amazon, Flipkart, Zepto, and Swiggy Instamart[^amzflip-2026-07-02]
**Owned by Eternal (formerly Zomato)** — Acquired by Zomato in 2022 for $568M; parent rebranded to Eternal Limited; led by CEO Albinder Dhindsa[^qcwar-2026-06-07]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Travel-first positioning** — Scapia is purpose-built for travel rewards and zero forex markup; Blinkit is a grocery and essentials play with no travel category overlap — different occasion, different user intent.
**No ecosystem lock-in** — Scapia's rewards accrue across travel categories; Blinkit's habit loop is confined to daily grocery spend, limiting its ability to capture travel-wallet share.

### Where they're ahead
**Profitability moat** — Blinkit is the only q-commerce player at operating profit, giving Eternal a defensible cost structure as Amazon and Flipkart flood the market with cash[^amzflip-2026-07-02]
**Daily-habit flywheel** — 2,200+ stores and ~1M daily orders creates a compounding engagement loop no travel card can match on frequency; Blinkit's checkout is a daily touchpoint, Scapia's is episodic[^qcwar-2026-06-07]
**Eternal cross-sell** — Parent Eternal (Zomato) can layer restaurant, event, and travel bookings onto the same app, broadening the ecosystem beyond grocery and narrowing Scapia's occasion exclusivity[^qcwar-2026-06-07]

### What to do
**Own the travel occasion sharply** — Blinkit wins on frequency; Scapia should win on value-per-transaction. Messaging should anchor on what a single travel booking is worth in rewards vs dozens of grocery runs.
**Watch Eternal's travel ambitions** — If Zomato/Eternal re-enters travel booking or co-brands a travel card, the competitive overlap becomes direct and urgent. [Inferred · Medium]

## Coverage

### Amazon Challenges Blinkit's City Lead — 2026-07-02

New data from this article updates Blinkit's dark-store count to 2,200+ across 200+ cities as of March 2026, a significant step up from earlier estimates[^amzflip-2026-07-02]. More critically, Blinkit is confirmed as the only quick-commerce operator at operating profit — Rs 370M adjusted EBITDA in the March quarter — a financial position that CLSA India describes as guaranteeing its spot among the two-to-three survivors expected after consolidation[^amzflip-2026-07-02].

The competitive picture has shifted: Amazon Now's stated target of 300+ cities would, for the first time, give a challenger a wider geographic ambition than Blinkit's current footprint[^amzflip-2026-07-02]. Blinkit's profitability advantage is real but its city-coverage lead is narrowing. For Scapia, the Eternal ecosystem watch remains the primary forward signal — grocery-to-travel cross-sell is the scenario that would make this overlap direct. [Inferred · Medium]

### Blinkit Leads Six-Way War — 2026-06-07

Blinkit has consolidated its leadership of India's quick-commerce sector, holding 46% market share in a $11.5 Bn market growing at 75%+ YoY, per Datum Intelligence data cited by Reuters[^qcwar-2026-06-07]. Its Q4 FY25 Gross Order Value of Rs 9,421 Cr — up 134% year-on-year — and a 1,500+ dark-store network delivering ~1 million orders daily place it well ahead of Swiggy Instamart (24%) and Zepto (22%). Parent Eternal (formerly Zomato) provides listed-company capital access that pure-play rivals cannot easily match[^qcwar-2026-06-07].

For Scapia, the overlap is currently indirect: Blinkit competes for daily spend occasions, not travel wallet. The strategic watch is Eternal's broader platform ambitions — if the Zomato/Eternal ecosystem extends meaningfully into travel bookings or co-branded travel credit products, the competitive read shifts from peripheral to direct.
