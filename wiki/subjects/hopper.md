## What we know

**HSBC India partnership live** — Hopper powers HSBC Rewards Marketplace for flights, hotel, and car rental bookings for select HSBC credit cardholders in India [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]
**B2B infrastructure role** — Hopper is operating as backend travel infrastructure (following similar ICICI iShop model), not as a consumer-facing brand in India [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]
**Distinctive protective features** — Price drop protection (up to ₹1,500 in 10 days), best-price guarantee (24-hour refund as travel credit), and cancel-for-any-reason (100% refund up to 3 hours before) are Hopper-native features now live in India via HSBC [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]
**Bank channel distribution strategy** — Partnering with HSBC follows a pattern of Hopper embedding within bank card ecosystems to reach premium cardholders [Inferred · High]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Direct consumer relationship** — Scapia owns the customer relationship end-to-end; Hopper is an invisible B2B layer behind HSBC's brand, limiting its loyalty surface.
**No convenience fees** — Hopper via HSBC charges ₹300–₹700/leg convenience fees; Scapia's booking experience carries no such friction.

### Where they're ahead
**Protective travel tech** — Price drop protection, CFAR, and best-price guarantee are differentiated features Scapia does not currently offer, adding perceived safety for high-value bookings.
**Bank distribution moat** — By embedding in HSBC (and reportedly ICICI), Hopper reaches premium cardholders at scale without direct customer acquisition cost [Inferred · High].

### What to do
**Watch for more bank tie-ups** — If Hopper partners with a larger Indian issuer (HDFC, Axis, SBI Card), its reach would expand dramatically and raise competitive stakes for Scapia's travel booking layer [Inferred · Medium].
**Evaluate protective features gap** — CFAR and price drop protection resonate with the premium travel segment Scapia targets; assess feasibility of analogous Scapia-native features.

## Coverage

### Hopper Powers HSBC India Portal
Hopper has gone live in India as the technology backbone of HSBC's newly launched Rewards Marketplace, enabling flight, hotel, and car rental bookings for select HSBC credit cardholders [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26]. The partnership brings Hopper's distinctive consumer-protection features — price drop refunds, cancel-for-any-reason, and best-price guarantee — into the Indian premium card ecosystem for the first time at scale.

The move confirms Hopper's India strategy as B2B-first: embedding within bank card programs (HSBC following earlier ICICI linkage signals) rather than competing directly as a consumer OTA [Inferred · High]. For Scapia, this is a signal to watch — a well-capitalised travel-tech platform quietly building distribution through the very card-issuer channel Scapia operates in, while layering in protective travel features that Scapia does not yet offer [^hsbc-rm-2026-06-26].
