## What we know

**Travel reward cap added** — 5 EDGE miles / ₹100 on travel now capped at ₹2L/month; spends above revert to 2 EDGE miles / ₹100 [^atlas-deval1]
**Exclusions list extended** — Gold/jewellery, government institutions, all insurance (previously >₹5K), fuel, utilities now excluded from rewards, milestone, and tier calculation — joining earlier exclusions [^atlas-deval1]
**Milestone miles halved** — Max milestone benefit cut from 17,500 to 10,000 EDGE miles; annual benefit cut from 10,000 to 5,000 EDGE miles (Silver tier loses annual benefit entirely) [^atlas-deval1]
**Transfer partners split into groups** — 20 partners divided into Group A (30,000 EDGE miles/year cap) and Group B (1,20,000 EDGE miles/year cap); total cap unchanged at 1,50,000; one loyalty ID per partner at a time [^atlas-deval1]
**Premium services discontinued** — Concierge service (Gold: 2, Platinum: 4 uses) and airport pick-up service (Platinum: 2 uses) both removed effective April 2024 [^atlas-deval1]
**Redemption ratio unchanged** — 1 EDGE Mile = 2 Partner Points across airlines and hotels; Marriott Bonvoy exception remains at 2 EDGE Miles = 1 Bonvoy point [^atlas-deval1]
**Group A carries international stalwarts** — Singapore Airlines (KrisFlyer), Qatar Airways, Turkish Airlines, JAL, United, Etihad, Air Canada among 12 Group A partners — but hard-capped at just 30,000 EDGE miles/year [^atlas-deval1]
**Heavy spender value loss quantified** — At ₹15L annual spend, combined milestone + annual benefit drops from ~₹27,500 equivalent to ~₹15,000 equivalent — a ₹12,500 or ~25,000 EDGE miles loss per year [^atlas-deval1]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Zero forex, zero fee** — Scapia's ₹0 annual fee and 0% forex markup contrast sharply with Atlas's ₹5,000 + GST fee and 3.5% markup — every international transaction compounds the gap.
**No reward caps** — Scapia imposes no monthly travel-spend cap on reward earning; Atlas now throttles accelerated earn above ₹2L/month travel spend [^atlas-deval1].
**No category exclusions drag** — Atlas's expanded exclusion list (fuel, utilities, all insurance, jewellery, government) reduces everyday earn; Scapia has no such accumulating carve-outs [^atlas-deval1].
**Simpler redemption** — Atlas's Group A / Group B split with distinct annual caps adds friction for frequent flyers loyal to Singapore Airlines or Qatar; Scapia's model carries no partner-group complexity [^atlas-deval1].

### Where they're ahead
**Established transfer ecosystem** — 20 airline and hotel partners including international heavyweights (KrisFlyer, MileagePlus, Qatar, Flying Blue) at a 1:2 ratio remains a meaningful advantage for points-optimisers [^atlas-deval1].
**Milestone bonus residual value** — Even post-devaluation, ₹15L spend still yields ₹15,000 equivalent in milestone miles, a standalone decent return for very high spenders [^atlas-deval1] [Inferred · High].
**Tiered lounge depth** — Priority Pass + DreamFolks tiered access (up to 12 intl / 18 dom at Platinum) continues to outgun most no-fee cards on raw lounge volume.

### What to do
**Exploit the cap story** — Market Scapia directly at Atlas cardholders who book heavy travel months (honeymoons, multi-leg trips) where Atlas's ₹2L/month cap bites hardest.
**Target the exclusions gap** — Atlas users now earning zero rewards on fuel, utilities, insurance, and jewellery represent an acquisition audience for Scapia's broader earn coverage.
**Flag devaluation credibility risk** — Atlas has now undergone two devaluation rounds (Sep 2023, Apr 2024) [^atlas-deval1]; use this pattern in CRM and paid messaging to reinforce Scapia's stability narrative [Inferred · Medium].
