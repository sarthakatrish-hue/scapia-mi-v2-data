## What we know

**First PSB partnership** — Kiwi partnered with Punjab National Bank to launch a co-branded RuPay credit card with UPI integration — its first public-sector bank tie-up [^pnb-kiwi-2026-06-22]
**Credit-on-UPI core** — The PNB Kiwi card allows users to make UPI payments directly from a credit line via QR codes, extending Kiwi's core proposition to PNB's 180 million customers [^pnb-kiwi-2026-06-22]
**Rural/semi-urban push** — PNB's 10,000+ branch network targets financial inclusion in semi-urban and rural areas where digital credit penetration is low [^pnb-kiwi-2026-06-22]
**Zero-fee, cashback model** — No joining or annual fee; cashback on both UPI and online transactions [^pnb-kiwi-2026-06-22]
**RuPay network dependency** — The card runs on RuPay, which supports credit-card-to-UPI linking — a key rails advantage over Visa/Mastercard for domestic QR-based credit [^pnb-kiwi-2026-06-22]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Travel-first rewards depth** — Scapia's unlimited travel rewards and zero forex are structurally richer than PNB Kiwi's flat cashback for any user with international or flight spend.
**Premium positioning** — Scapia targets frequent travellers; Kiwi's PNB co-brand is mass-market and financial-inclusion oriented — different audiences with limited direct overlap today [Inferred · Medium].
**Network reach abroad** — Scapia runs on Visa/RuPay with international acceptance; RuPay's QR-credit utility is primarily domestic, limiting Kiwi's travel relevance [Inferred · High].

### Where they're ahead
**UPI credit rails** — Kiwi's entire product is built around credit-on-UPI, a fast-growing payment mode where Scapia has no equivalent positioning [^pnb-kiwi-2026-06-22].
**Public-sector distribution** — PNB's 180 million customer base and 10,000+ branches give Kiwi a distribution moat in Tier 2/3 markets that Scapia's Federal Bank partnership does not match [^pnb-kiwi-2026-06-22].
**Financial-inclusion moat** — By targeting semi-urban and rural QR-credit users, Kiwi is building a customer cohort Scapia is not competing for — creating a large base that could trade up over time [Inferred · Medium].

### What to do
**Monitor UPI credit adoption curve** — If credit-on-UPI becomes a mainstream habit via PNB Kiwi's scale, the user expectation for any credit card to support UPI payments will rise — Scapia should track RuPay UPI credit link adoption rates [Inferred · High].
**Watch PSB partnership playbook** — Kiwi's move to PSBs after smaller bank pilots signals a deliberate scale-up strategy; if SBI or Canara follow, Kiwi becomes a mass-market credit infrastructure player, not just a fintech card [Inferred · Medium].

## Coverage

### PNB Kiwi RuPay-UPI Card Launch
Punjab National Bank partnered with fintech Kiwi to launch the PNB Kiwi Credit Card — a RuPay co-brand with native UPI credit integration, available to PNB's 180 million customers [^pnb-kiwi-2026-06-22]. The card carries no joining or annual fee, offers cashback on UPI and online spends, and is fully digitally onboarded [^pnb-kiwi-2026-06-22]. This is Kiwi's first public-sector bank partnership, a meaningful step up from prior private/small-bank co-brands [^pnb-kiwi-2026-06-22].

The strategic signal is distribution at scale. PNB's 10,000+ branches anchor a financial-inclusion play targeting semi-urban and rural users who transact via QR codes but lack formal credit access [^pnb-kiwi-2026-06-22]. For Scapia, this is not a direct competitive threat today — the target segments and reward propositions are distinct — but it validates that credit-on-UPI is becoming a product category expectation, and Kiwi is now the best-distributed player in it [Inferred · High].
