## What we know

**Fintech-first planning emerging** — 45-52% of affluent Indian travellers now use fintech apps to compare forex rates and optimise payments before booking[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28]
**8-15% savings documented** — Strategic currency management and card selection delivers 8-15% reduction in total trip costs; ₹80K-₹1.5L annual savings on ₹10L+ spend[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28]
**Rewards gaming goes mainstream** — Premium cardholders spending ₹5L+ internationally are accumulating 4-6 complimentary flights annually through deliberate point-stacking across cards[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28]
**Multi-app portfolio standard** — Affluent travellers maintain 2-3 premium travel cards plus multiple fintech apps (Wise, Revolut, regional); single-platform loyalty is declining[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28]
**60-day planning buffer emerging** — Financial optimisation now begins 6-8 weeks before travel, creating a long pre-booking window for card and platform acquisition[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28]
**5-10x multipliers drive concentration** — International spend multipliers of 5-10x on premium cards are the primary driver of booking consolidation on specific payment instruments[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28]

## Market Intel

**Direction:** Tailwind

**What's shifting:** Affluent Indian travellers are formalising payment strategy as a distinct phase of trip planning — comparing forex rates weeks in advance, stacking rewards across 2-3 premium cards, and using fintech apps (Wise, Revolut) to lock currency rates before booking[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28]. Financial optimisation now "rivals destination selection" in decision weight[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28].

**Why it matters:** This behaviour pattern structurally favours Scapia's proposition. The segment spending ₹8L-₹20L annually on international travel[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28] is precisely who a zero-forex-markup, travel-native credit card is built for. The 60-day planning window creates a long acquisition opportunity — these users are actively researching card choices well before they book[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28]. The multi-card standard means Scapia does not need to displace a primary card; it can enter as the travel-specialist leg of a 2-3 card portfolio [Inferred · High].

**Implication:** Scapia should invest in pre-travel research content — rewards calculators, forex comparison tools, "how to build a travel card portfolio" guides — to capture this segment during their 6-8 week planning window[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28]. The risk is that Wise and Revolut are named alongside banks as the preferred fintech tools, meaning Scapia must assert its credit-card structural advantages (no capital lock, rewards earn, credit building) before the user defaults to a prepaid mental model [Inferred · High].

## Coverage

### Affluent Indians Strategise Travel Finance — 2026-06-28

Consumer research published on NomadLawyer.org documents a structural shift among India's affluent travellers: 45-52% now use fintech apps to optimise forex and rewards before booking, and financial strategy "rivals destination selection" in planning priority[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28]. Travellers spending ₹8L-₹20L annually are accumulating 4-6 complimentary flights through deliberate card stacking, with 5-10x international multipliers driving booking concentration and 8-15% total trip cost savings achievable through combined forex and rewards optimisation[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28].

For Scapia, this is a direct tailwind: the segment described — digitally native, internationally mobile, rewards-aware, maintaining 2-3 premium travel cards — is Scapia's core acquisition target[^affluent-travel-2026-06-28]. The 60-day pre-booking planning window is an underexploited acquisition moment. The competitive note is that Wise and Revolut are named as default fintech tools in this segment, requiring Scapia to actively insert the credit-card-vs-prepaid argument — no capital lock, rewards earn, domestic usability — into the research flow before users default to a multi-app prepaid posture [Inferred · High].
