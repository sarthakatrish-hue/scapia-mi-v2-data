## Coverage

### Amex Reshuffles MR Redemptions, Cuts Taj Value — 2025-04-01

American Express revised the Membership Rewards redemption programme on its Platinum Travel Credit Card from 1 April 2025, removing Flipkart as a redemption option, adding Postcard Hotels (20,000 MR = ₹10,000) and Air India (20,000 MR = ₹6,000), and devaluing Taj vouchers by 20% — from ₹0.50 to ₹0.40 per MR point, meaning cardholders now need 12,500 MR for a ₹5,000 Taj voucher versus the prior 10,000 MR[^amex-apr2025-cc]. Amex frames the overall value as roughly neutral, pointing to Postcard Hotels' ₹0.50/MR rate as an offset.

The Taj devaluation is the sharpest cut: Taj properties are a prestige redemption that high-fee cardholders specifically seek, and a 20% value reduction is not neutralised by adding a boutique hotel chain most cardholders haven't heard of[^amex-apr2025-cc]. The Air India addition at ₹0.30/MR (6,000 value on 20,000 points) is notably lower than even the Taj devalued rate. For Scapia, this is additional evidence that premium-tier incumbents are quietly narrowing programme value — and that travel cardholders increasingly need to recalculate their redemption maths after every annual cycle.
