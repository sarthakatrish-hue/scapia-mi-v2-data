## Market Intel

**Direction:** Neutral → watch

**What's shifting:** Nykaa is pivoting from broad marketplace loyalty to a brand-linked micro-loyalty model (Glam Pass), as quick-commerce and lifestyle platforms encroach on its beauty stronghold. The Indian loyalty program market itself is entering a steep growth curve[^nykaa-2026-06-29].

**Why it matters:** Loyalty programs are becoming the primary retention weapon in Indian retail. Nykaa's brand-co-funded approach signals that the next wave of loyalty design disaggregates rewards by vertical and brand affinity — a model that travel-adjacent lifestyle categories (beauty, fashion, experiences) may replicate[^nykaa-2026-06-29].

**Implication:** For Scapia, Nykaa-style brand-linked passes represent a potential partnership surface — co-branded or co-funded rewards on beauty/lifestyle spend could deepen card stickiness beyond pure travel. More immediately, the loyalty market trajectory validates Scapia's own rewards-led positioning as differentiated and durable [Inferred · Medium].

## Coverage

### Nykaa's Glam Pass loyalty bet
Nykaa is rolling out Glam Pass, a brand-specific loyalty pass priced from ₹39, offering 10% discounts on initial purchases across labels like NYX and Minimalist — currently live for select Mumbai users with city-wide expansion imminent[^nykaa-2026-06-29]. The move is a direct counter to quick-commerce rivals (Blinkit, Swiggy Instamart) and fashion-beauty crossover players (Myntra, Reliance Tira) that are eroding its ~20% online beauty market share[^nykaa-2026-06-29].

The broader signal is structural: India's loyalty program market is on track to nearly quadruple from $4.3B to $17.1B by 2035[^nykaa-2026-06-29]. Nykaa's brand-co-funded model — letting individual labels finance their own rewards — could become a template across lifestyle verticals. For Scapia, this opens a potential partnership lane where beauty/lifestyle brand rewards sit alongside travel perks on the card, reinforcing spend stickiness [Inferred · Medium].
