## What we know

**Industry spending crosses ₹2 lakh crore** — Total credit card spending reached ₹2.02 lakh crore in May 2026, up 2.6% MoM and 6.6% YoY, recovering after April's slowdown[^cc-mkt-2026-06-27]
**~10 lakh new cards added** — The industry added approximately 10 lakh new credit cards in May, taking total cards in force to nearly 12.1 crore[^cc-mkt-2026-06-27]
**Transaction volumes up 25.8% YoY** — 587 million transactions were recorded in May, but average transaction value fell 15% YoY to ₹3,445, indicating a shift to smaller-ticket usage[^cc-mkt-2026-06-27]
**Average spend per card declining** — Average spend per card dropped 1.8% YoY to ₹16,769, reflecting the small-ticket transaction mix shift[^cc-mkt-2026-06-27]
**Top four issuers losing incremental share** — HDFC, SBI, ICICI, and Axis together accounted for only 63.7% of incremental spending in May despite holding ~76% of total spend stock[^cc-mkt-2026-06-27]
**Mid-tier challengers accelerating** — Federal Bank, IDFC First Bank, and RBL Bank are gaining momentum in both new issuances and incremental spend[^cc-mkt-2026-06-27]
**Axis Bank lagging in new cards** — Axis Bank issued only 50,000 new cards in May — significantly below SBI Cards (1.8 lakh), HDFC Bank (1.4 lakh), and even Federal Bank (~1 lakh)[^cc-mkt-2026-06-27]

## Market-Signal Intel

**Direction:** Tailwind

**What's shifting:** The Indian credit card market's growth momentum is decentralising — large incumbents are losing incremental share to mid-tier and fintech-affiliated issuers, while the nature of credit card usage is shifting toward higher-frequency, lower-value transactions. The average transaction value falling 15% YoY signals credit cards are penetrating everyday spend categories rather than concentrating in premium discretionary purchases.

**Why it matters:** For Scapia, this structural shift has two dimensions. First, Federal Bank's emergence as the fastest-growing issuer by new cards (11.7 lakh over 12 months, overtaking both ICICI and Axis) validates the co-brand fintech model and confirms that mid-tier bank distribution can compete with large incumbents when paired with a sharp product proposition[^cc-mkt-2026-06-27]. Second, the 15% fall in average transaction value and 1.8% decline in spend per card indicate that credit card growth is now being driven by mass-market everyday usage — which widens the addressable base but also means the premium travel segment (Scapia's core) remains a distinct, high-value niche rather than the industry's growth engine.

**Implication:** Scapia should monitor whether Federal Bank's issuance surge is driven by its own-label cards or reflects latent co-brand demand that will accelerate once the RBI freeze lifts[^cc-mkt-2026-06-27]. The top-four concentration erosion is a structural tailwind for challenger propositions — but Scapia needs to convert the Federal Bank freeze resolution into rapid acquisition to capture this window before incumbents defend with further product launches. ## Coverage

### India Card Market Decentralises in May — 2026-06-27

India's credit card market posted solid May 2026 metrics — ₹2.02 lakh crore in spending (+6.6% YoY) and ~10 lakh new cards — but the headline numbers mask a structural shift beneath[^cc-mkt-2026-06-27]. The top four issuers (HDFC, SBI, ICICI, Axis) captured only 63.7% of incremental spending despite controlling ~76% of the stock, as Federal Bank, IDFC First Bank, and RBL Bank accelerated. Transaction volumes jumped 25.8% YoY to 587 million, yet average transaction value fell 15% to ₹3,445 — credit cards are going mass-market.

The read for Scapia is a mixed tailwind: the challenger model is being validated by Federal Bank's 12-month issuance surge, but the market's small-ticket shift means travel-premium spend remains a distinct niche requiring deliberate targeting[^cc-mkt-2026-06-27]. Axis Bank's sharply lagging new issuances (50,000 vs Federal Bank's ~1 lakh in May) underscore how quickly card-market momentum can shift — and how much Scapia stands to benefit once its own Federal Bank co-brand pipeline reopens.
