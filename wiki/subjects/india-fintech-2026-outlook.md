## What we know

**UPI at massive scale** — UPI processed more than 14 billion monthly transactions by late 2025, now powering over 80% of all digital payments in India [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]
**Lending leads capital flows** — Growth and late-stage funding is concentrating in lending (affordable housing, MSME, consumer embedded finance); payments funding is skewing early-stage as the market stabilises [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]
**IPO pipeline building** — Kreditbee, Fibe, and Moneyview are cited as likely 2026 lending-tech IPO candidates; Groww and Pine Labs already listed in 2025 [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]
**Payments banks upgrading** — Airtel Payments Bank and Fino are pursuing small finance bank pathways, signalling ambition beyond narrow digital rails [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]
**Cross-border push** — Razorpay, Cashfree Payments, and others are expanding into cross-border remittances as India's outward remittance market exceeded $29 billion in FY25 [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]
**Tokenisation gap** — Only 40% of users complete card tokenisation; ~40% of card payment failures stem from mistyped details or OTP timeouts — a significant conversion opportunity [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]
**CardSync launched** — Razorpay, CRED, and Visa launched CardSync, India's first ecosystem-wide saved card tokenisation solution, targeting checkout failure reduction [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]
**Wealthtech consolidation** — Smaller wealthtech platforms face acquisition pressure from larger players segmenting across HNI, mass affluent, and fully digital cohorts [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]
**Sector legitimacy confirmed** — Industry observers and investors describe 2025 as the year fintech moved from margin to mainstream, with regulators and capital markets both validating the category [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]
**Credit-on-UPI opportunity** — Credit-on-UPI is expected to create new partnership opportunities for fintechs and banks to co-own the full lending stack [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]

## Market Intel

**Direction:** Tailwind
**What's shifting:** Indian fintech is transitioning from hypergrowth to disciplined scale — capital is concentrating in lending and AI-led models, UPI is embedding into everyday financial life at 14B+ monthly transactions, and tokenisation infrastructure is being standardised to reduce card payment failures. The next growth wave is projected to come from conversational/agentic payments, biometric authentication, and intelligent cross-border flows [^fintech2026-2026-06-28].
**Why it matters:** For Scapia, the macro tailwinds are structural. A maturing credit market — with lending-tech IPOs, credit-on-UPI expansion, and embedded finance growth — creates a larger addressable pool of creditworthy, digitally-native card users. The push to fix tokenisation (CardSync, unified verification frameworks) directly reduces checkout friction for credit card transactions, improving conversion for co-branded card products [^fintech2026-2026-06-28] [Inferred · High].
**Implication:** Scapia sits at the intersection of two accelerating themes — travel spending and co-branded credit cards — both of which benefit from a more mature, trusted fintech ecosystem. The risk is increased competitive intensity: as lending-tech players scale and list, and as UPI-native rewards platforms (like Razorpay/POP) capture daily spend, the window to deepen travel card loyalty with digitally-native users narrows. Scapia should lean into the tokenisation push and credit-on-UPI rails to reduce onboarding friction and expand its addressable market [Inferred · Medium].

## Coverage

### Fintech 3.0 — India's 2026 Outlook — 2026-06-28

India's fintech sector enters 2026 in a markedly different posture than the hypergrowth years — more profitable, more regulated, and more embedded in mainstream infrastructure [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]. UPI crossed 14 billion monthly transactions by late 2025, payments banks are pursuing full banking licences, and Groww and Pine Labs have already validated the public-market thesis [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]. Capital is flowing selectively: lending (affordable housing, MSME, embedded consumer credit) leads, while payments funding skews early-stage. Kreditbee, Fibe, and Moneyview are flagged as 2026 IPO candidates [^fintech2026-2026-06-28].

For Scapia, two signals stand out. First, tokenisation remains broken — only 40% of users complete the process and ~40% of card failures stem from checkout friction — creating a real conversion opportunity that CardSync (Razorpay + CRED + Visa) is beginning to address [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]. Second, credit-on-UPI is expected to unlock a new bank-fintech partnership stack, expanding the pool of creditworthy users reachable via co-branded card models [^fintech2026-2026-06-28] [Inferred · High]. The 2026 environment rewards precision: fintechs with clear profit-pool visibility and strong product-market fit will attract capital; those without will consolidate or exit.
