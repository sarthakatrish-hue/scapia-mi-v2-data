## What we know

**Verified name display** — UPI apps must now show bank-registered recipient names on QR/mobile-number payments, removing unverified nicknames[^npci-jun2026]
**EPFO UPI withdrawals** — EPFO testing instant PF withdrawals via UPI; testing reportedly complete, full rollout pending[^npci-jun2026]
**Fraud-reduction mandate** — Change eliminates spoofed merchant tags, making scammer impersonation significantly harder[^npci-jun2026]

## Regulatory Intel

**Status:** Active — changes taking effect June 2026[^npci-jun2026]
**What it requires:** UPI apps must display only bank-verified recipient names at point of payment confirmation; custom or unverified merchant nicknames are disallowed[^npci-jun2026]
**Posture:** Broadly positive for trust in digital payments; no direct compliance burden on card issuers, but any app or partner integrating UPI (including travel-payments flows) must surface the verified name[^npci-jun2026]
**Implication:** Raises consumer confidence in UPI as a payment rail, which could accelerate UPI adoption at travel merchants and reduce chargeback/fraud risk on UPI-linked products. For Scapia, which sits on a travel-payments stack, a safer UPI environment is a tailwind for any UPI-linked feature roadmap. [Inferred · Medium]

## Coverage

### NPCI Verified Names, EPFO UPI Withdrawals — 2026-06-24

NPCI is rolling out a verified-name display requirement for UPI transactions in June 2026: whenever a user scans a QR code or enters a mobile number, apps must show the bank-registered name of the recipient rather than any custom alias[^npci-jun2026]. The move is designed to close a common fraud vector where scammers use lookalike display names to deceive payers.

Simultaneously, EPFO has completed testing of instant provident fund withdrawals via UPI, with Union Labour Minister Mansukh Mandaviya confirming members will soon be able to withdraw EPF balances directly into their bank accounts through UPI[^npci-jun2026]. Taken together, these two developments expand UPI's utility and trustworthiness — a structural tailwind for any fintech building on top of the UPI rail.
