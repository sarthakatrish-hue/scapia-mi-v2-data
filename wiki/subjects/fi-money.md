## What we know

**B2B AI pivot announced** — Fi is sunsetting consumer products and repositioning as an AI-driven enterprise technology provider for startups and large enterprises [^fi-b2b-2026-06-22]
**Shared issuer with Scapia** — Fi's consumer banking ran on Federal Bank infrastructure, the same partner that issues Scapia's co-branded card [^fi-b2b-2026-06-22]
**Workforce under 100** — Layoffs over the past year reduced Fi's total headcount to under 100 as of July 2025, with further role cuts signalled [^fi-b2b-2026-06-22]

## Competitor Intel

**Direction:** Tailwind — Fi Money's exit from consumer neobanking removes a direct competitor for Scapia's core under-30, digital-first, Federal Bank-adjacent user base and validates the difficulty of building revenue on free-payment rails without travel-native differentiation.

**Where we win**
**Travel-native moat confirmed** — Fi's failure on generalist rewards (mutual funds, US stocks, loyalty programmes) proves that undifferentiated fintech perks do not retain or monetise young Indians; Scapia's travel-rewards stack addresses a specific, high-intent spend category Fi never owned [Inferred · High].
**Federal Bank relationship** — Scapia is now Federal Bank's premier consumer fintech co-brand; Fi's departure reduces reputational and operational noise on the shared issuer, potentially freeing bandwidth for Scapia-aligned product development [Inferred · Medium].

**Where they're ahead**
**AI/B2B pivot is unproven** — Fi's enterprise AI pivot could yield nothing competitively relevant to Scapia, but if successful it could resurface as infrastructure for a future challenger card issuer [Inferred · Low].

**What to do**
**Capture displaced users** — Fi's 3M consumer base is now orphaned; a targeted acquisition push (especially Federal Bank existing customers) could convert churned Fi users into Scapia cardholders.
**Monitor Federal Bank capacity** — Fi's wind-down of consumer operations on Federal Bank's stack may free up issuer bandwidth; Scapia should explore whether this accelerates any product or limit-increase roadmap with Federal Bank.

## Coverage

### Fi Money's Consumer Exit Explained — 2026-06-22

Fi Money, the Federal Bank-backed neobank founded in 2019, is abandoning its consumer banking products to pivot toward AI-driven enterprise technology [^fi-b2b-2026-06-22]. Cofounder Sujith Narayanan announced the reset publicly, citing unsustainable burn, weak monetisation, and the need for a structural rebuild [^fi-b2b-2026-06-22]. The move follows layoffs that cut Fi's workforce to under 100, a runway collapse from 18 to ~10 months between December 2024 and March 2025, and FY23 losses of ₹300 Cr on just ₹38 Cr in operating revenue [^fi-b2b-2026-06-22].

The strategic read for Scapia is largely positive. Fi and Scapia share a common issuer in Federal Bank, and Fi's consumer exit removes a digitally native competitor that once chased the same under-30 cohort [^fi-b2b-2026-06-22]. More importantly, Fi's implosion — despite $137M raised and strong early design credentials — validates that generic fintech rewards (mutual funds, US stocks, undifferentiated cashback) cannot build durable engagement or revenue among young Indians [^fi-b2b-2026-06-22]. Scapia's travel-native positioning sidesteps exactly the commoditisation trap Fi fell into [Inferred · High].

The residual watch item is Federal Bank's operational bandwidth. Fi's winding consumer operations sat on Federal Bank's infrastructure, and user complaints about delayed refunds and slow support highlighted the shared-issuer risk [^fi-b2b-2026-06-22]. As Fi's load on Federal Bank's systems diminishes, Scapia should actively assess whether the freed capacity can be directed toward faster card issuance, higher credit limits, or new product features — and monitor whether any acquirer of Fi's user base introduces a competing card product [Inferred · Medium].
