## What we know

**Four-OTA earnings cluster** — MakeMyTrip, Ixigo, TBO Tek, and Yatra all reported within days of each other in May 2026, enabling direct strategic comparison [^skift1]
**Sharp AI divergence** — Ixigo is rebuilding its platform; MakeMyTrip is measuring AI outcomes; TBO treats AI as a productivity tool; Yatra's AI plans are vague [^skift1]
**Conversational booking emerging** — The leading thesis among AI-bullish OTAs is that menus and filters will give way to preference-aware conversational interfaces [^skift1]
**No consensus on disruption timing** — At least one major player (TBO) explicitly expressed skepticism that meaningful disruption is close [^skift1]
**Millions of Indians affected** — The pace of AI adoption across these four companies will shape how the mass market books and manages travel in coming years [^skift1]

## Market Intel

**Direction:** Accelerating — India's largest OTAs are beginning to structurally differentiate on AI investment, moving from experiment to platform commitment

**What's shifting:** The travel booking interface itself is being contested; Ixigo is betting on conversational AI while others hedge, meaning the booking surface Scapia cardholders use daily is in flux [^skift1]

**Why it matters:** Scapia's value proposition is tied to travel spend; if the OTA a cardholder primarily uses becomes AI-native and more sticky, Scapia must ensure it is the preferred payment method inside that experience [Inferred · High]

**Implication:** Scapia should (a) monitor whether Ixigo NEXT drives measurable GMV growth that lifts co-brand card spend, (b) evaluate whether MakeMyTrip's measured AI approach creates a partnership opening, and (c) treat the OTA AI race as a spend-surface risk — not just a competitor intelligence item [Inferred · Medium]

## Developments
