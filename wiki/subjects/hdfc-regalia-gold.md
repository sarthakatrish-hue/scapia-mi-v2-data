## What we know

**Recommended as Axis devaluation alternative** — Consumer guides in April 2026 are positioning HDFC Regalia Gold as a top replacement card for cardholders moving away from Axis Bank travel cards, citing its 4 RP per ₹150 spend, 12 domestic + 6 international lounge visits, and SmartBuy 10X portal[^regalia-axis-alt-2026]
**SmartBuy 10X highlighted as differentiator** — The Regalia Gold's accelerated rewards via the SmartBuy portal are specifically cited as a flexible redemption benefit attracting Axis defectors[^regalia-axis-alt-2026]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Unconditional lounge access** — Scapia offers unlimited domestic lounge visits with no quarterly spend gate; Regalia Gold's 3-visit allowance now disappears entirely if the cardholder spends below Rs 60,000 in the prior quarter[^regalia-deval-2026-06-26]
**Zero forex cost** — Scapia's 0% forex markup vs Regalia Gold's 3.5% markup plus the new 1.75% DCC fee means international travellers paying in INR now face up to 5.25% cost on those transactions with HDFC[^regalia-deval-2026-06-26]
**No annual fee** — Scapia removes the Rs 2,500 joining and annual fee barrier and the Rs 4,00,000 spend-waiver pressure entirely[^regalia-deval-2026-06-26]
**Simpler value proposition** — Multiple simultaneous cuts (reward rate, lounge condition, reissuance fee, DCC fee) create cardholder confusion; Scapia's single-layer benefit structure is a clarity advantage[^regalia-deval-2026-06-26]

### Where they're ahead
**Priority Pass international lounge** — 6 international lounge visits via Priority Pass remain intact and unconditional, a benefit Scapia does not currently match for international travellers[^regalia-deval-2026-06-26]
**Brand trust at scale** — HDFC's distribution and brand recognition mean Regalia Gold remains the default mid-premium card for millions despite the devaluation[^regalia-deval-2026-06-26]
**Boarding Edge Programme** — The new travel perk (spa, buffet, upgrade, transfer) partially offsets the lounge cut and adds perceived novelty that Scapia lacks an equivalent for[^regalia-deval-2026-06-26]

### What to do
**Target sub-Rs 60,000 quarterly spenders** — These cardholders now lose all domestic lounge access; Scapia's unconditional lounge offer is a direct, quantifiable upgrade for this segment
**Amplify DCC comparison** — Surface the cumulative forex cost (3.5% markup + 1.75% DCC) in international travel content; the combined number is a compelling switching trigger
**Comparison content** — Publish side-by-side benefit breakdowns at the post-devaluation values, not the pre-May numbers that still circulate widely online
**Activate switcher moment** — The July 1 lounge condition effective date creates a real-world "first bad experience" moment; deploy retargeting around that date[^regalia-deval-2026-06-26]

## Coverage

### Regalia Gold Positioned as Axis Defector Target — 2026-04-02

Consumer card guides published in April 2026 are directing Axis Bank devaluation refugees — particularly those leaving Atlas and Magnus — toward HDFC Regalia Gold as the primary travel-rewards alternative, citing 12 domestic + 6 international lounge visits, 4 RP per ₹150, and SmartBuy 10X access as the competing value proposition[^regalia-axis-alt-2026]. This is a notable indirect competitor signal: HDFC is positioned to capture the Axis cardholder churn that Scapia should also be targeting.

The Regalia Gold's own May 2026 devaluation (reward rate cut 6%, lounge conditionalised from July) means it is simultaneously being recommended as an upgrade while itself becoming less competitive — a window Scapia can exploit by reaching Axis defectors before they default to HDFC. [Inferred · High]

### Regalia Gold Reward Rate Cut, Lounge Conditionalised — 2026-06-26

HDFC Bank reduced the Regalia Gold's base reward rate from 4 points per Rs 150 to 5 points per Rs 200 from 15 May 2026, cutting effective earn rate by 6% to 1.625%[^regalia-deval-2026-06-26]. More impactfully, domestic lounge access — previously unconditional — now requires Rs 60,000 in prior-quarter spend to unlock just 3 visits, effective 1 July 2026. HDFC also introduced a Rs 199 card reissuance fee and raised the DCC fee to 1.75% on international INR transactions, adding cost layers that compound the value erosion[^regalia-deval-2026-06-26].

At Rs 1 lakh/month spend, cardholders lose approximately Rs 650/year on rewards alone — modest in isolation but material when stacked with conditional lounge loss and higher forex costs[^regalia-deval-2026-06-26]. For Scapia, the July 1 lounge condition effective date is a concrete acquisition moment: Regalia Gold holders who miss the spending threshold will experience their first zero-lounge quarter and are primed to switch to a card with unconditional access. [Inferred · High]
