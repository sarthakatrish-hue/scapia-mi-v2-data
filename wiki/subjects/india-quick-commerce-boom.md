## Market Intel

**Direction:** Tailwind

**What's shifting:** Quick commerce is moving from a grocery niche to a dominant e-commerce format, capturing 17% of India's GMV today and projected at 40% by 2030[^amzflip-2026-07-02]. The category is expanding beyond FMCG into electronics, luggage, and travel accessories — spend occasions adjacent to Scapia's core travel customer.

**Why it matters:** As q-commerce platforms capture pre-trip and daily spend, they accumulate transaction data and loyalty relationships with the exact users Scapia wants to own. Amazon's Prime flywheel, Flipkart's Gen Z penetration, and Blinkit's profitability moat all compound this dynamic. The fintech implication: co-branded cards on q-commerce platforms (Amazon Pay ICICI, Flipkart Axis) could evolve to reward travel spend, narrowing Scapia's differentiation.

**Implication:** Scapia should treat the q-commerce boom as a tailwind for customer acquisition (travel-adjacent spend is growing) but a structural watch for loyalty competition — the platforms that own daily-spend relationships will have increasing leverage to cross-sell financial products, including travel credit. ## Coverage

### India Q-Commerce Hits Inflection Point — 2026-07-02

Bain & Company's April 2026 report, cited in new CNBC coverage, confirms India as the global leader in quick-commerce adoption with 17% of e-commerce GMV already on q-commerce platforms — and projects the market growing sixfold to $65–70 Bn by 2030, representing up to 40% of online retail sales[^amzflip-2026-07-02]. The category has expanded well beyond grocery into luggage, travel accessories, and electronics, while Gen Z has emerged as the fastest-growing user cohort, making up 40% of Flipkart Minutes' base[^amzflip-2026-07-02].

For Scapia, this is a structural market signal on two levels: the travel-accessories adjacency means q-commerce platforms are now capturing pre-trip spend occasions, and the Gen Z loyalty relationships being built on Blinkit, Zepto, and Flipkart Minutes are with the same customers Scapia needs to convert. The co-branded card ecosystem layered onto these platforms — Amazon Pay ICICI, Flipkart Axis — is the channel through which this tailwind could become a headwind if extended to travel rewards.
