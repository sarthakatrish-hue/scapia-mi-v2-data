## What we know

**B2B travel distributor** — TBO Tek operates as a travel distribution platform, not a consumer-facing OTA; business model differs from Scapia's B2C card [^skift-ai-div2]
**Cautious AI posture** — TBO Tek sees AI primarily as an internal productivity tool, not as an imminent disruptive force in travel [^skift-ai-div2]
**No platform redesign signaled** — Unlike Ixigo, TBO is not rebuilding customer-facing products around AI [^skift-ai-div2] [Inferred · High] suggesting conservative investment appetite in AI for now
**Earnings call cluster May 2026** — Participated alongside MakeMyTrip, Ixigo, and Yatra in a cluster of four Indian travel earnings calls within days of one another [^skift-ai-div2]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win

**AI ambition gap** — TBO's own management signals low near-term AI disruption confidence, reducing the chance of a sudden product leap that affects Scapia's market [^skift1]

### Where they're ahead

## Coverage

### TBO confirms cautious AI stance — 2026-06-01

TBO Tek's May 2026 earnings call reinforced its position as the most conservative among the four major Indian travel companies on AI [^skift-ai-div2]. Management described AI as an internal efficiency and productivity tool, stopping well short of signaling any consumer-facing platform transformation or near-term industry disruption.

For Scapia, TBO's posture is reassuring in the short run — its B2B model and low AI ambition mean no sudden consumer loyalty surface emerges to compete with Scapia cardholders [^skift-ai-div2] [Inferred · High]. The signal to watch is any future pivot toward B2C, which would change the calculus materially.
