## What we know

**B2B travel distributor** — TBO Tek operates as a travel distribution platform, not a consumer-facing OTA; business model differs from Scapia's B2C card [^skift1]
**Cautious AI posture** — TBO Tek sees AI primarily as an internal productivity tool, not as an imminent disruptive force in travel [^skift1]
**No platform redesign signaled** — Unlike Ixigo, TBO is not rebuilding customer-facing products around AI [^skift1] [Inferred · High] suggesting conservative investment appetite in AI for now
**Earnings call Q1 2026** — Participated in a cluster of four Indian travel company earnings calls in late May 2026 [^skift1]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Consumer-facing relevance** — Scapia operates where travelers transact directly; TBO's B2B model means it has no direct consumer loyalty surface to threaten Scapia cardholders [Inferred · High]
**AI ambition gap** — TBO's own management signals low near-term AI disruption confidence, reducing the chance of a sudden product leap that affects Scapia's market [^skift1]

### Where they're ahead
**Distribution infrastructure** — TBO's B2B rails power travel agent and SME bookings at scale; if a future Scapia B2B or corporate product emerges, TBO is an incumbent [Inferred · Low]

### What to do
**Keep on watch, not threat** — TBO's current AI stance and B2B focus make it low-urgency for Scapia's consumer card; reassess if TBO announces a B2C pivot [Inferred · Medium]
