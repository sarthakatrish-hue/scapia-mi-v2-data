## What we know

**Consumer UPI entry** — Razorpay acquired a majority stake in rewards-first UPI app POP for $30 million, marking its first move into consumer-facing UPI payments [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22]
**Loyalty stack deepening** — Previously acquired PoshVine (2022) for loyalty; POP now extends that into a merchant-facing payments + loyalty + engagement single platform [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22]
**Profitable unicorn** — Razorpay reported revenue of ₹2,068 crore and profit of ₹35 crore in FY24, one of few profitable fintech unicorns in India [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22]
**India re-domicile** — Razorpay completed regulatory steps to shift domicile back to India from the US, including transition to public limited company and merger approval [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22]
**CardSync launch** — Razorpay partnered with CRED and Visa to launch CardSync, India's first ecosystem-wide saved card tokenisation solution, targeting the ~40% card payment failure rate from checkout friction [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]
**Cross-border expansion** — Razorpay is widening into cross-border remittances as India's outward remittance market exceeded $29 billion in FY25 [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]
**Unified verification push** — Razorpay COO Rahul Kothari is advocating for a shared fraud intelligence network combining signals from banks, telecoms, devices, and user behaviour, with universal tokenisation and mandatory SIM/device-account linking [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Travel-first rewards depth** — Scapia's card is purpose-built for travel with zero forex markup and flight/hotel rewards; POP's POPcoins are generalised multi-brand currency with no disclosed travel-specific advantage [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22].
**Co-brand card credibility** — Scapia operates a regulated Federal Bank co-branded Visa card; POP's RuPay card is early-stage with ~40,000 issued vs Scapia's established cardholder base [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22].

### Where they're ahead
**Merchant network moat** — Razorpay's existing merchant payment rails give POP instant distribution across millions of merchant touchpoints — a closed rewards loop Scapia cannot replicate [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22].
**UPI native rewards** — POPcoins earned at the UPI payment moment create habitual daily engagement; Scapia's rewards accrue only on card swipes, not UPI transactions [Inferred · High].
**Tokenisation infrastructure** — CardSync (with CRED and Visa) positions Razorpay at the centre of the card tokenisation stack, reducing checkout friction across the ecosystem and strengthening merchant lock-in [^fintech2026-2026-06-28].
**Cross-border rails** — Expansion into a $29B+ outward remittance market opens a high-value, internationally mobile user cohort that overlaps with Scapia's travel-first audience [^fintech2026-2026-06-28] [Inferred · Medium].
**Capital and scale** — $30 million fresh capital plus profitable balance sheet gives significant runway to subsidise rewards and grow the card base aggressively [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22].

### What to do
**Watch POP travel redemptions** — If POPcoins add flights/hotels as redemption categories, competitive overlap with Scapia sharpens materially; set a trigger to review at next product announcement.
**Track CardSync adoption** — As tokenisation completion rates improve via CardSync, credit card checkout conversion rises across the board; Scapia should ensure it benefits from, not is bypassed by, this infrastructure.
**Monitor cross-border product** — Razorpay's remittance push targets internationally mobile users — the same traveller Scapia wants; watch for any bundled forex or travel card offering.

## Coverage

### Razorpay Expands Beyond Payments Rails
A 2026 fintech outlook piece positions Razorpay as one of the payments players actively widening its canvas beyond domestic rails [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]. Alongside its POP acquisition, Razorpay is expanding into cross-border remittances (India's outward market: $29B+ in FY25) and co-launched CardSync with CRED and Visa — India's first ecosystem-wide saved card tokenisation solution — to address the ~40% card payment failure rate caused by checkout friction [^fintech2026-2026-06-28]. COO Rahul Kothari is publicly advocating for a shared fraud intelligence network with universal tokenisation and device/SIM-to-account linking as the next infrastructure layer [^fintech2026-2026-06-28].

The CardSync initiative is notable for Scapia specifically: if tokenisation completion rates rise, card-on-file transactions improve across all co-branded cards including Scapia's [Inferred · High]. But Razorpay's central position in that infrastructure — combined with its merchant distribution and POP's consumer rewards loop — means it is building a closed ecosystem where everyday and travel spend can be captured end-to-end, before users reach a dedicated travel card [Inferred · Medium].

### Razorpay Bets Big on Consumer Rewards — 2026-06-22

Razorpay acquired a majority stake in Bengaluru-based rewards-first UPI app POP for $30 million, marking the payments giant's first direct entry into the consumer UPI space [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22]. POP, which launched its UPI platform in June 2024, claims 6 lakh daily transactions and 1 million unique monthly users within its first year, and has issued over 40,000 RuPay credit cards in partnership with Yes Bank [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22]. POP will operate as a separate entity under the deal.

The acquisition is strategic layering rather than opportunism: Razorpay bought loyalty platform PoshVine in 2022 and now adds POP to complete a merchant-facing stack of payments, loyalty, and engagement in one platform [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22]. POP's core product — POPcoins, a multi-brand rewards currency earned on UPI payments and redeemable across its merchant network — gives Razorpay a consumer hook that its B2B merchant business never had [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22].

For Scapia, the signal is indirect but meaningful. Razorpay's merchant distribution combined with POP's daily-spend rewards loop targets the same digitally-native, rewards-motivated consumer Scapia courts [Inferred · High]. If POPcoins expand into travel redemptions, or if POP's RuPay card scales rapidly under Razorpay's capital, the competitive overlap will tighten — particularly among users who may satisfy their rewards appetite within the UPI layer before ever gravitating toward a dedicated travel credit card [Inferred · Medium].
