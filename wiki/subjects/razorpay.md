## Competitor Intel

**Direction:** Headwind
**What's shifting:** Razorpay is moving from pure B2B merchant payments into consumer-facing UPI rewards, acquiring POP to combine payments, loyalty, and commerce in one platform — directly competing for the digitally-native, rewards-seeking consumer [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22].
**Why it matters:** POP's POPcoins multi-brand rewards currency, 6 lakh daily transactions, and 40,000+ RuPay credit cards issued with Yes Bank signal a credible, scaling consumer rewards play that could intercept the same user Scapia targets at the UPI spending layer — before they seek a travel card [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22] [Inferred · High].
**Implication:** Razorpay's merchant distribution + POP's consumer rewards loop creates a closed ecosystem where everyday spend earns cross-brand rewards, reducing the marginal appeal of a dedicated travel rewards card for casual or aspirational travelers [Inferred · Medium]. Scapia should monitor POP's travel-category redemption options and whether Razorpay steers POPcoins toward flight or hotel rewards.

### Where we win
**Travel-first rewards depth** — Scapia's card is purpose-built for travel with zero forex markup and flight/hotel rewards; POP's POPcoins are generalised multi-brand currency with no disclosed travel-specific advantage.
**Co-brand card credibility** — Scapia operates a regulated Federal Bank co-branded Visa card; POP's RuPay card is early-stage with 40,000 issued vs Scapia's established cardholder base [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22].

### Where they're ahead
**Merchant network moat** — Razorpay's existing merchant payment rails give POP instant distribution across millions of merchant touchpoints — a closed rewards loop Scapia cannot replicate [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22].
**UPI native rewards** — POPcoins earned at the UPI payment moment create a habitual daily engagement loop; Scapia's card rewards accrue only on card swipes, not UPI transactions [Inferred · High].
**Capital and scale** — $30 million fresh capital plus Razorpay's profitable balance sheet gives POP significant runway to subsidise rewards and grow its card base aggressively [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22].

### What to do
**Watch POP travel redemptions** — If POPcoins add flights/hotels as redemption categories, the competitive overlap with Scapia sharpens materially; set a trigger to review at next funding or product announcement.
**Track RuPay card issuance pace** — POP at 40,000 cards in under a year with Yes Bank is early but fast; monitor whether Razorpay pushes this to a flagship credit product with travel benefits.

## Coverage

### Razorpay Bets Big on Consumer Rewards — 2026-06-22

Razorpay acquired a majority stake in Bengaluru-based rewards-first UPI app POP for $30 million, marking the payments giant's first direct entry into the consumer UPI space [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22]. POP, which launched its UPI platform in June 2024, claims 6 lakh daily transactions and 1 million unique monthly users within its first year, and has issued over 40,000 RuPay credit cards in partnership with Yes Bank [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22]. POP will operate as a separate entity under the deal.

The acquisition is strategic layering rather than opportunism: Razorpay bought loyalty platform PoshVine in 2022 and now adds POP to complete a merchant-facing stack of payments, loyalty, and engagement in one platform [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22]. POP's core product — POPcoins, a multi-brand rewards currency earned on UPI payments and redeemable across its merchant network — gives Razorpay a consumer hook that its B2B merchant business never had [^razorpay-pop-2026-06-22].

For Scapia, the signal is indirect but meaningful. Razorpay's merchant distribution combined with POP's daily-spend rewards loop targets the same digitally-native, rewards-motivated consumer Scapia courts [Inferred · High]. If POPcoins expand into travel redemptions, or if POP's RuPay card scales rapidly under Razorpay's capital, the competitive overlap will tighten — particularly among users who may satisfy their rewards appetite within the UPI layer before ever gravitating toward a dedicated travel credit card [Inferred · Medium].
