## What we know

**Annual fee ₹500, waived** — Effectively free; waiver condition not specified in source[^bttc-2026-06-26]
**Zero forex markup** — One of two cards in this roundup (alongside Scapia's positioning) with 0% foreign transaction fee[^bttc-2026-06-26]
**FD-backed issuance** — Minimum fixed deposit of ₹5,000 required for card issuance — a secured-credit model, not a standard unsecured card[^bttc-2026-06-26]
**Niyo Coins on domestic spend** — 10% Niyo Coins on domestic spend cited as the primary reward rate[^bttc-2026-06-26]
**Lounge access spend-gated** — Lounge access exists but requires meeting a spend criterion; not unconditional[^bttc-2026-06-26]
**No income requirement stated** — FD requirement replaces income criteria, broadening access to those without salary documentation[^bttc-2026-06-26]

## Competitor Intel

### Where we win
**Unconditional lounge access** — Scapia's unlimited lounge access is not gated by spend criteria; Niyo SBM's conditional lounge requires hitting a threshold[^bttc-2026-06-26]
**Unsecured card model** — Scapia is a standard credit card without requiring a fixed deposit; Niyo SBM's FD model restricts appeal to users with liquid capital to lock up[^bttc-2026-06-26]
**Richer travel rewards breadth** — Scapia's travel-specific rewards ecosystem vs Niyo Coins' domestic-spend focus[^bttc-2026-06-26]

### Where they're ahead
**Zero forex parity** — Niyo SBM matches Scapia's 0% forex, removing that differentiator for this segment[^bttc-2026-06-26]
**Credit-history-free access** — FD-backed model enables cardholders with no credit history to access a zero-forex card — a segment Scapia's standard credit model cannot serve[^bttc-2026-06-26]
**No-documentation path** — FD substitutes income proof, widening access to students, gig workers, and self-employed without ITR[^bttc-2026-06-26]

### What to do
**Compete on lounge conditionality** — Surface the spend-gate on Niyo SBM's lounge access in comparison content for travellers who want guaranteed access[^bttc-2026-06-26]
**FD capital-lock narrative** — ₹5,000+ locked in FD has an opportunity cost; frame Scapia as accessible without tying up savings [Inferred · High]
**Target new-to-credit graduators** — Users who started with Niyo SBM to build credit history are natural upgrade targets for Scapia's full unsecured travel card [Inferred · Medium]

## Coverage

### Niyo SBM Zero-Forex Card Profiled
A 2026 travel-card roundup introduces the Niyo SBM Credit Card to this wiki: effectively zero annual fee (₹500 waived), 0% forex markup, and issuance backed by a minimum ₹5,000 fixed deposit rather than income documentation[^bttc-2026-06-26]. The 10% Niyo Coins on domestic spend and spend-gated lounge access round out the offer. No income threshold is required, making this the most accessible card in the comparison by entry barrier.

The zero-forex match with Scapia is the key competitive signal: Niyo SBM removes Scapia's primary cost differentiator for a segment that needs the FD-backed route[^bttc-2026-06-26]. However, the secured-credit model, conditional lounge, and domestic-spend-weighted rewards mean Niyo SBM targets a structurally different user — credit-history builders and new-to-banking customers — rather than Scapia's established travel-first audience [Inferred · High].
