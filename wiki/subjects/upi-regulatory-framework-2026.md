## What we know

**Federal Bank limit confirmed** — Federal Bank's daily UPI transaction limit is ₹1,00,000, in line with most major private banks [^upi-2026-rules]
**Interchange fees introduced** — Merchant UPI transactions above ₹2,000 now attract interchange of 0.5%–1.1% depending on category (fuel, utilities, supermarkets, insurance/MF/railway etc.) [^upi-2026-rules]
**P2P stays free** — Peer-to-peer transactions and all transactions under ₹2,000 remain free of charge for users; fee is merchant-side only [^upi-2026-rules]
**Payee name verification mandate** — NPCI required all UPI apps (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM) to display only the bank-verified payee name (via Validate Address API) on the pre-transaction screen, effective 30 June 2025 [^upi-2026-rules]
**Applies to P2P and P2PM** — The payee name verification rule covers both peer-to-peer and peer-to-peer merchant transactions [^upi-2026-rules]
**Goal: fraud reduction and ecosystem sustainability** — RBI/NPCI frame the interchange and limit changes as enabling long-term viability of the UPI infrastructure and reducing high-value fraud [^upi-2026-rules]

## Regulatory Intel

**Status:** Enacted/rolling out — interchange fee schedule and transaction limit framework are described as 2026 expected rules; payee name verification effective 30 June 2025 (already in force) [^upi-2026-rules]

**What it requires:** UPI apps must show only banking-verified payee names on pre-transaction screens. Banks and PSPs apply per-bank daily/weekly/monthly transaction caps. Merchants absorb interchange fees of 0.5%–1.1% on UPI transactions above ₹2,000; P2P and sub-₹2,000 flows remain zero-cost [^upi-2026-rules]

**Posture:** Neutral-to-positive for Scapia — Scapia is a credit card product, not a UPI PSP, so it does not bear interchange costs directly. Federal Bank's ₹1L daily UPI limit is standard and does not constrain Scapia card usage. The payee-name verification rule improves trust in digital payments generally, benefiting the broader fintech ecosystem Scapia operates in [Inferred · Medium]

**Implication:** The merchant-side interchange fee on UPI transactions above ₹2,000 makes UPI slightly less cost-free for merchants compared to the prior zero-MDR regime, marginally narrowing the cost gap between UPI and credit card acceptance. This is a mild structural tailwind for credit card spend share at merchants [Inferred · Low]. For PhonePe and other UPI-credit competitors, the interchange framework legitimises revenue models around credit-on-UPI, which could accelerate their investment in UPI credit products that compete with Scapia for travel and everyday spend [Inferred · Medium]

## Coverage

### UPI 2026 Rules: Fees and Limits
RBI and NPCI have formalised a set of UPI rule updates for 2026, introducing merchant-side interchange fees (0.5%–1.1% on transactions above ₹2,000), standardised daily transaction limits (₹1L for most major banks including Federal Bank), and a payee-name verification mandate effective 30 June 2025 requiring all UPI apps to display only bank-verified recipient names[^upi-2026-rules]. P2P transactions and sub-₹2,000 flows remain free, preserving the zero-cost experience for most consumer use cases.

For Scapia, the direct regulatory impact is limited — the interchange applies to merchants, not cardholders, and Federal Bank's ₹1L daily UPI cap is in line with peers[^upi-2026-rules]. The more strategic read is structural: interchange legitimises UPI-credit revenue models for players like PhonePe, potentially accelerating their build-out of credit-on-UPI products that compete with Scapia for everyday and travel spend [Inferred · Medium]. The payee verification rule is a broad trust improvement for digital payments that benefits all participants.
