## 1. Identity

Scapia is a Bengaluru travel fintech (founded 2022 by Anil Goteti, ex-SVP
Flipkart) whose core product is a lifetime-free co-branded travel credit
card paired with an in-app travel booking platform (flights, hotels,
trains, buses, visas, store) [^facts]. Cards are issued with Federal Bank
(since June 2023) and BOBCARD [^facts]. ⚠ "Third bank partner planned" is
DISPUTED between verification passes — do not state until re-confirmed.
Series C closed May 2026: $63M led by General Catalyst at a >$500M
post-money valuation; $135M raised across four rounds ($9M seed + $23M A +
$40M B + $63M C) [^facts]. Target user: the
digitally-native Indian traveller. Scapia discloses no absolute card or
user counts — only growth multiples (flights ~6x, hotels ~8x, customers
~7x YoY as of May 2026) [^facts].

## 2. Product mechanics

All facts current as of 2026-06-11, post the Feb 27, 2026 changes [^facts].

- **Card:** Scapia Credit Card · lifetime free (₹0 joining/annual)
- **Issuers:** Federal Bank · BOBCARD
- **Network:** dual variant — Visa + RuPay (RuPay enables UPI) on one
  credit line and statement
- **Reward unit:** Scapia Coins · **5 Coins = ₹1** in-app travel value ·
  redeemable up to 100% of a booking (flights/hotels/trains/buses/visas/
  store) · no cashback or statement credit · 36-month validity
- **Earn rates:** 20 coins/₹100 (4%) on travel booked in-app · 10 coins/₹100
  (2%) on other Visa spends · 5 coins/₹100 (1%) on UPI via RuPay variant
  (⚠ ₹500 minimum per transaction to earn) · **0 coins on international
  spends**
- **Earns nothing:** insurance · utilities · rent · fuel · wallet loads
  (⚠ unverified specifics: the exact fuel-surcharge-waiver terms and the
  exact "1% fee + GST" on rent/wallet — do not state these as fact)
- **Forex:** 0% markup on international spends (vs 2–3.5% market typical)
- **Lounge:** unlimited domestic lounge on ₹20,000 spend in previous billing
  cycle (combined across both variants; rent/utilities/insurance/fuel
  excluded from the milestone) · **no international lounge** — instead
  Airport Privileges: 100% value back in coins on airport dining/shopping/
  spa — up to ₹1,000/trip at major domestic airports, ₹500 at smaller ones,
  ₹2,000 international (the international tier is unlocked by an
  international flight booking ≥₹50,000 made in-app — one benefit, not two)
- **Feb 2026 devaluation:** lounge milestone doubled ₹10K→₹20K; insurance,
  utilities AND rent cut to zero rewards; International Airport Privileges
  added; forex, fees, earn structure unchanged
- **Eligibility (aggregator figures — not first-party-verified):** ~21–65,
  ~₹35,000/month income, good bureau score; fully app-based onboarding with
  video KYC, instant virtual card

## 3. Strategic positioning

⚠ DRAFT — written from public positioning; curator to confirm/edit.

- **Wedge:** travel-first credit card for digitally-native India — the
  reward currency, redemption surface, and benefits all point at travel
- **Vs general-purpose flagships (HDFC/ICICI/Axis):** narrower but deeper
  on travel value-per-rupee; lifetime-free vs their fee-gated premium tiers
- **Vs spend-utility (CRED):** Scapia is a primary card + booking platform,
  not a payments/utility layer over existing cards
- **Vs neobank forex cards (Niyo Global, Fi):** underwritten credit with
  rewards, not prepaid forex
- **Vs OTAs (MMT, ixigo, Cleartrip):** the card + rail under the booking;
  OTAs are distribution and potential disintermediators, not card rivals
- **Moats (real, verifiable):** 0% forex at a lifetime-free price point ·
  in-app booking integration with coin redemption · dual-network UPI
  capability · multi-issuer model (Federal + BOBCARD) de-risks single-bank
  dependence
- **Moats (claimed/soft):** brand among 25–35 urban travellers ·
  ⚠ NOTE: "Federal Bank co-brand exclusivity" is NOT a moat — Scapia
  itself is multi-issuer now, and Federal also partners other fintechs

## 6. Key competitor frame (one-liners only)

Factual anchors verified 2026-06-11 (Atlas discontinuation, Kiwi's 4 banks,
AmEx pause); the FRAMING is the curator's to tune. Full assessments live in
wiki/entities/.

- **Axis Atlas** — closest direct travel-card rival; discontinued to new
  applicants in 2026 — every devaluation/exit is our acquisition moment
- **HDFC premium tier (Infinia/DCB/Regalia)** — status incumbents; we
  compete on value-per-rupee and zero fees, not status
- **CRED** — adjacent spend-utility, not a direct card rival
- **Kiwi** — credit-on-UPI distribution machine (4 issuer banks incl. PNB);
  competes on UPI ubiquity and cashback, not travel value
- **Niyo Global / Fi-Federal** — forex-focused neobank plays; we beat them
  on underwritten credit + travel reward depth
- **OneCard / Slice / Jupiter Edge** — new-age cards without a travel
  wedge; product-experience benchmarks more than direct threats
- **MakeMyTrip / ixigo / Cleartrip / EaseMyTrip** — distribution, not card
  competition; potential partners and potential disintermediators
- **AmEx Platinum Travel** — paused new India applications; its stranded
  premium-travel seekers are an acquisition pool
- **Federal Bank** — partner, not competitor — but also issues for other
  fintechs; partnership health is a standing watch item
