# BOBCard Etihad Guest Premium Credit Card Review

BOBCARD has finally entered the airline co-brand space with the launch of two Etihad Guest Credit Cards. While the base variant targets casual travellers, the BOBCARD Etihad Guest Premium Credit Card is the one that packs serious value.

With 0% forex markup, complimentary Gold tier status and generous lounge access, the card has a lot going for it. Let's break it all down to help you decide if this card is worth picking up.

## Overview

| | |
|---|---|
| Type | Airline Credit Card |
| Reward Rate | ~5.8% |
| Joining Fee | 5,000+GST |
| Best for | International travellers |
| USP | Etihad Guest Gold Status |

After Etihad Airways ended its partnership with SBI Card after five years, which included cards like the SBI Etihad Guest Premier Card, it has now entered into a new partnership with BOB Financial Solutions (BOBCARD).

The BOBCARD Etihad Guest Premium Credit Card is a metal card, aimed at travellers who fly Etihad & its partners frequently and want to stack miles from everyday spends on top of their flying miles.

## Fees & Benefits

| | |
|---|---|
| Joining Fee | 5,000 INR+GST |
| Welcome Benefit | – 10,000 Miles (25K Spend) <br> – 5,000 Bonus Miles (50K Spend) |
| Renewal Fee | 5,000 INR+GST |
| Renewal Benefit | 5,000 Miles |
| Renewal Fee waiver | 5L INR spend in a Year |

Note: The welcome & additional bonus miles are offered on reaching the required spends within the first 60 days.

The joining fee is quite reasonable for a premium travel card. And the fact that renewal is waived at just 5L annual spend is a big plus, as most airline cards don't offer a fee waiver.

**Limited Period Offer ends by 30th April**

Limited Period Offer: Get up to 15,000 Etihad Guest Miles: 10,000 joining miles on spending 25,000 INR in 60 days + 5,000 additional miles on cumulative spends of 50,000 INR. Offer valid till 30th April 2026.

## Etihad Guest: Gold Tier

This is easily the highlight of this card.

| Tier | Activation Criteria |
|---|---|
| Silver Tier | On first card transaction |
| Gold Tier | On first transaction on Etihad.com (within 6 months) |

With the Premium variant, you get Etihad Guest Silver on your very first card swipe. And once you make your first purchase on Etihad's website (even a small one like seat selection), you get upgraded to Etihad Guest Gold.

Getting Gold status otherwise requires earning 50,000 Tier Miles (with at least 20,000 from flying), which is not easy. So getting it with just a credit card transaction is a fantastic deal.

Here are the key benefits that come with Etihad Guest Gold status:

- Etihad Chauffeur service on First/Business GuestSeat bookings
- Business class lounge access (self + 1 guest) on Etihad flights
- Complimentary standard seat selection in Economy
- Free GuestSeat cancellation (once per year)

Note: Etihad Guest follows a Core + Custom + Beyond benefits structure. Gold members can choose 4 custom benefits annually. Lounge access is offered as a custom/default benefit, not automatic. So make sure you select it within 30 days of tier upgrade.

Important: Etihad is not part of any airline alliance. So Gold status benefits are limited to Etihad flights only, unlike Oneworld or Star Alliance programs where status works across multiple airlines.

## Rewards

| Spend Type | Miles per 100 INR |
|---|---|
| Etihad Airways (etihad.com) | 6 |
| Retail spends | 2 |

The base reward rate of 2 miles per 100 INR (i.e., 2% return as miles) is quite low for regular spends given that Etihad miles are of low value generally.

Note: Miles earned are credited to your Etihad Guest account within 3 days of statement generation.

### Exclusion for rewards

This deserves its own section because the exclusion list is unusually long for a premium credit card.

These include regular spends like: education fees, hospital expenses, telecom bills, supermarket purchases, fast-food restaurants, and much more, apart from usual exclusions like insurance, government spends, etc.

This is a major downside, as this card blocks a huge chunk of everyday spend categories.

## Milestone Benefits

| Milestone Type | Spend Requirement | Bonus Miles |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly | 4 transactions totaling 50,000 INR | 500 Miles |
| Quarterly | 3,00,000 INR / Qtr | 4,000 Miles / Qtr |
| Annual | 12,00,000 INR / Yr | 24,000 Miles / Yr |

Note: Each milestone benefit is applicable only once during its respective period.

There's also a bonus for Etihad flyers, you earn additional miles when you fly with Etihad twice in a year (applicable once per year).

## Redemptions

Etihad Guest Program has gone through multiple rounds of devaluation and it carries one of the least point value in the industry, that's as low as 50Ps/point in most typical redemption use cases.

However, after doing about 100+ award seat searches across the globe, I can confidently say that there are still some sweet spots that you may consider, and it's broadly useful for:

- Upgrade with Miles on Etihad revenue ticket
- Partner redemptions

One of the easiest redemptions for Indians is the partner redemption on SriLankan Airlines. You can get an award seat to Colombo from India for as low as 6K/20K miles on Eco/Biz one way, with award seats available in plenty, that's suitable for family trips.

There are also some unbelievable awards, like 10K miles on economy to Europe, thanks to Akasa Airlines partnership.

Apart from that, you could also get a pretty good value on many regional partner redemptions like intra-US on American Airlines, which is similar to Air India redemptions on United.

## Zero Forex Markup

| | |
|---|---|

This is one of the noteworthy feature of the card. Zero forex markup on all international transactions along with rewards is good to have for many.

If you travel internationally or shop on foreign websites, this feature alone can save you a good amount of money.

## Airport Lounge Access

| Access Type | Limit |
|---|---|
| Domestic | 12/Year (3 per quarter) |
| International | 8/Year (2 per quarter) |

The lounge access limits are decent, with no spend-based conditions attached. This is refreshing because many cards these days have started asking for spends to be eligible for lounge access.

## Maximising the Card

Let's do some math. If you spend 12 Lakhs in a year on eligible categories, here's what you could earn:

- Regular spend rewards: 24,000 Miles (2 per 100 on 12L)
- Monthly milestone (assuming all 12 months hit): 6,000 Miles
- Quarterly milestone (assuming all 4 quarters hit): 16,000 Miles
- Annual milestone: 24,000 Miles
- **Total: ~70,000 Miles on 12L spend (5.8%)**

That works out to about 5.8% return if you value Etihad miles at 1 INR or at-least 2.9% return if you value Etihad miles at 50ps.

Then you've Gold status, lounge access and much more. So, as you see, this is quite competitive if you know how to get value out of it.

## Should You Get It?

The BOBCARD Etihad Premium Credit Card makes a lot of sense if:

- You fly Etihad or it's partners
- You fly at-least once a year (to keep miles from expiring)
- You have significant international spends
- You value Etihad Guest Gold status
- You can achieve milestones spend targets

If above sounds doable, BOBCard Etihad Credit Card is a must have in your wallet for obvious reasons.

If you already hold another Credit Card from BOBCard, your application may initially get rejected, but the card would be issued the following week, just like how it happened to many others.

## Bottomline

**CardExpert Rating: 4.5/5**

The BOBCARD Etihad Guest Premium Credit Card is a solid airline co-brand card that delivers good value for foreign travellers.

The zero forex markup, Gold tier status, lounge access, and milestone bonuses make it worth considering, especially at this fee point with a waiver option.
