India's quick commerce sector is one of the fastest-growing startup segments. Delivering everything from electronics to beauty products to groceries and medicines within minutes at customers' doorsteps, it has really grown into a multi-billion-dollar ecosystem. As leading players like Flipkart Minutes, Amazon Now, Blinkit, Zepto, and Instamart race to expand their dark store networks and enter new cities, there is a huge competition for growing profitability and raising funding.

## Flipkart Minutes crosses 1,000 micro fulfilment centers

Flipkart Minutes, a quick commerce platform run by ecommerce marketplace Flipkart has crossed 1,000 micro fulfilment centers across 130+ cities and over 8,000 pincodes, a milestone achieved in under two years since its August 2024 launch, according to an official statement today, June 24.

## Key achievements by Flipkart Minutes

- Orders grew 5X from the previous year as the network expanded across 130+ cities and 8,000+ pincodes.
- Tier 2 and Tier 3 markets are driving growth with 42X scale as compared to last year, aided by new city and store expansion.
- Gen Z emerges as the fastest-growing customer cohort as quick commerce expands beyond daily essentials into 250+ categories.

According to the Flipkart Minutes data, Gen Z contributes to more than 40 per cent of the overall customer base, buying across daily essentials and new categories with high frequency and growing loyalty. This generation is approaching quick commerce differently, not just as a grocery top-up but as an on-demand shopping habit that spans beauty, electronics, wellness, and lifestyle.

"What began as a way to fulfil everyday essentials has evolved into a fundamentally new shopping habit for millions of Indians. Customers are not just ordering more; they are ordering differently. The average order value of fruits and vegetables increased by 30%, repeat purchases are up, and demand is spreading well beyond grocery into electronics, beauty, wellness, and lifestyle. Order volumes have grown 5X since last year. Beyond the infrastructure, this milestone is about redefining convenience for millions of Indian households across the country," said Kunal Gupta, SVP - head of Flipkart Minutes.

Over the past year, Flipkart Minutes expanded into 90+ new cities, with strong demand momentum emerging from markets such as Ambala, Arrah, Bokaro, Darbhanga, Jorhat, Ongole, Purnia, Saharsa, and Tenali. Tier 2 and Tier 3 markets have recorded massive growth with 42X scale as compared to last year. This dual momentum: depth in metros and rapid acceleration in emerging markets, reflects a platform scaling reach and adoption simultaneously, rather than trading one for the other.

## Other quick commerce platforms' dark stores

Blinkit, an Indian quick-commerce company owned by Eternal Limited, currently owns the highest number of dark stores, i.e., 2,243, in over 200 cities. Instamart by Swiggy, which has 1,143 stores in 129 cities, followed by Zepto and Amazon Now, which have 1,139 and 1,000 dark stores in 89 and over 50 cities, respectively.
