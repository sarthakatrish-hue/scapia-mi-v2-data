## What is a zero forex markup credit card?

A Zero Forex Markup Credit Card is a kind of card that helps you save money when you buy things in other countries or from foreign websites. Normally, when you use a regular credit card to pay for something in a foreign currency, the bank adds an extra fee called the forex markup. This fee is usually between 2% to 5% of the total transaction amount and is charged on top of the currency exchange rate.

However, a zero forex markup credit card does not charge this extra fee. When you use this card, you only pay the real exchange rate used by your card network (like Visa or Mastercard) to convert the foreign currency into your home currency. So, with a zero forex markup card, you avoid the additional 2% to 4% fee charged by most banks.

For instance, If you buy something that costs $100 USD:
- Regular credit card: You pay ₹1000 × 89 (rate) + ₹356 + 64.08 (forex charges @ 4% + 18% GST on fee) = ₹9,676.08
- Zero forex card: You pay ₹100 × 89 (rate) only = ₹8,900
- You save ₹776 on this purchase!
