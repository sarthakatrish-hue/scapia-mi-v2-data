# Zero Forex Markup Credit Cards

Some credit cards charge foreign transaction fees or forex markup fees on foreign transactions made in foreign currency. The charges range up to 3.5% approximately, but some credit cards even offer zero forex markup fees.

## What is a Zero Forex Card?

A zero-forex markup credit card enables users to make international transactions without incurring the typical foreign currency conversion fee of around 2% to 3%. Such cards can lead to meaningful savings for frequent travellers and individuals who regularly spend in foreign currencies.

## Foreign Transaction Fees

A foreign transaction fee, also known as a foreign markup fee, is a charge imposed by banks when you utilise your credit and debit cards for transactions outside your home country. Foreign transaction fees typically fall within the range of 0% to 3.5%, varying across different banks.

The foreign transaction fees are applicable for:
- Foreign retail transactions
- Online foreign currency transaction
- Overseas cash withdrawal via ATM
- POS machine linked overseas but transaction made in India
- Other transaction made in foreign currency

## Understanding the Calculation of Foreign Transaction Fees

To illustrate, let's consider an example: Suppose you make a purchase worth Rs.10,000 in foreign currency, and the foreign transaction fee stands at 3%. In this case, you would incur a markup fee of Rs.300. Additionally, with a GST rate of 18%, the total fee amounts to Rs.354. The higher the markup fee, the greater the overall expense incurred. Therefore, opting for cards with minimal or zero markup fees is advisable. When a foreign transaction is conducted in any other currency other than US dollars, then the amount is converted to US dollars and then the markup fee is applied.

## Additional Costs Associated with Using a Credit Card for Overseas Expenses

When employing your credit card for international transactions, you may encounter supplementary expenses beyond the foreign transaction fee. These expenses are not obligatory and are only applicable under specific circumstances.

**Taxation:** Goods and Services Tax (GST) is imposed on all fees imposed by the bank.

**Cash Withdrawal Fee:** If you withdraw cash from an ATM abroad, you will incur a cash withdrawal fee. This fee may either be a fixed amount or a percentage of the withdrawn sum.

**Finance Charges:** Failure to pay your credit card dues on time or withdraw cash can result in the bank charging you interest. Typically, this interest rate falls within the range of 30% to 45% per annum.

**Currency Conversion Fee:** When an Indian credit card is used for transactions in a foreign currency, banks levy a currency conversion fee. Typically ranging from 3% to 5% of the transaction amount, this charge is applied on the settlement date rather than the actual date of the transaction.

## Foreign Transaction Made with an Indian Credit Card

From the bank's viewpoint, a foreign transaction is conducted solely in US Dollars or another currency.

**For US Dollars Transactions:** When transactions are made in US Dollars, the Indian credit card converts the amount into Indian currency initially, after which the foreign markup fee is applied.

**For Transactions in Other Currencies:** If expenditures are in a different currency, the amount is first converted into US Dollars and then into Indian currency. Subsequently, the foreign markup fee is imposed, which you are required to settle with the bank when paying off your credit card balance.

## Points to Consider Before Applying for a Credit Card for Overseas Travel

Credit cards can be highly rewarding for international travel, offering perks like free lounge access and reward points. However, before using one abroad, it's important to keep the following factors in mind:

- Ensure your card handles the relevant foreign currency before travelling to that country.
- Check for additional fees beyond zero foreign-transaction markup, such as issuance fees, balance-inquiry fees, inactivity, or dormancy charges.
- Evaluate the card's security features, such as instant blocking/locking, two-factor authentication, fraud protection, and alerts.
- Review the usage or withdrawal limits.
- Ensure the card offers easy online top-up or reload options.
- Compare annual fees, joining fees and interest rates associated with the card — even with zero markup.
- Compare extra benefits or perks such as reward points, lounge access, insurance, and multi-currency support.

## FAQs on Zero Forex Markup Credit Cards

**What expenses are involved besides the Foreign Transaction Fee?**

Apart from the foreign transaction fee, you may encounter other expenses when using your credit card internationally. These include taxes (GST), cash withdrawal fees at overseas ATMs, finance charges for late payments, and currency conversion fees.

**How is a Foreign Transaction Processed on an Indian Credit Card?**

From the bank's perspective, a foreign transaction is usually conducted in USD or another foreign currency. If in USD, the amount is first converted into Indian currency, and then the foreign markup fee is applied. For other currencies, the amount is converted to USD and then to Indian currency, with the markup fee applied accordingly.

**How can you avoid surprises with my credit card's foreign transaction fee?**

To avoid surprises with your credit card's foreign transaction fee, it's crucial to check the fee associated with your card before using it for international transactions. Understanding the fee structure can help you plan your expenses more effectively.

**Are there any restrictions on using zero forex markup fee cards?**

Yes, most zero forex markup fee cards come with certain terms and conditions that may restrict card usage. It's essential to review these conditions carefully to understand any limitations or restrictions before using the card for international transactions.

**How can you understand the conversion charges before using my credit card abroad?**

You can understand the conversion charges by reviewing the terms and conditions of your credit card agreement or contacting your bank directly. Make sure to inquire about any currency conversion fees and how they are calculated to avoid unexpected charges.

**Can credit cards help accumulate rewards while travelling internationally?**

Yes, credit cards can accumulate rewards on international spending, including free lounge access and other benefits. By using your credit card wisely, you can maximise rewards and enjoy additional perks during your travels.

**What precautions should you take to avoid unnecessary fees when using your credit card abroad?**

You can opt for cards that offer low or zero foreign transaction fees to minimise avoidable costs while using your credit card internationally. You can also limit cash withdrawals to genuine necessities to reduce ATM-related charges, and ensure timely bill payments to prevent finance charges. It is also advisable to review your card's terms and conditions in advance, so you are fully aware of any applicable fees, usage limits, or restrictions.
