This fee is added by credit card companies to cover the cost of converting your money into foreign currency. ## What are Zero Forex Markup Credit Cards?

Zero forex markup cards are credit cards that don't charge extra fees when you make purchases in a foreign currency. Unlike regular credit cards, which often add a forex markup fee of 2-3% for international transactions, these cards let you shop or travel abroad without those extra costs. This means when you use a zero-forex markup card, you're only paying for what you buy – no surprise charges for converting your money. It's a great way to save, especially if you frequently make overseas purchases or travel abroad.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### Do all credit cards charge foreign transaction fees?

No, not all credit cards charge a foreign transaction. There are a few cards that charge a zero markup fee.

### What credit cards do not charge a foreign fee?

There are five cards that do not charge a foreign transaction fee. They are the Scapia Federal Bank Credit Card, Axis Bank Burgundy Private Credit Card, RBL World Safari Credit Card, Club Vistara IndusInd Explorer Credit Card, and IDFC First Wow Credit Card.

### How do I know if my credit card has foreign transaction fees?

You can check your card's terms and conditions, including the different kinds of fees it charges, in the factsheet or terms and conditions sheet. You can find this on the card issuer's website.

### What is a 3% foreign transaction fee?

If a credit card is charging a 3% foreign transaction fee, it means your bank will charge an additional 3% from you when billing your credit card dues. For example, if you use Rs 10,000 for a foreign currency transaction, then the bank will charge you an additional Rs 300 for the foreign currency transaction you did using the credit card. Hence, the credit card statement will reflect the amount of Rs 10,300 instead of Rs 10,000.

### Can you use Indian credit cards abroad?

Yes, you can! Most Indian credit cards work internationally. Just make sure your card is activated for international transactions. If you're unsure, check with your bank or card provider before you travel.

### What is a foreign transaction fee?

A foreign transaction fee is a small charge you pay when you use your credit card abroad. It covers currency conversion and other processing costs. The fee is usually a percentage of the transaction amount.

### What are the expenses involved in using a credit card for international spending?

When you use your credit card abroad, you might face a few costs like foreign transaction fees, currency conversion charges, and sometimes dynamic currency conversion fees if you choose to pay in Indian Rupees instead of the local currency.

### How is a foreign transaction calculated on an Indian credit card?

When you make a payment in a foreign currency, your credit card provider converts it to Indian Rupees using the current exchange rate. Then, they add any fees, like a foreign transaction fee or currency conversion charges, to the final amount.

### What happens when you use an Indian credit card for a foreign transaction?

When you swipe your card or make an online payment in a foreign currency, the amount gets converted to Indian Rupees. You'll see the converted amount and any added fees on your credit card statement.

### Are there any restrictions on using zero forex markup cards?

Zero forex markup cards let you skip currency conversion fees, but there might still be some limitations. For example, you may need to pay an annual fee or ensure the card is activated for international use. Always check the terms and conditions to avoid surprises.
