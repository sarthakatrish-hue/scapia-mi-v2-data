These cards not only reduce the cost of your international spends but often come with additional travel-focused benefits, such as accelerated reward points on travel, points-to-miles conversion, complimentary lounge access, and discounts on flights and hotels.

## FAQs

**Are zero forex mark-up credit cards completely free of foreign transaction charges?**

Yes, zero forex mark-up credit cards do not charge any fee on foreign currency transactions. However, all credit card cash withdrawals come with additional charges.

**Which ICICI credit card has zero forex mark-up?**

ICICI Bank does not offer credit cards with zero forex mark-up charges, however, MakeMyTrip ICICI Bank Credit Card comes with a reduced forex mark-up fee of 0.99%.

**Do zero forex markup credit cards still attract GST or other taxes?**

If a credit card does not come with any forex mark-up fee, your foreign currency transactions will not attract any taxes that are generally associated with the fee either. However, additional charges apply on foreign currency cash withdrawals made using a zero forex mark-up card.

**Who should apply for a zero forex mark-up credit card?**

The customers who frequently travel abroad or make international purchases should consider applying for a zero forex mark-up credit card to save on additional charges and reduce the total cost.

**Can I use a zero forex mark-up credit card for online international purchases?**

Yes, you can use zero forex mark-up credit cards for online international purchases.

**Do zero forex mark-up credit cards offer reward points on foreign spends?**

Yes, the reward program of zero forex mark-up credit cards generally includes reward points earnings on foreign spends.

**What is the difference between zero forex mark-up and low forex mark-up credit cards?**

Primarily, most credit cards come with a forex mark-up fee of 3.5% of the international transaction amount. On the contrary, zero forex mark-up credit cards do not come with any such charges and low forex mark-up credit cards are those which charge a reduced fee, generally below 3.5% but more than zero.

**Do zero forex mark-up credit cards have higher annual fees?**

No, credit cards across the annual fee range can come with zero forex mark-up fee. For instance, cards with zero forex mark-up fee include lifetime free cards like Federal Bank Scapia and ixigo AU, along with cards with higher annual fees, like RBL World Safari and Axis Burgundy Private.

**Can students or first-time users get a zero forex markup credit card?**

Yes, students and first-time users can get certain zero forex mark-up credit cards. Even if they are not eligible for unsecured credit card options, some secured credit cards can also be considered. For instance, IDFC FIRST WOW! comes with zero forex mark-up and AU NOMO comes with a reduced forex mark-up of 0.99%.

**Which zero forex mark-up credit card is the best for frequent travellers?**

While zero forex mark-up credit cards can be a good option for all frequent international travellers, the best-suited option should be chosen based on overall card features and the card's value-back potential based on your spending patterns.

**Do zero forex mark-up cards support international ATM cash withdrawals?**

Yes, you can use zero forex mark-up credit cards for international ATM cash withdrawals. But you should keep in mind that similar to general credit cards, such cash withdrawals using zero forex mark-up cards are also accompanied with a one-time withdrawal fee and high interest charges from the day of withdrawal.
