## What to look out for when choosing a credit card for travel in India

When choosing the best credit card for travelling overseas from India, it's important to understand the costs and benefits so the card aligns with your travel and spending habits.

**Annual and monthly fees:** Check whether the card's rewards, lounge access, or partner benefits justify the cost. If a card's benefits don't offset its fees, a low-fee alternative may be better. For example, a travel debit card like Wise, with no monthly or annual fee, can help you avoid ongoing charges.

**Foreign transaction fees (forex markup):** Look at what you'll pay on international transactions. For many credit cards, foreign transaction fees can add about 2% to 3.5% to your overall bill where they apply.

**Cash advance fees:** Using a credit card at an ATM is often expensive. Cash withdrawals can cost around 2.5% of the withdrawn amount, and interest usually starts accruing immediately (there's typically no interest-free period for cash advances).

**Travel benefits:** Compare perks like travel insurance, airport lounge access, airline miles, hotel status, or partner discounts, and make sure these are benefits you'll actually use and value.
