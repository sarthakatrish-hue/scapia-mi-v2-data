## IndusInd Tiger — 1:1 Air India Flying Returns Transfer Announced

IndusInd Bank has officially announced that Tiger reward points now transfer to Air India Flying Returns miles at a 1:1 ratio. The Tiger is a lifetime-free card with no annual fee and no spend criteria, making this one of the best value propositions in the no-fee segment — cardholders now have a direct path to Air India miles without a fee hurdle.

**Transfer ratio:** 1 Tiger Reward Point = 1 Air India Flying Returns mile (1:1)

**Applicable to the IndusInd Tiger Credit Card** — lifetime free, ₹0 joining and annual fee

**No minimum transfer threshold** confirmed; transfers processed through IndusInd's rewards portal

**Air India Flying Returns miles** can be redeemed for Air India flights, upgrades, and partner redemptions

**Context:** Tiger was already strong on lounge access (8 domestic), movie discounts, and quarterly golf — the 1:1 Air India transfer adds a meaningful travel reward layer to a ₹0-fee card

**Caveat:** Air India is the only transfer partner — no diversification option unlike multi-airline cards such as HSBC TravelOne or Axis Atlas

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Tiger reward points had no airline transfer partner; rewards were redeemable only against the rewards catalogue | Tiger reward points transfer to Air India Flying Returns at 1:1 — direct path to Air India miles at zero annual-fee cost |

**Source:** IndusInd Bank official announcement, June 2026

## SBI Card — PhonePe SBI SELECT BLACK Devalued (Effective 01 July 2026)

SBI Card has notified PhonePe SBI Card SELECT BLACK holders of reward-point cuts effective 01 July 2026. The monthly cap on Eligible Online Spends is halved from 2,000 RPs to 1,000 RPs. The 2,000 RP cap on Eligible PhonePe Spends is being split — only 500 RPs/month on Insurance plus 1,500 RPs/month on Other PhonePe spends. Six new spend categories are added to the no-RP exclusion list: Tolls & Bridges, Gifts & Souvenir, Jewellery, Educational Institutions, plus Utility/Insurance/UPI when paid outside the PhonePe app.

**Eligible Online Spends cap halved:** 2,000 RPs → 1,000 RPs per month (effective 1 July 2026)

**Eligible PhonePe Spends cap split:** 2,000 RPs → 500 RPs on Insurance + 1,500 RPs on Other PhonePe spends

**New zero-RP exclusions:**
- Tolls & Bridges (MCC 4784)
- Gifts/Souvenir (MCC 5947)
- Jewellery (MCC 5094, 5944)
- Educational Institutions (MCC 8220)

**Utilities** (MCC 4900, 4814, 4899) now earn 0 RPs unless the payment is routed through the PhonePe app

**Insurance** (MCC 5960, 6300, 6380) now earns 0 RPs unless the payment is routed through the PhonePe app

**UPI spends** earn 0 RPs unless made via the PhonePe app — kills RP accrual on Google Pay, Paytm and other third-party UPI apps

**Net impact:** heavy online spenders see up to 50% lower monthly RP earn; PhonePe-app exclusivity becomes the only path to RPs on utilities, insurance and UPI

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| PhonePe Spends: 2,000 RPs/month combined cap (any PhonePe spend type); Online Spends: 2,000 RPs/month cap; Utility, Insurance and UPI earn RPs regardless of the app used; Tolls, Gifts, Jewellery and Education earn at standard rates | PhonePe Spends: split cap — max 500 RPs on Insurance + 1,500 RPs on Other; Online Spends: 1,000 RPs/month cap; Utility, Insurance and UPI earn 0 RPs unless paid via the PhonePe app; Tolls (4784), Gifts/Souvenir (5947), Jewellery (5094, 5944) and Educational Institutions (8220) now earn 0 RPs — all effective 01 July 2026 |

**Source:** SBI Card official cardholder communication, June 2026
