## What Is a Zero Forex Credit Card?

Most Indian credit cards charge a foreign currency markup fee of 2% to 3.5% on international transactions. This fee is applied every time you swipe your card abroad or shop on international websites.

A zero forex credit card eliminates this markup entirely — meaning:

- You pay exactly what you spend
- No hidden currency conversion surprises
- Big savings on flights, hotels, shopping, and subscriptions

Even on a ₹2 lakh international trip, a regular card could cost you ₹6,000–₹7,000 extra just in forex charges.

## Who Should Use a Zero Forex Credit Card?

Zero forex cards are ideal if you:

- Travel internationally at least once a year
- Are a student studying abroad
- Work with overseas clients or subscriptions
- Shop frequently on international websites
- Book hotels, flights, or experiences in foreign currency

For gig workers and freelancers, this is especially useful when paying for tools, ads, or SaaS products priced in USD or EUR.
