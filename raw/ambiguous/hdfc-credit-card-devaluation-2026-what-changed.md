## HDFC Credit Card Devaluation 2026 – What Changed?

HDFC Bank, India's largest credit card issuer, quietly devalued three of its most popular mid-premium credit cards in May 2026. The HDFC credit card devaluation 2026 affects Regalia Gold, Diners Club Privilege, and BizPower — cards held by millions of Indians who chose them specifically for their reward rates and free lounge access. The changes took effect in two phases: May 15 for reward points and fee revisions, and July 1 for lounge access. The numbers look small on paper but add up to meaningful value loss over a full year, particularly for cardholders who do not hit the new spending thresholds. This guide covers exactly what changed, what it costs you in rupees, and what your options are — including unaffected HDFC alternatives available on PickMyWork right now.

## HDFC Regalia Gold — Every Change Explained

The Regalia Gold is HDFC's most widely held mid-premium card. The 2026 changes affect it across reward earning, lounge access, and fees — all confirmed on the official HDFC Bank product page.

**Reward rate — effective 15 May 2026:**
Previously 4 reward points per Rs. 150 spent. Now 5 reward points per Rs. 200 spent. The effective reward rate drops from 1.73% to 1.625% — a 6% reduction in value earned per rupee spent.

**Domestic lounge access — effective 1 July 2026:**
Previously free with no spend condition. From July 1, cardholders must spend Rs. 60,000 in the previous quarter to unlock 3 domestic lounge visits. If you spend below Rs. 60,000 in April-June 2026, you will have zero domestic lounge access in July-September 2026.

**International lounge access — unchanged:**
6 complimentary visits per year via Priority Pass remain fully intact with no spend condition attached. This benefit has not been touched.

**New Boarding Edge Programme — effective 15 May 2026:**
A new travel benefit — upload your airline boarding pass on HDFC SmartBuy and choose any 2 of the following per quarter: spa access, airport buffet, hotel room upgrade, or airport transfer. This partially offsets the lounge change for frequent flyers.

**Card reissuance fee — effective 15 May 2026:**
Rs. 199 fee introduced for replacing a lost, stolen, or damaged card. Previously free.

**Dynamic Currency Conversion fee hike — effective 15 May 2026:**
DCC fee increased to 1.75% on international transactions billed in Indian rupees. Affects cardholders who travel abroad and pay in INR rather than local currency.

## HDFC Diners Club Privilege — What Changed

The Diners Club Privilege saw the same reward rate change as Regalia Gold, confirmed on the official HDFC Bank Diners Privilege product page.

**Reward rate — effective 15 May 2026:**
Changed from 4 points per Rs. 150 to 4 points per Rs. 200. This is a steeper effective cut than Regalia Gold — the earn rate drops from 1.73% to 1.30%, a 25% reduction in base reward value.

**Domestic lounge access — effective 1 July 2026:**
2 domestic and 1 international lounge visit per calendar quarter now require Rs. 60,000 spend in the previous quarter. Previously available without any spend condition.

**Accelerated rewards remain strong:**
5X reward points on Swiggy and Zomato and up to 10X on SmartBuy are unchanged. The devaluation primarily hits base spend earners, not those who concentrate spend on partner platforms.

## HDFC BizPower — What Changed

BizPower is a business credit card and its devaluation has an additional dimension beyond the reward rate cut that directly affects business owners and self-employed professionals.

**Reward rate — effective 15 May 2026:**
Changed from 4 points per Rs. 150 to 5 points per Rs. 200, matching the Regalia Gold change. The effective base reward rate drops from 1.73% to 1.625%.

**IT and GST transaction cap — effective 15 May 2026:**
Only the first 2 income tax and first 2 GST transactions per billing cycle earn reward points. Any additional IT or GST payments in the same month earn zero points. This specifically targets business owners who were routing large tax payments through the card to accumulate reward points.

**Monthly 5X rewards cap increased:**
The monthly cap on 5X accelerated rewards has been raised from 5,000 to 7,500 reward points — a slight improvement that partially offsets the base rate reduction for high-spend business users.

## Should You Keep or Cancel Your Devalued HDFC Card?

The answer depends on your spending pattern. Here is a simple decision guide:

**If you spend Rs. 60,000 or more per quarter on the card:**
Keep it. The domestic lounge access unlocks automatically, and the reward rate reduction is marginal. The Boarding Edge Programme adds back some travel value if you fly even occasionally.

**If you mainly use the card for base spends and lounge access:**
The card's value proposition has weakened significantly. The reward rate is now below what several no-fee cards offer. Consider switching to an alternative that delivers better base rewards without a spend condition on lounge.

**If you are a BizPower cardholder routing IT or GST payments:**
The IT/GST cap effectively caps your reward earning on two of the largest business expense categories. Review whether the card still justifies its annual fee based on non-tax business spends alone.

**Before cancelling:**
Call HDFC Bank and request a downgrade to a no-fee card rather than an outright cancellation. Cancellation closes the credit account, which can temporarily lower your CIBIL score by reducing your overall credit limit and shortening your credit history.
