## Eligibility Criteria for Zero Forex Markup Credit Cards in India

Eligibility for zero forex mark-up credit cards depends on whether the card is secured or unsecured, along with income profile, age, and documentation requirements set by the issuing bank.

**Age Requirement:** Most unsecured cards require applicants to be 21–65 years old. Secured cards like FD-backed variants may allow entry from 18 years onwards.

**Income Criteria:** Premium cards such as IDFC FIRST Mayura require high and stable income proof, while entry-level cards accept moderate salary or self-employed income levels.

**Credit Score:** Unsecured cards usually need a good credit score for approval. Secured cards like IDFC FIRST WOW or Niyo variants do not require prior credit history.

**Fixed Deposit Requirement:** Secured cards require a minimum FD, such as ₹5,000 or more, which determines your credit limit and must remain locked during usage.

**KYC & Documentation:** Resident Indians must provide valid PAN, Aadhaar, and address proof. Some travel-focused cards may require an active passport for international features.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid While Using Zero Forex Markup Credit Cards

Zero forex cards can reduce overseas transaction costs, but incorrect usage may reduce their real benefit. Avoid these common mistakes to maximise savings and prevent hidden expenses.

**Paying in INR Abroad:** Always choose to pay in local currency overseas. Selecting INR triggers Dynamic Currency Conversion, which adds hidden markups despite 0% forex fees.

**Ignoring Reward Exclusions:** Some zero forex cards do not offer reward points on international transactions. Check reward eligibility to avoid overestimating return value.

**Missing Spend Conditions:** Certain lounge access or premium benefits require monthly or quarterly spend thresholds, such as ₹10,000–₹20,000. Failing to meet this cancels perks.

**Not Tracking FD Requirements:** Secured cards require maintaining a minimum FD or lien amount. Reducing the deposit can lower your credit limit unexpectedly.

**Delaying Bill Payments:** Zero forex does not mean zero interest. Carrying international balances attracts standard finance charges, often above 3% per month.

## Tips to Maximise Zero Forex Markup Credit Card Benefits

Zero forex cards help reduce international spending costs, but strategic usage can further improve savings, rewards, and travel comfort during overseas trips.

**Pay in Local Currency Always:** When paying abroad, select the local currency instead of INR to avoid dynamic currency conversion charges that cancel zero forex savings.

**Use for Online Global Purchases:** Zero forex cards are useful not only for travel but also for international subscriptions, course fees, and overseas shopping websites.
