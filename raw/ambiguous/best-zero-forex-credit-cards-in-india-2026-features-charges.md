## What is a zero forex markup credit card?

A zero forex markup credit card is a credit card which converts overseas spending to INR for billing using the network or bank exchange rate with no additional forex markup or margin. While the bank itself doesn't add an extra foreign transaction fee, the rate used for the conversion may still differ slightly from the real interbank rate.

Some zero forex markup credit cards are aimed at high earning individuals looking for lifestyle and travel perks while others have features aimed at frequent travellers who may have a more modest budget. If you're thinking of a card to cut your overall travel costs we've got some zero forex markup credit cards with lifetime free maintenance fees which can be a good pick.

## What should you look out for when choosing a credit card with zero forex markup?

It's important to know that a zero forex markup card isn't necessarily truly free to use. There are often fees involved which may be in the exchange rate, or may be based on your transaction habits.

When a bank or provider calls themselves a "zero forex markup" service, they mean they do not charge a foreign transaction fee on top of the exchange rate. Most of these cards use their own set rate or a network rate (like Visa or Mastercard), which can include a hidden margin compared to the interbank rate you see on Google. This means you could still be paying an extra cost without even realising it. To understand the basis your card uses for conversion, you should check the fine print carefully.

Aside from possible fees incorporated into the rate you get, there are also often annual fees, cash advance costs, interest, penalty fees and other charges which may be unavoidable or may be as a result of how you use your card. To decide if a card is good value for your purposes you'd need to take these costs into account when comparing your options.
