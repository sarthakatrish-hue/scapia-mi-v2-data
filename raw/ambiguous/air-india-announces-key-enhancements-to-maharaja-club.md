Air India has announced a set of enhancements to Maharaja Club, its frequent flyer programme launched in November 2024 as a successor to the long-running Flying Returns. The changes centre on three areas: widening where points can be redeemed, creating new everyday earning channels and improving the value equation on award flights.

Taken together, the announcements represent the most substantive evolution of the programme since its launch.

## The Programme: A Brief Orientation

Maharaja Club replaced Flying Returns in November 2024 following the merger of Vistara into Air India. Club Vistara members were migrated into the new programme, and the loyalty currency was renamed Maharaja Points. The programme operates four tiers — Red, Silver, Gold and Platinum — with a separate Tier Points currency used exclusively to determine status.

Tier Points and Maharaja Points are distinct. You earn Tier Points to climb the ladder; you spend Maharaja Points on awards. Every tier accrues both simultaneously when flying, but only Maharaja Points carry over as redeemable currency.

### Qualifying for Status

Tier qualification requires earning a minimum number of Tier Points within a membership year, with a portion mandated to come specifically from Air India flights. Members can also qualify on a flights-flown basis.

| Tier | Tier Points Required | Min. from Air India | OR: Flights Flown | Min. Air India Flights |
|------|---------------------|-------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Red | Entry level | — | — | — |
| Silver | 15,000 TP | 4,500 TP | 30 flights | 4 flights |
| Gold | 30,000 TP | 9,000 TP | 60 flights | 8 flights |
| Platinum | 45,000 TP | 13,500 TP | 90 flights | 12 flights |

## Change One: Maharaja Points on Air India Express

The most consequential announcement for domestic travellers is the forthcoming integration of Air India Express into the Maharaja Club redemption network. Express, which operates an extensive network of routes to smaller Indian cities and Gulf destinations not served by the mainline carrier, will begin accepting Maharaja Points for award bookings.

Redemptions are confirmed to launch first. Accrual — earning points when flying Express — will follow at a later stage, with no date currently committed.

### What This Opens Up

Air India Express serves over 50 destinations, many of which the mainline Air India network does not touch at all: Kannur, Kozhikode, Trichy, Tuticorin, Coimbatore, Bhubaneswar, and a broad array of Gulf points including Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Doha and Muscat. These routes have historically been inaccessible to Maharaja Points entirely. That changes with this integration.

### Why Accrual Is Taking Longer

The gap between redemption and accrual launch is not arbitrary. Air India Express operates on Navitaire, a reservations platform commonly used by low-cost carriers, while Air India mainline runs on a full global distribution system. Connecting the two loyalty engines requires significant technical work.

There is an additional layer of complexity: Air India Express had previously integrated its earning with the Tata Neu super-app ecosystem. The eventual solution is expected to give passengers the ability to choose whether a given Express flight earns into Tata Neu or Maharaja Club. Until that architecture is in place, accrual remains on hold.

For now, the practical implication is straightforward: once launched, members will be able to book Express flights using accumulated Maharaja Points, but those same Express flights will not generate new points until the second phase is live.

Air India Express connects over 50 cities — many unreachable on mainline Air India — including a substantial Gulf network. All of these will become redeemable with Maharaja Points.

## Change Two: A Co-Branded Maharaja Club Credit Card

Air India has confirmed that a Maharaja Club co-branded credit card is in development. The stated objective is to give members a way to accumulate points through everyday spending — purchasing groceries, dining, travel bookings — rather than relying solely on flying.

The announcement is notable for what it acknowledges implicitly: the existing Air India credit card landscape has a significant gap. The Air India SBI Platinum card has been unavailable for new applications for an extended period. The Vistara co-branded cards issued by Axis Bank and SBI are being phased out following the merger. At the moment, there is no actively issuable dedicated Air India credit card in the Indian market.

### What Remains Unconfirmed

Air India has not disclosed the issuing bank, fee structure, earning rates, welcome bonus or lounge access policy for the new card. The announcement positions it as an "everyday earning" product, but the specific category multipliers — which would determine whether it is genuinely competitive against established travel cards — are unknown.

If the card follows the architecture of the old Club Vistara products, it is likely to come in multiple variants with status acceleration benefits at higher spend levels. Whether it can compete on earn rates with the HDFC Infinia or Axis Atlas remains the central question for credit card users.

## Change Three: Expanded Access to Best-Priced Award Flights

Air India has committed to making more seats available at the Value Fare pricing tier — the lower of two award price points — across its long-haul network. The programme currently operates a two-tier pricing model: Value Fares offer the best redemption rate but have historically been difficult to find on popular routes, while Prime Fares are more widely available but cost substantially more points.

The gap between Maharaja Points pricing and what partner programmes charge for the identical Air India flight has been a persistent criticism of the programme.

The same Air India business class seat can cost up to 55% fewer miles when booked through a partner programme. Air India has committed to closing this gap by making Value Fare inventory more accessible.

| Route | Maharaja Points | Partner Programme (same flight) | Difference |
|-------|-----------------|--------------------------------|-----------|
| Mumbai to New York, Business | 1,95,000 MP | 88,000 United MileagePlus miles | 55% more expensive |
| India to Europe, Business | 90,000 MP | 62,000 Singapore KrisFlyer miles | 31% more expensive |
| Delhi to Pune, Economy (Value) | 5,000–7,500 MP | Not redeemable via partners | — |

## Tier Benefits: The Full Picture

The tier structure and associated benefits are unchanged.

Tier Points are earned at a flat rate of 6 per ₹100 spent on fare, regardless of tier. Maharaja Points earning is tier-dependent and gets a 2-point bonus per ₹100 for direct bookings made through the Air India website or app.

| Benefit | Red | Silver | Gold | Platinum |
|---------|-----|--------|------|----------|
| Maharaja Points per ₹100 (fare) | 6 MP | 8 MP | 9 MP | 10 MP |
| Direct booking bonus | +2 MP | +2 MP | +2 MP | +2 MP |
| Domestic airport lounge access | None | None | Yes, plus one guest | Yes, plus one guest |
| Priority check-in and boarding | No | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Priority Connect helpline | No | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Cabin upgrade vouchers (domestic) | None | 1 per year | 2 per year | 3 per year |
| Extra baggage allowance (domestic) | None | +5 kg | +20 kg | +20 kg |
| Extra baggage (Star Alliance flights) | None | +20 kg or 1 piece | +20 kg or 1 piece | +20 kg or 1 piece |
| Free itinerary changes (domestic) | No | No | 2 changes free | Unlimited |
| Free cancellations (domestic) | No | No | No | Yes |
| Star Alliance status equivalent | None | Star Alliance Silver | Star Alliance Gold | Star Alliance Gold |

## The Physical Membership Kits

One of the more tangible additions to the programme is the introduction of physical membership kits for qualifying Gold and Platinum members. These began shipping from October 2025 for members reaching the respective tier from that point onwards.

### Gold Tier Kit

The Gold kit contains a new-format Gold membership card, two customised luggage tags in gold-toned finishes and a set of acrylic coasters featuring vintage Maharaja artwork from Air India's archive.

### Platinum Tier Kit

The Platinum kit is more considered in its composition. It includes a custom leather folio crafted by Nappa Dori — a Delhi-based accessories house with a strong premium positioning — fitted with utility pockets. The kit also contains a redesigned Platinum membership card, a Nappa Dori passport holder, two personalised luggage tags and an exclusively produced deck of playing cards featuring the Maharaja character.

The standout piece is a limited-edition brass medallion commemorating J.R.D. Tata's inaugural Tata Airlines flight in 1932 — the origin story of what eventually became Air India.

## What Remains Unresolved

The announcements are substantive but incomplete. Several questions that matter to members are still without answers.

**Express accrual timeline.** Redemptions on Air India Express are confirmed. The ability to earn points on Express flights — which would make the integration genuinely reciprocal — has no committed date. For members who fly Express frequently on affordable fares, this is the half of the announcement that matters more.

**Co-branded card specifics.** The card exists as a concept. Without an issuing bank, fee structure or earning rates, it is not yet possible to assess whether it will be competitive with transferring points from an existing premium card. The gap it fills is real; whether it fills it well is unknown.

**Award availability verification.** The commitment to increased Value Fare inventory is meaningful if it holds in practice. Long-haul routes to London, New York, Melbourne and Tokyo are where it would need to show up to be credible. That verification takes months of booking data, not a press release.

**Tier benefits on Express flights.** Gold and Platinum members receive lounge access, priority boarding and additional baggage on mainline Air India flights. Whether those same benefits apply when flying an Air India Express-coded flight post-integration has not been addressed.

## Assessment

Maharaja Club is a materially better programme today than Flying Returns was in 2023. The tier benefits are competitive for a home-carrier programme. The Star Alliance Gold status that comes with Gold and Platinum tier provides real utility on partner airlines. The 24-month validity extension on every flight is among the most generous in the market.

The programme's weaknesses are also clear. Award pricing on long-haul routes remains uncompetitive when benchmarked against what partner programmes charge for the same Air India flights. The absence of a dedicated co-branded earning card has made it difficult for non-frequent-flyers to accumulate points in any meaningful quantity.

The announcements address both weaknesses — Express integration broadens the redemption landscape, the co-branded card addresses the earning gap, and the Value Fare commitment targets the pricing problem. Whether the execution matches the intent is a question that April 2026 cannot yet answer.

For regular Air India flyers: Silver status is attainable with moderate flying and unlocks meaningful soft benefits. Gold is where the programme becomes genuinely compelling — lounge access, two free itinerary changes and Star Alliance Gold on partner carriers. The path to Platinum requires serious commitment but the benefits, particularly free cancellations on domestic travel, are substantial.

For credit card points collectors: The most efficient current path to Maharaja Points is HDFC Infinia or ICICI Emeralde transfers, targeted at specific high-value long-haul redemptions rather than domestic. Wait for the co-branded card before drawing conclusions about everyday earning. If the issuing bank is one of the top-tier issuers and the earn rates are competitive, the calculus changes significantly.
