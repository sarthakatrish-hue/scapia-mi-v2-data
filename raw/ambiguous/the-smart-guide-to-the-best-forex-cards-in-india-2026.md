## What's a Forex Card?

A forex card is a debit card which is intended for use when you're traveling abroad, or when you spend in a foreign currency online.

Forex cards are often linked to a digital account which can hold multiple currencies, allowing you to add a balance in INR and then convert to the currency you need before you travel. You can then spend with minimal fees when you're abroad, and manage your budget from your phone or smart device.

In this guide we explore which bank forex card is best - and also touch on a few alternatives which are from specialist providers rather than banks, like the Wise Travel Card. Specialists like Wise may be able to offer a broader range of supported currencies as well as low fees and great rates when you spend.

## Key Things to Look for When Choosing a Forex Card

We'll dive into a review of the best forex cards in India in just a moment - first, here are a few things to consider while you choose the ideal provider for your needs:

**Currencies available:** Most forex cards let you hold multiple currencies in one account - but some only support a single currency, or may force you to hold your balance in INR and only convert when you pay. If you'd prefer to have your foreign currency balance set before you travel, pick a provider which covers the currencies you need for your trip - like the Wise Travel Card which has 40+ currencies for holding and exchange.

**Exchange rate:** Some cards state that there's no exchange fee or forex fee, but this may simply mean that the costs involved have been absorbed into the exchange rate you get when you convert currencies. Look out for hidden charges so you don't lose out on exchanging your INR to the currency required.

**Currency conversion fee:** Forex cards may add a markup or margin - a forex fee - to the interbank rate to convert your INR to the currency you need to make a payment. This can be 2% - 4% of the sum you convert. Look out for cards which don't have any hidden or hard to spot forex fee, like the Wise Travel Card.

**Digital card:** Having a digital card can be a bonus if you shop online or make mobile payments. Your digital card has different card details to your physical card so you can delete and dispose of your digital card if you need to without needing to cut up your physical card.

**Application process:** Some providers need you to visit a store or branch to order your card or to load your account. Finding a provider which operates fully online can be much simpler and take less time overall.

**Fees:** Finally look out for issuance, annual and inactivity fees, ATM costs and any other charges which may mean you have less to spend on your travels.
